# Core Anchors (0–5 for all competencies)
0 Missed | 1 Poor | 2 Weak | 3 Adequate | 4 Strong | 5 Excellent
- Same anchors across competencies to prevent drift.

## Personal Finance Discovery Quality (Pain Identification)
- 2/5: Identifies a goal (e.g., "retire") without quantifying gap; no horizon/priority.
- 3/5: Surfaces 2–3 specifics (e.g., current savings rate, horizon, basic risk preference).
- 4/5: Quantifies gap with simple math (needs vs assets vs time); confirms success criteria.
- 5/5: Multi-layer laddering: cashflow → protection → investing → timeline → tradeoffs; gains commitment on next step.

## Objection Handling (Finance-specific)
- 2/5: Addresses fees/performance generically; no isolate/resolve/advance.
- 3/5: Normalizes concern; provides one piece of evidence; partial check for resolution.
- 4/5: Isolates ("Other than fees..."); shows value math or risk framing; explicit check.
- 5/5: Reframes to outcomes & constraints; contrasts alternatives (DIY/ETF/insurer options) fairly; secures conditional commitment.

## Communication (Compliance-aware)
- Penalize: implied guarantees, inaccurate claims, fear-mongering, pushing complex products without needs analysis.
- Reward: clarity, disclaimers where appropriate, plain-language analogies, numerical summaries, and next-step clarity.
