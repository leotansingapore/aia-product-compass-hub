# Day 54 — Practice: 5 Objection Drills

> **The one idea for today:** Objection handling is a reflex, not a recitation. This week proves yours is built.

By the time you close today you'll have recorded 5 separate 2-minute drills — each handling a different objection with diagnostic + response + re-close — integrated the 3 Magic Questions, ART, and Iceberg into live drills without over-thinking which to deploy, and submitted the 5 recordings + objection log + Loom reflection. Those three artefacts unlock Week 10.

---

## The bar today

Week 9's gate is different from earlier weeks. The reps aren't live pitches — they're *objection drills* on tape.

**Why drills, not live meetings:** objection-handling speed is built through repetition. In a live meeting, you get one objection at a time, spread across weeks. In drills, you get 5 objections in 45 minutes — the volume that actually builds reflex.

**The bonus target:** *first case closed.* Week 9 is also when your Week-4 to Week-8 pipeline work should start producing actual signed cases. If one comes through this week, document it. If not — drills are the gate.

---

## The 5 drills

Pick **5 different objections** from Day 53's Top 10. Cover a range:

Suggested set:
1. *"Too expensive"*
2. *"Let me think about it"*
3. *"I have an advisor"*
4. *"Discuss with spouse"*
5. Your weakest — the one you handle worst

Don't pick 5 easy ones. Include the one that makes you uncomfortable — that's the rep that matters most.

---

## The drill structure — each recording

Each of the 5 drills is **2 minutes on camera.** Structure:

### Minute 1 — Diagnostic
- Prospect (you or a roleplay partner) delivers the objection
- You apply the right diagnostic — Magic Q1 at minimum; full 3 if ambiguous
- Surface the real concern

### Minute 2 — Response + Re-close
- Use the right technique (ART for clear, Iceberg for ambiguous, Magic Qs if needed)
- Deliver the word-for-word response in *your voice*
- Re-close — either confirm the commitment or book a specific next step

**Keep to 2 minutes.** Each drill isn't a full pitch — it's an objection rep. Shorter forces focus on the skill being trained.

---

## Recording setup

- **Camera at eye level**
- **Continuous take** per drill
- **Label files:** `day-54-objection-1-price.mov`, `-2-think.mov`, etc.
- **No notes in frame** — you deliver from memory. Stumbling is fine; reading is not.

**Option — with a roleplay partner:**
- Better than solo because their objection comes with real unpredictable energy
- Mentor, peer, spouse — anyone who'll play the objection honestly
- Agree the 5 objections beforehand so they know what to throw

**Option — solo:**
- Record yourself delivering the objection (in character as prospect), pause the camera, then re-roll delivering your response
- Less realistic but forces you to handle your own worst phrasing

Both count. Partner is preferred.

---

## Self-review rubric

Watch all 5 back. For each, rate 1–5 on:

| Dimension | What to watch for |
|---|---|
| **Diagnostic** | Did you use Magic Q1 before responding, or jump straight to defence? |
| **Technique fit** | ART for clear objection, Iceberg for ambiguous, Magic Qs if needed? |
| **Tonality** | Calm, Reason-tone close? Or defensive / rushed? |
| **Response quality** | Was your response in your own voice, or did you recite a script? |
| **Re-close** | Did you end with a specific next step or just trail off? |

Total possible: 25 per drill × 5 drills = 125. A score of 100+ is solid; 80–100 is competent with clear gaps; <80 means re-record at least the weakest 2.

## The first-case-closed bonus

If you close your first case this week:

**Document:**
- What was the final objection (if any) before the close?
- What technique cleared it?
- What specifically moved the prospect from *"maybe"* to *"yes"*?

This case study becomes your highest-value learning artefact. The first close teaches you more than the next 10 combined — capture it carefully.

**If you don't close this week:**
- That's expected for many Week-9 FCs
- The gate is the drills, not the close
- Week 10 will cover after-sales so you're ready when it happens

## Submission format

Four artefacts:

### Artefact 1 — 5 drill recordings
Loom or Google Drive links. Shareable.

### Artefact 2 — Objection log
One table: objection / technique used / self-score / note.

### Artefact 3 — First-case documentation (if applicable)
Brief — what was the objection, what cleared it, what moved the prospect.

### Artefact 4 — Day-54 Loom reflection (3–5 min)
Answering:
1. Which of the 5 drills was hardest? Why?
2. Which technique do you reach for most reflexively? Which do you *underuse*?
3. What specifically will change about how you handle objections in Week 10 and beyond?

### Mentor review
Book a **20-minute** mentor check-in. Mentor watches the 5 drills with you and gives live feedback on:
1. Did each drill actually use the diagnostic, or skip it?
2. Which drill's response felt most authentic? Which felt most scripted?

## Quiz

**Q1. The 5 drills are deliberately short (2 min each) because:**
- A) Loom has a time limit
- B) The skill being trained is *objection-handling reflex* — shorter reps build reflex faster than long meetings ✓
- C) It's easier to review
- D) Prospects have short attention spans

**Why:** Reflex skills (objection recognition → technique selection → clean response) are built through volume of reps. A full 60-minute pitch gives you 1 objection rep; a 45-minute drill session gives you 5. 2-minute focus forces each drill to concentrate on the skill being trained without bleeding into pitch-craft. The brevity is intentional, not a constraint.

**Q2. The diagnostic step (Magic Q1 at minimum) matters because:**
- A) It sounds professional
- B) It ensures you're addressing the real objection, not a decoy — saving you from spending energy on the wrong concern ✓
- C) It's a compliance requirement
- D) It's polite

**Why:** About half of initial objections are decoys for deeper concerns. Without the diagnostic (*"Is there anything else?"* as the minimum), you'll respond to *"too expensive"* with a price-justification when the real issue was *"I don't see the need."* The 90 seconds of diagnostic saves 10 minutes of misdirected response — and avoids the worst outcome, which is the prospect disengaging because you addressed the wrong concern.

**Q3. First case closed this week is:**
- A) Required for Week-10 unlock
- B) A bonus — document it carefully because first closes teach more than the next 10 combined ✓
- C) Irrelevant to Week 9
- D) The only metric that matters

**Why:** The gate is the drills, not the close — Week 9 unlocks on output, not outcome. But if a close does happen, the first one contains high-value information: which objection cleared right before the close, what technique worked, what moved the prospect from maybe to yes. That documentation becomes your personal case study. Many FCs close their first case in Week 9 or Week 10 depending on their pipeline pace; both are normal.

**Q4. The 5th drill should be:**
- A) The easiest objection (to build confidence)
- B) Your weakest — the one you handle worst ✓
- C) A random draw
- D) Skipped

**Why:** 5 easy drills build false confidence. Including the uncomfortable one is what makes the session a real training rep — your weakest skill gets the reps it most needs. Doing it under camera and mentor review accelerates the improvement. "Drill the thing you avoid" is the shortest path to becoming competent at it; avoiding it is the fastest path to it remaining your weakness for the next 5 years.

**Q5. A drill where the prospect (roleplay partner) comes with unpredictable energy is:**
- A) Harder to handle
- B) Closer to real pitch conditions — mentor / peer / spouse throwing objections with real inflection produces better training than solo re-recording ✓
- C) A waste of time
- D) Only useful for experienced FCs

**Why:** Real prospects don't deliver objections cleanly. They add emotion, sarcasm, follow-up questions, physical distance, silence. A roleplay partner who delivers with real unpredictable energy gives you the same training conditions. Solo recording is still legitimate (forces you to handle your own worst phrasing) but partner is superior for building the reflex that transfers to live meetings.

**Q6. The 2-minute drill structure is:**
- A) Minute 1 diagnostic + minute 2 response + re-close ✓
- B) Minute 1 argue + minute 2 close
- C) Random structure
- D) All 2 minutes on response only

**Why:** Compressing to 2 minutes forces the 3 moves — diagnostic (Magic Q1 at minimum) + response (right technique in your voice) + re-close (specific next step). Longer drills drift into pitch-craft; shorter ones skip the diagnostic. The 2-minute shape is the minimum viable objection rep — same shape as you'd use in a live meeting, just concentrated.

**Q7. If you score <80 across the 5 drills, the move is:**
- A) Pass anyway — the volume counts
- B) Re-record at least the weakest 2 drills before submitting ✓
- C) Drop out of the module
- D) Skip Week 10

**Why:** <80/125 means at least 2 of 5 drills had gaps serious enough to warrant another rep. Re-recording the weakest 2 (not all 5) is the honest middle path — the good drills are kept, the weak ones get targeted re-work. Dropping (C) or skipping (D) abandons the muscle build entirely. Passing anyway (A) leaves real weaknesses that Week 10 can't compensate for.
