# Day 18 — Practice: 3 Posts + 5 DM Conversations Opened

> **The one idea for today:** Week 3 becomes real when you've shipped posts someone can screenshot and opened DMs someone could reply to. Output, not intention.

By the time you close today you'll have shipped 3 IG posts — each answering a different silent question from Day 13 (Q1–Q5) — opened 5 DM conversations with real prospects (not mentors, not peers) using the texting rules + CRAB, and submitted a screenshot log + Loom reflection. Those two artefacts unlock Week 4.

---

## The bar today

Week 1 shipped a recorded intro. Week 2 shipped 3 live intent-statement deliveries. Week 3 ships *two* things — content and conversation.

Three posts. Five DM conversations opened. Logged and submitted by Sunday 9 pm.

The reason for two deliverables this week: content is half the pipeline (pull) and DMs are the other half (push). If you only shipped posts, you'd be hoping. If you only DMed, you'd be pushing without proof. Both together is the full rep.

---

## The 3 posts — what counts

Each post has to answer a different silent question from Day 13. The minimum mix:

| Post | Must answer | Format |
|---|---|---|
| **Post 1** | **Q5** — easy to approach | Offer post with a DM keyword CTA |
| **Post 2** | **Q1 or Q3** — social proof OR origin story | Pre-during-post testimonial OR 60-sec origin reel |
| **Post 3** | **Q2 or Q4** — expertise OR get along | Educational carousel OR PPVV lifestyle post |

**Why the mix:** if all three posts answer Q2 (expertise) — which is the new-FC default — you won't build social proof or connection. The 3 posts need to *stretch* your content across at least three of the five questions.

**What doesn't count:**
- A story that lives for 24 hours — must be a feed post
- A repost of someone else's content
- A post with no CTA or call-to-action (if the post doesn't ask the reader to do *something*, it's not doing Q5's job)

---

## The 5 DM conversations — what counts

A DM counts as "opened" when:

1. You sent a message that follows the three non-negotiable rules (link back + reason to meet + easy question) **and**
2. They replied with something other than *"thanks"* or a single emoji, **or**
3. If they blue-tick'd, you sent a CRAB follow-up this week

So *"opened"* means **there was a real exchange** — minimum 3 bubbles back and forth, or a completed CRAB cycle.

**Examples that count:**
- A value-first DM that got a reply and a short back-and-forth
- An offer-post reply ("DM me 'plan'") that you responded to and kept going
- A blue-ticked chat you CRAB'd this week that got a date reply
- A reactivation of a warm contact with a real conversation

**Examples that don't count:**
- A one-way message that got read but no reply (still pending — re-send as CRAB before Sunday)
- A mentor or peer conversation
- An automated *"happy birthday"* that got a single *"thanks!"*

Five real conversations by Sunday. If you hit 3 by Saturday, use Sunday to push to 5. If you hit 5 by Friday, keep going — volume is the whole game.

## Submission format

### The screenshot log

Create one note (in Apple Notes, Notion, or a Google Doc) with two sections:

**Section 1 — 3 posts**
- Screenshot of each shipped post (or a link to the live post)
- Label each with the silent question it answers (Q1 / Q2 / Q3 / Q4 / Q5)

**Section 2 — 5 DM conversations**
- Screenshot of each DM (names/faces redacted)
- One-line note per screenshot: *"Opener type (hook opener / value drop / CRAB / offer-post reply) + outcome (replied, booked, scheduled follow-up)"*

Ten screenshots total, plus ten lines of context. No essay. No novel.

### The Loom reflection (3–5 minutes)

Record one Loom answering three questions:

1. Which of the 3 posts performed best — and why do you think it landed?
2. Which DM opener worked best for *your* warm-market — hook-based, value-first, offer-post reply, or CRAB?
3. What's your v3 going to fix for Week 4? (Content type? DM cadence? A specific silent question gap?)

### Mentor review

Book a 10-minute mentor check-in before Sunday 9 pm. Your mentor critiques two things only:

1. Are your posts answering *different* silent questions, or are you stuck on Q2 (expertise) only?
2. Are your DMs following all three non-negotiables, or are you still opening with *"hey, how are you?"*

## The submission anti-pattern to avoid

The Week-3 practice anti-pattern is *"I posted 3 times and sent 5 DMs but none of them converted, so I feel like I failed."*

You didn't fail. Week 3's gate is **output + quality of reps**, not **outcomes**. Nobody closes from Day-18 output — that's not what the week is designed to do. Week 3 is designed to prove you can ship content and open conversations repeatedly. Outcomes follow reps; reps don't follow outcomes.

The only way to actually fail Week 3 is:
- Not shipping 3 posts
- Not opening 5 conversations
- Shipping 3 posts that all answer the same silent question
- Opening 5 DMs that all broke rule 1, 2, or 3

If you shipped real output and the conversations were structured, Week 3 is passed. Outcomes are a Month 2 measurement.

## Week 3 KPI — the unlock gate

Week 4 unlocks when you have all four:

- [ ] **3 posts shipped** — different silent questions answered
- [ ] **5 DM conversations opened** — real exchanges, following the three rules
- [ ] **Screenshot log submitted** — 10 screenshots + 10 lines of context
- [ ] **Loom reflection recorded + mentor review booked**

If you're short at Sunday 6 pm, use Sunday to finish. Unlock is automatic when all four are logged.

## Quiz

**Q1. To "ship 3 posts" for Week 3's gate, the posts must:**
- A) Be published on the same day
- B) Answer the same silent question for maximum reinforcement
- C) Each answer a different silent question (mix of Q1–Q5) ✓
- D) Be posted only on IG, never TikTok

**Why:** The gate is about *content-mix discipline*, not volume. Three Q2 posts would prove you know one thing. Three different-Q posts prove you can build a balanced content engine — which is what leads actually respond to. Q5 (offer post) is required because that's what opens the DM funnel.

**Q2. A DM "counts as opened" for Week 3's gate if:**
- A) You sent a message that got read
- B) You sent a message AND got a real reply (3+ bubbles back and forth) OR completed a CRAB cycle on a blue tick ✓
- C) You said hi to the prospect on IG
- D) You liked their post

**Why:** *Opened* requires exchange. A read-only message is still pending — you don't count it until they reply or until you've sent the CRAB follow-up and closed the loop either way. Quality over volume: 5 real exchanges is better proof of the rep than 20 one-way sends that all died.

**Q3. Week 4 unlocks when:**
- A) You closed your first case
- B) Your posts got over 100 likes
- C) 3 posts shipped + 5 DMs opened + screenshot log submitted + Loom + mentor review ✓
- D) Your mentor approves your pitch

**Why:** The gate is built around *output*, not outcomes. You don't need a closed case, viral likes, or pitch approval. You need to prove you can ship content and open real conversations — because those are the inputs that eventually produce the outcomes. Gating on outcomes would penalise beginners for not yet having compounding pipelines; gating on output measures the thing they can control today.

**Q4. Day 18 calls out the Week-3 anti-pattern: "I posted 3 times and sent 5 DMs but none of them converted, so I feel like I failed." Why is this framing wrong?**
- A) It's wrong to measure outcomes at all
- B) Week 3's gate is output + quality of reps, not outcomes — nobody closes from Day-18 output by design ✓
- C) Three posts are too few to measure
- D) Conversions only happen in Week 10

**Why:** Confusing input quality with output conversion is the fastest way to quit. Week 3's job is to prove you *can ship repeatedly* — which is the precondition for conversion, not the same thing as conversion. Outcomes follow reps over months; reps don't follow outcomes. Measuring Week 3 by conversions is measuring a seedling by fruit yield.

**Q5. Which of these still "counts" as a shipped post for the Week-3 gate?**
- A) A story that lives for 24 hours
- B) A reposted quote from someone else's account
- C) A feed post with a Q5 DM-keyword CTA ✓
- D) A comment you left on someone else's post

**Why:** The gate is specifically about feed posts (permanent, discoverable). Stories fade in 24h — they're a separate surface. Reposts don't build your authority. Comments are engagement, not output. The feed-post-with-CTA is the one that compounds — it stays up, it can be referenced in DMs, and it contributes to the Q5 function of opening conversations.

**Q6. The mentor review on Day 18 critiques two things only. What are they?**
- A) Grammar and aesthetics
- B) Whether posts answer different silent questions (not all Q2), and whether DMs follow all three non-negotiables (not opening with "hey, how are you?") ✓
- C) Whether you closed a case this week
- D) Whether your highlights look professional

**Why:** The narrow critique protects the learner from cosmetic feedback. Content-mix discipline (Q1-Q5 spread) and DM discipline (the 3 non-negotiables) are the two diagnostics that predict whether Week-4 and beyond will compound. Everything else is handleable in Weeks 4–10 as long as those foundations are right.

**Q7. A Week-3 FC ships 3 posts but all three are educational/expertise carousels. Is the gate passed?**
- A) Yes — three posts is three posts
- B) No — the gate requires *different silent questions* answered; 3 Q2 posts fails the content-mix criterion ✓
- C) Yes, with a warning
- D) No — the posts must all be videos

**Why:** The gate is deliberate about *mix*, not just count. A learner who ships 3 Q2 posts has proven they can ship volume but not that they can balance the trust-building work. Walk them back to Day 13, identify the other silent questions they skipped, and re-ship one of the posts as a different type. Three of the same kind is not three.
