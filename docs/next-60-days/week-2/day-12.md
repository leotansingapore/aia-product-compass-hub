# Day 12 — Practice: Deliver Intent Statement to 3 Prospects

> **The one idea for today:** An intent statement doesn't exist until you've delivered it to a real person. This is where Week 2 becomes real.

By the time you close today you'll have delivered your v2 intent statement to 3 real people (not a mentor, not a peer, actual prospects), self-rated each delivery on 4 dimensions (hook landed, pattern interrupt registered, buy-in secured, tone matched), and submitted a 3-line log + 1 Loom reflection. Those two artefacts unlock Week 3.

---

## The bar today

Most advisors who fail their first 12 months didn't fail because they had a weak pitch. They failed because they had a strong pitch they never delivered enough times.

This module's default failure mode is *learning without shipping*. Week 1 shipped a recorded intro (to tape, to mentor). Week 2 ships something harder — **real delivery to real prospects, with a real pulse on the other side of the conversation.**

Three deliveries this weekend. Three real prospects. No roleplay, no peer practice, no mentor warm-up.

---

## Who counts as a "real prospect"

Anyone you would genuinely want to have a Fact-Find with in the next 60 days. The bar:

- You know their name
- They know yours
- You would actually want to close them if the fit was right

**Counts:**
- An existing friend you haven't yet opened the advisor conversation with
- A warm-market contact you're reactivating
- Someone who responded to a DM or post this week
- A referral a client gave you but you haven't called yet

**Does not count:**
- Your mentor
- A classmate / peer
- Someone whose role is specifically to listen to you practise

The difference is the *stakes*. A mentor won't react the way a prospect does. You need the real pulse to know if your v2 actually works.

---

## Pre-delivery prep

Before each of the three deliveries, do a 5-minute warm-up:

1. **Re-read your v2.** Don't rehearse it — just re-read it once.
2. **Picture the specific prospect.** Not "a prospect" — this specific person. What profile are they? Which of your 5 tailored "what do you do?" answers matches?
3. **Pick your tonality mode.** You've had the *Certainty + Reason* training (Day 5). Which load-bearing sentence lands on Certainty? Which line ends with the Reason *"fair?"*
4. **Decide the format.** Are you delivering over phone, WhatsApp voice note, or in-person? Each format lands differently — plan which you're using *before* you dial.

---

## During delivery — the in-moment check

You'll feel the difference between delivery 1, 2, and 3. That's expected. Four in-moment things to notice:

- **Did the hook land?** Did they stop scrolling / pause the other thing they were doing?
- **Did the pattern interrupt register?** Watch for: a pause, a *"hmm"*, a *"huh, that's different"* reaction, or even a question back.
- **Did you secure buy-in?** Did they actually say *"yes, that sounds fair"* — not just *"mm-hmm"* politely?
- **Did your tone match your Day-5 drill?** Or did you rush the close because you were nervous?

You don't need to hit all four on delivery 1. You should be hitting 3 of 4 by delivery 3.

---

## Post-delivery — the 3-line log

After each delivery, write **three lines** in the same note:

1. **Who + context.** *"Called Amir, former colleague. Reactivation after 8 months."*
2. **What landed / didn't.** *"Hook landed, pattern interrupt got a laugh — he said 'that's a first.' Buy-in not clean — he said 'okay, let's see' instead of 'fair.'"*
3. **One thing to fix for delivery 2.** *"Slow the pace between the interrupt and the buy-in ask. I rushed it."*

Three lines × three deliveries = a nine-line log. That's your submission — not a story, not a novel. Nine lines.

---

## Format + submission

### The 3-line log
Paste the log below (or link to a Google Doc / Notion page):

```
DELIVERY 1 — [who + context]
[what landed / didn't]
[one fix for delivery 2]

DELIVERY 2 — [who + context]
[what landed / didn't]
[one fix for delivery 3]

DELIVERY 3 — [who + context]
[what landed / didn't]
[what I'd change for Week 3]
```

### The 1-Loom reflection (3–5 minutes)

Record a short Loom answering three questions:

1. Which of the 3 deliveries worked best, and why?
2. Which of the 4 ingredients (hook / impression / pattern interrupt / buy-in) needs the most work?
3. What's your v3 going to fix?

**Your mentor won't critique the content — they'll critique two things only:**
1. Did you actually deliver to 3 real prospects (vs roleplay)?
2. Did the buy-in land cleanly on at least one of the three?

Book the 10-minute mentor review before Sunday ends.

## Quiz

**Q1. A "real prospect" for Day 12 delivery is best defined as:**
- A) Your mentor, because they can give feedback
- B) A peer in the same cohort, because they know what to listen for
- C) Someone you know, who knows you, and who you'd actually want to close if the fit was right ✓
- D) Anyone you can get on a call

**Why:** The whole point of Day 12 is to test the v2 against a *real pulse*. Mentors and peers react differently — they're listening for technique, not fit. A prospect reacts to whether your opening earns their continued attention. That's the only feedback loop that matters this week.

**Q2. After each delivery, the 3-line log captures:**
- A) Everything the prospect said, verbatim
- B) Who + context · what landed or didn't · one fix for next time ✓
- C) A full debrief with your mentor
- D) A transcript of the call

**Why:** The log is a feedback tool, not an archive. Three lines keeps it lightweight enough that you'll actually write it — verbatim logs sound thorough but never get written. The *"one fix for next time"* line is the whole point: each delivery should be better than the last.

**Q3. Week 3 unlocks when you have:**
- A) Delivered to 3 real prospects, whether or not logged
- B) Intent statement v2 + 3 real deliveries logged + Loom reflection + mentor review booked ✓
- C) A good Loom
- D) At least one prospect saying yes to a full Fact-Find

**Why:** The gate is built around *ships that prove the work happened*, not outcomes. You don't need a *yes* from a prospect this week — you need to have delivered three times, reflected honestly, and booked the mentor review. Outcomes follow from reps; the gate protects the reps.

**Q4. Before each delivery, the 5-minute warm-up includes:**
- A) Rehearsing the whole statement 10 times
- B) Re-read v2 once, picture the specific prospect, pick tonality mode, decide format ✓
- C) Reading your entire Week 2 notes
- D) Calling your mentor first

**Why:** Rehearsal right before delivery tenses you up — you deliver in "performance mode" instead of conversation. Reading once, then picturing the specific person and picking your tonality + format, keeps you in conversation mode. Five minutes, not fifty.

**Q5. During delivery, which of these is *not* one of the 4 in-moment checks?**
- A) Did the hook land?
- B) Did the pattern interrupt register?
- C) Did you secure buy-in?
- D) Did you hit 150 words exactly ✓

**Why:** Word count is not the test. The 4 in-moment checks all test *whether the prospect responded the way the intent statement is supposed to make them respond* — attention pause at hook, interrupt reaction, clean buy-in agreement, tonal match. Length matters less than response pattern.

**Q6. The mentor on Day 12 critiques two things only:**
- A) Whether you memorised the script perfectly, and whether your Loom was under 5 min
- B) Whether you actually delivered to 3 real prospects (vs roleplay), and whether buy-in landed cleanly on at least one ✓
- C) Whether your pattern interrupt was clever
- D) Whether your writing was grammatically correct

**Why:** The narrow critique exists to protect the learner from cosmetic feedback that doesn't change behaviour. Did the work happen (3 real prospects)? Did anything land (one clean buy-in)? Two binary checks. Everything else is learnable across the next 50 weeks; those two questions are this week's only load-bearing diagnostic.

**Q7. "You should be hitting 3 of 4 in-moment checks by delivery 3." Why 3-of-4 rather than 4-of-4?**
- A) Because 4 is unreasonable at any stage
- B) Because delivery 3 is still early-stage practice — 3-of-4 is an honest first-ship bar; full 4-of-4 comes with more reps ✓
- C) Because mentors always grade on a curve
- D) Because buy-in is optional

**Why:** Expecting perfection on delivery 3 guarantees the learner either fakes their log or quits before Sunday. A 3-of-4 bar is rigorous but achievable — it forces improvement across the three deliveries (1 < 2 < 3) without demanding mastery. The fourth check catches up in Week 3 as tonality ships more reps.
