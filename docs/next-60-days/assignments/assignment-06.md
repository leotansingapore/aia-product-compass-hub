# Assignment 6 — 12-Month Plan: Signed + Delivered

> **What you're producing:** A one-page signed Year-1 plan with a specific FYC target, quarterly milestones, weekly rhythm, systems committed, the *one-thing* Month-3 focus, and your signature. Photographed. Saved to phone wallpaper. Delivered to your mentor.

## Why this matters

Day 60 is the pivot from *learning* to *executing*. The 60 days built the foundation; the next 10 months build the business. Without a written, signed, photographed plan, most Year-1 FCs drift back into ad-hoc rhythms. The plan is the commitment artefact that makes the next 10 months different from the default — and signing it in front of your mentor is what encodes the commitment into something external to willpower.

FCs who compound hardest by Year 5 almost universally had a signed Year-1 plan. FCs who drifted almost universally didn't.

## The plan — what it contains

One page. Not two. The constraint forces specificity.

### 1. Your Year-1 FYC target
Single number. Based on the Day-2 formula:

> **Annual FYC = Appointments/week × Close rate × Case size × 48 working weeks**

Plug your Day-59 actuals + a realistic stretch (~20–30% on each lever). Slightly stretching but believable; not delusional.

### 2. Quarterly milestones
One line per quarter:

| Quarter | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Q1 (Mo 3–5)** | Consolidate foundation — sustain Week-10 rhythm, close 3–5 more cases |
| **Q2 (Mo 6–8)** | Scale the engine — double weekly appointments, first client event |
| **Q3 (Mo 9–11)** | Deepen the book — A-client deepening + referral compounding |
| **Q4 (Mo 12)** | Close strong + plan Year 2 |

Plus specific numeric milestones per quarter if useful (e.g., *"Q1: $15K FYC cumulative, 6 closes"*).

### 3. The 5 weekly non-negotiables (from Day 58)
- [ ] Monday 3-hour calling block — protected
- [ ] Protected meeting grid Tue–Thu
- [ ] Friday weekly review — CAR scorecard + target list + 3 big moves
- [ ] Onboarding checklist runs automatically — Day 7 / 30 / 90 per client
- [ ] Sunday 30-min prep — pipeline, target list, calendar

### 4. The 3 systems (committed, built, running)
- [ ] Strategic Target List (Day 23) — 20+ names per month, ABC/123 tagged
- [ ] Onboarding checklist (Day 56) — Day 7 / 30 / 90 CRM-automated
- [ ] Touchpoint calendar (Day 57) — 4 types (Review / Value / Celebration / Reach-out), ~7 per year for B-tier

### 5. Mentor + community rhythm
- [ ] Weekly mentor check-in — 15–30 min
- [ ] Monthly peer roleplay session — 2–3 new FCs
- [ ] Quarterly senior-advisor session — 5+ years ahead

### 6. Your *one thing* from Day 59
One specific, measurable, observable-in-4-weeks behaviour. *Not* "improve prospecting". Something like:
> *"I'll hold 8 seconds of silence after every loaded Fact-Find question, tracked daily."*
> *"I'll run FACT Method verbatim on every Fact-Find for 30 days until it's reflex."*
> *"I'll hit 40 calls per week, every week, protected Monday morning block."*

### 7. Signature + date

Sign it. Date it. This is the commitment artefact.

## Submission

Upload one file:
- **PDF of the signed plan** (preferred), OR
- **Photograph of the signed plan** on actual paper, OR
- **Signed Canva/Notion document** with visible signature

Or paste a Google Doc / Canva / Dropbox link with view access in the notes field.

## The three things you do AFTER uploading

1. **Set as phone wallpaper.** You'll look at your phone 80 times today. This is how the plan reaches the 10pm-Wednesday moment.
2. **Print it** (or save to phone photos) — put a physical copy on your desk or fridge.
3. **Read it every Monday morning** before the calling block. This is the operating ritual for the next 10 months.

## The mentor delivery

Send the plan to your mentor. This isn't for approval — it's for *witnessing*. A signed plan held only by you is a private intention; a signed plan in your mentor's inbox is a committed one.

## Graduation

Submitting this assignment graduates you from the Next 60 Days module. What you have at this moment that you didn't have 60 days ago:

- A voice (intent statement, story, tonality, frame)
- A pipeline (Strategic Target List, warm-market named, referrals flowing)
- A pitch (tested through 3+ recorded pitches and live deliveries)
- A close (technique matched to profile, objection drills in reflex)
- An after-sales system (onboarding ready)
- A plan (signed, dated, photographed, read weekly)

Most advisors reach this level of structure in Year 3. You're here in Month 2.

**The work now is executing the plan.** Not learning more. Not optimising. *Executing* — every Monday, every referral ask, every Day-30 touchpoint, every weekly review. See you at Month 12.
