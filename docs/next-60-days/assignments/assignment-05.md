# Assignment 5 — Live Pitch: Full Integration + 8-Dim Self-Review

> **What you're producing:** One continuous recorded pitch (8–15 minutes) that integrates everything from Weeks 1–8. Live to a real prospect if possible; simulation with a mentor otherwise. Then the honest 8-dimension self-review that makes Week 9's priorities targeted rather than linear.

## Why this matters

Weeks 1–8 layered voice, intent statement, content, prospecting, referrals, DISC recognition, DISC tailoring, hot-button activation, question-asking, silence, sales angles, and product craft. Day 48 is the first full-integration check. Every element has to work together for the pitch to land — story (for I), structure (for C), hot-button callback (for depth), close technique (for commitment).

The 8-dimension self-review is *not* a grade. It's a **diagnostic** — the 2–3 lowest scores are what Week 9 (closing + objections) targets for your personalised prep. A pitch where closing was strong but objection handling was weak gets a different Week 9 than a pitch that stalled at the Fact-Find.

## Recording format

- **Live delivery (preferred):** with prospect consent, record from phone propped discreetly OR take notes + record 10-min debrief video immediately after
- **Simulation (acceptable):** with mentor or senior FC as pre-agreed profile + hot button, 8–15 min continuous take
- **Target length:** 8–15 min — short enough to re-watch, long enough to show full pitch flow
- **Continuous take** — single take, no stitched segments

## The 8 dimensions

Watch the recording twice — first for story, second for craft. Score 1–5 per dimension with specific evidence (quote the line).

| Dimension | What to watch for |
|---|---|
| **Intent statement** | Structured open with hook + impression + pattern interrupt + buy-in? Felt scripted or natural? |
| **Tonality** | Certainty on load-bearing sentences, Reason on close, Empathy on hot-button moments? |
| **DISC match** | Pace + energy + content fit the profile you identified? |
| **Hot button callback** | Did you explicitly quote something the prospect said earlier? |
| **Angle clarity** | Can you name primary + secondary (W/R/L/LS)? Visible in the recording? |
| **Feature-to-benefit** | Every feature translated to personal benefit tied to their hot button? Or feature-dump? |
| **Close technique** | Profile-matched (Assumptive for D, Choice for S, Procedural for C, warm for I)? Booked specific next step? |
| **Silence discipline** | Held 5–10 seconds after loaded questions, or filled the pauses? |

## Scoring interpretation

| Total / 40 | Diagnosis |
|---|---|
| 34+ | Ready for real-world cases; minor polish |
| 27–33 | Competent with 1–2 dimensions needing targeted Week-9 work |
| < 27 | Foundational gap; use Week 9 to re-record |

**Nobody scores 40/40 on a first integration pitch.** Being honest about the 3 lowest dimensions is the whole point.

## Submission

Fill the form below — pitch link, profile/angle identification, 8 scores with evidence, 3 lowest + Week-9 priorities.

**Book a 20-minute mentor review** (longer than usual — the mentor watches the recording with you and gives live feedback on delivery). Mentor specifically critiques:
1. Did the pitch *integrate* the Weeks 1–8 stack, or did parts feel detached?
2. Where would the prospect have been most likely to close or drop off?

## Using this recording in Week 9

Week 9 is The Close. The 3 lowest scores from your self-review directly determine where you lean hardest:

- Low *close technique* → prioritise Days 49–51 (closing techniques)
- Low *silence* or *hot button* → prioritise Day 44 + Day 40
- Low *feature-to-benefit* → revisit Day 47 before Day 54's drills
- Low *DISC match* → revisit the specific profile day (D32-35) you missed

Personalised prioritisation beats linear content.
