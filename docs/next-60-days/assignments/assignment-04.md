# Assignment 4 — DISC Roleplay Tape: 4 Profiles

> **What you're producing:** Same underlying scenario (Fact-Find opening to a new prospect), 4 different deliveries — one per DISC profile. 90 seconds each. On tape. The tape reveals whether your delivery actually shifts — which, for most new FCs, it barely does on the first recording.

## Why this matters

Knowing the 4 profiles intellectually is easy. Adapting your voice in 3 minutes live is the hard part. The gap between *"I know D differs from S"* in theory and *"my delivery actually shifts across the 4 profiles"* in practice is the skill Week 6 builds. You can only close the gap if you can *see* it — and the only way to see your own delivery is on tape.

Most new FCs are shocked by how little their tonality changes across profiles in their first recording. That shock is the unlock.

## The scenario — same across all 4

> You're at the opening of a first Fact-Find. Small talk is done. You're about to deliver your intent statement, 90-second pitch, and transition to the first Fact-Find question.

Record 4 versions. 90 seconds each. The *content* is roughly the same; the *delivery* changes entirely.

### D roleplay (90 sec)
- Pace: **fast** (15–20% faster than your default)
- Opener: cut to the chase. No warm-up.
- Pitch: outcome-first. *"I help [profile] achieve [outcome] — that's what we'll look at today."*
- First question: bottom-line. *"What's the one thing you'd want this meeting to accomplish?"*

### I roleplay (90 sec)
- Pace: matched to their high-energy default
- Opener: warmer, more animated, a compliment or observation about them
- Pitch: story-led. *"I'll tell you about a client who was in a similar stage…"*
- First question: emotion-driven. *"What's on your mind right now when you think about money?"*

### S roleplay (90 sec)
- Pace: **slow** (15–20% slower than your default). Longer pauses.
- Opener: genuine interest. *"How are the kids? How's the new role going?"*
- Pitch: assurance-led. *"There's no pressure to decide today — this meeting helps you see what you have."*
- First question: permission-seeking. *"Would it be okay if I asked about your family first?"*

### C roleplay (90 sec)
- Pace: measured, deliberate
- Opener: structured. *"Here's the agenda for today: 40 minutes on current setup, 15 on gaps, 15 on options."*
- Pitch: data-first. *"I've prepared a 1-page overview. Two assumptions I'm flagging — want me to walk through those so we align?"*
- First question: precise. *"Can you walk me through the exact policies you have, and when each was taken?"*

## Recording setup

- Camera at eye level. Tripod or laptop on books. Not handheld.
- Window light or lamp on your face.
- Quiet room.
- 4 separate takes, 90 sec each. Label each file.
- Start each take with *"Roleplay for [profile]"* on camera so intent is captured.

## Self-review — the "did you actually shift?" test

Watch all 4 same-day. Rate 1–5 on each:

| Dimension | D | I | S | C |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Pace match (did I speed up / slow down?) | | | | |
| Energy match (animation / calm fit?) | | | | |
| Content match (led with the right thing?) | | | | |
| Transition (same person adapting, or 4 different people?) | | | | |

**Your primary profile's roleplay will score highest** — it's your native delivery. **Your opposite profile's roleplay will score lowest** — counter-programming is the hardest skill. That lowest profile is Week 7's focus.

## The point

This assignment isn't for grading. It's for *seeing*. Once you've watched yourself fail to slow down for an S when you intended to, you can never unsee it in a real meeting. That awareness — not the tape itself — is the skill.
