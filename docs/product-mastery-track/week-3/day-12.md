# Day 2 — Run the Meeting: Fact-Find + 6-Phase Pitch Flow

> **Today's frame:** Before you draw the rocket-ship, you need three pieces of intelligence — the coverage gap, the budget reality, and the cash-value pain point. Then run the source's explicit 6-phase flow (Open → Illustrator → Pitch → iPOS+ → Layer Early CI → Package) for the close. Skip the fact-find and you'll argue against term later instead of qualifying for whole life upfront.

This is a merged module — Part 1 covers the fact-find. Part 2 covers the explicit 6-phase pitch flow from the source (videos 9–14). Estimated read + drill: ~60 minutes. Take in two sittings if you need to.

---

# Part 1 — The Fact-Find: What to Ask Before Pitching GPP

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Three things, in order — the gap, the budget, the cash-value pain. The first two come from the Total Wealth / CST sequence. The third is the question that filters term clients from whole-life clients before you even start pitching.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: surface a CI shortfall against the 5x-annual-income benchmark in under 3 minutes, anchor a 10%-of-income protection budget without flinching, and ask the cash-value pain-point question in your own voice.

---

## The fact-find has three jobs

1. **Surface the gap.** Use Goals Mapper or CST calculators to expose the CI / death shortfall against AIA's 5x-income benchmark.
2. **Anchor the budget.** 10% of annual income is the protection-budget benchmark for the *full* Total Wealth stack (CI / death / hospitalisation / accident).
3. **Triage with the cash-value question.** This is the most important step — and the most-skipped. The answer tells you whether GPP is the right product or whether the prospect is genuinely term-only.

If you finish a fact-find without surfacing all three, you'll lose the close at Phase 3 — guaranteed.

---

## The Total Wealth four-bucket setup

Open the conversation by naming the four protection buckets the prospect needs to address. GPP only addresses two of them (CI + death) — say so:

> *"For long-term planning, allocate about 10% of income. With that 10%, I can help you plan for life, CI, death, hospitalisation, and accident coverage."*

This is the broader context that makes GPP feel like *part of a plan*, not a one-off product push.

For a $60k income → ~$500/month protection budget. **Use this as your anchor for every recommendation.**

---

## Surface the CI gap (the 5x benchmark)

LIA / industry guideline: critical illness cover should equal **5 years of annual income** (recovery + family income replacement). Run the math out loud:

> *"Based on CST, you need at least 5 years of income for CI coverage. Currently you have a shortfall."*

Concrete: $60k income → $300k CI cover needed. If they have $0 CI today, that's the gap GPP closes.

For early CI specifically, the recovery-shorter benchmark is **3 years of annual income** ($180k for the same prospect). Use this when proposing the half-half Early CI add-on later (Day 2 Part 2 Phase 5).

---

## The cash-value pain-point question (the triage)

This is the single most important question in a GPP fact-find. Ask it slowly, and *wait* for the answer:

> *"Mr Prospect, let's just say you pay this $500 a month for insurance and you pay this all the way and at the end of the day you don't get any money back. How would you feel?"*

The verbatim diagnostic for the answer:

> *"Then a person would say: quite a waste, ah. Wah, I pay this $5 a month, $10 a month, I don't get anything back. That seems like a waste to me."*

When you hear that — the *"quite a waste"* response — the prospect has just told you GPP is the right product.

> *"Immediately I'll know that this guy is very concerned about the cash value."*

If instead they say *"I don't care, I just want maximum cover for the lowest premium until 65"* — they're a term client. Don't pitch GPP. Pitch term and respect the close. They'll refer you to whole-life prospects later.

---

## What a complete GPP fact-find looks like

5–8 minutes max. You should walk away with:

- ✅ Annual income (the 5x and 10% anchors)
- ✅ Existing CI cover (the gap)
- ✅ Cash-value pain answered (triage)
- ✅ Family situation (dependants, mortgage, kids — booster years)
- ✅ Budget comfort with 10% of income (~$500/month at $60k)
- ✅ Pay-term preference indicated (15 / 20 / 25 yr — usually 25)

If any of these is missing, the close at Phase 6 will fumble. Go back and ask.

---

## Drill (Part 1)

1. **Practise the cash-value question** in your own voice, 3 different ways. Pick the one that sounds most like you.
2. **Run the 5x-income gap math** out loud for 3 hypothetical incomes ($48k / $84k / $120k). Recite the gap each time.
3. **Pull up your last 3 first-meeting prospects.** For each, write whether you surfaced (a) the gap, (b) the budget, (c) the cash-value pain. The missing ones are why you didn't close (or had to come back).
4. **Pre-write your "10%-of-income" anchor sentence** — what you'd say verbatim. Save it as a snippet.

# Part 2 — The 1-1 Pitch Flow (Phase 1–6): How to Actually Run the Meeting

> **The one idea for Part 2:** The source describes an explicit 6-phase flow that maps cleanly onto a single 25-minute meeting. Each phase ends with a specific micro-commitment. By Phase 5 the prospect has cleared the four hoops; Phase 6 is the formal binary close (with-Early-CI or without). Run the phases out of order at your peril.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to: name all 6 phases in order, know which micro-commitment each phase is hunting for, recognise the Apple Store "layer last" move at Phase 5, and run the full appointment in under 30 minutes.

---

## The 6 phases

```
Phase 1     Phase 2        Phase 3            Phase 4         Phase 5             Phase 6
Open    →   Illustrator →  Pitch cash value → iPOS+ quote  →  Layer Early CI  →   Package + close
```

| Phase | Target time | Target micro-commit |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Open (Total Wealth + cash-value question) | 5 min | "Yes, I'd feel it's a waste if I get nothing back" |
| 2. Illustrator (rocket-ship + travel-class) | 3 min | Multiplier picked (default 3x) |
| 3. Pitch cash value (now-vs-+10-years + warranty) | 5 min | "OK, I see the value" |
| 4. iPOS+ quotation (surrender value column) | 5 min | "Numbers look fair" |
| 5. Layer Early CI (Apple Store layering) | 4 min | "Yes, with Early CI" |
| 6. Package + close (UCC + reinforce 4 buckets) | 3 min | Sign |
| **Total** | **~25 min** | **4 hoops cleared** |

---

## Phase 1 — Open (5 min)

This is your Day 2 Part 1 fact-find compressed into the meeting:

1. Run the Total Wealth four-bucket frame
2. Anchor the 10%-of-income budget
3. Ask the cash-value question
4. Surface the CI gap with the 5x benchmark
5. Position the limited-pay / lifelong cover / sure-win framing

The bridge into Phase 2:

> *"It is a limited payment term, meaning from now until 50 years old, you pay, but you get coverage for life. You're putting in $72,000 and getting back $285,000. There's actually a profit, the yield is 2.11%."*

**Phase 1 micro-commit target:** the *"quite a waste"* answer to the cash-value question.

---

## Phase 2 — Illustrator (3 min)

Set the illustrator defaults. Don't volunteer the rocket-ship analogy unless the prospect probes:

| Setting | Default |
|---|---|
| Cover | $300k |
| Multiplier | 3x |
| Pay term | 25 years |
| Drop-off | 65 |
| Smoker status | Non-smoker (confirm) |

If they ask about the multiplier or want a comparison — that's when the rocket-ship + travel-class diagram comes out (Day 1 Part 2).

**Phase 2 micro-commit target:** multiplier picked. Default 3x.

---

## Phase 3 — Pitch cash value (5 min)

This is where most FCs fumble — they lead with coverage. **Lead with cash value.** Coverage is table stakes; cash value is the conversion.

The verbatim:

> *"I'll just say, oh, you know, it's this amount, $2,800, and can you see, I'll focus more on the part where they get cash value, because honestly that is the biggest, attractive point of view."*

Then the now-vs-+10-years contrast:

| If you start now (age 25) | If you wait 10 years (age 35) |
|---|---|
| $2,800/yr × 25 yrs = $72,000 in | $4,160/yr × 25 yrs = $104,000 in |
| Same cover, same drop-off 65 | ~$32,000 more for less coverage |

The script:

> *"You buy this plan today, then maybe 25, 30, 40 years later, you call and say 'do I still have that plan?' And you'll find maybe $100k+ sitting inside, waiting for your retirement."*

The warranty analogy — for prospects who push back on premium:

> *"Think of it like a warranty on a $300,000 home. The warranty premium is small relative to the asset it protects."*

**Phase 3 micro-commit target:** *"OK, I see the value"* — verbalised back to you.

---

## Phase 4 — iPOS+ quotation (5 min)

Generate the actual quote at FHR (Financial Health Review). Walk through it focused on three columns:

1. **Surrender value column** — show how it grows even after pay term ends
2. **Post-pay-term cash growth** — frame as "set and forget, money keeps working"
3. **Partial vs full surrender** — you almost never use this; it's there for completeness

Keep it tight. Don't read every row of a 30-year table — anchor on year 25 (end of pay), year 35 (post-pay growth), year 40 (retirement).

**Phase 4 micro-commit target:** *"Numbers look fair"* — no objection on the cash value column.

---

## Phase 5 — Layer Early CI (the Apple Store move, 4 min)

This is the source's most explicit teaching. Quote:

> *"When you buy a MacBook from the Apple Store, they don't tell you straight away — oh, you've got to get AppleCare, the keyboard, the MagSafe, the Apple Pencil. They tell you the price first, then they layer."*

Same move with GPP:

1. Phase 4 closed on the Major-CI-only price (~$2,800/yr)
2. NOW you layer Early CI — with the half-half split

The half-half split:

> *"Major CI covers 73 conditions. Early CI covers about 150 conditions plus 15 special conditions. We can split your $300k CI cover as $150k Early + $150k Major. That gives you full coverage at every stage of every condition."*

The cost differential:

> *"It's only $60 more per month to get this additional $150K Early CI coverage."*

(Source example: $3,200/yr Major-CI-only vs $3,995/yr with half-half Early CI = ~$795/yr = ~$60/month.)

The transparency move:

> *"I just want to be very transparent with you and show you the critical illnesses that are covered."*

Walk them through 2–3 conditions live (e.g. *carcinoma in situ* via live search). Don't read the whole list — pick conditions that resonate (e.g. cancer types if family history was mentioned in fact-find).

The binary close (this is Phase 5's conversion):

> *"Mr Prospect, would you prefer without Early CI or with Early CI?"*

**Phase 5 micro-commit target:** *"With Early CI"* (most prospects).

---

## Phase 6 — Package + close (3 min)

Two moves:

### 1. Package GPP + UCC

The source's explicit packaging strategy:

> *"I do not sell the Early CI part — I just purely sell the Major CI part. And then I package the rest on a UCC for the Early CI part. The life plan gives the coverage for the Major CI part, the death part, and the savings part. For subsequent claims, recurring claims, and Early CI claims, that's where the UCC would actually come in."*

(Day 4 Part 2 covers the full UCC packaging — for Phase 6 today, just plant the seed.)

### 2. Reinforce the four buckets

Close with the value-stack reassurance:

> *"Death disability settled, CI settled, early CI settled, savings settled — basically all four areas are now adequately covered."*

> *"Constantly reinforcing, reassuring the customer that you're doing a good thing."*

**Phase 6 micro-commit target:** signature on FHR + iPOS+.

---

## The four-hoops progressive commitment

The source frames the close as a sequence of 4 progressive yeses. Never collapse them into one decision:

| Hoop | What the prospect agrees to | Phase |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "I need CI cover" | Phase 1 |
| 2 | "I need to buy now (not later)" | Phase 3 (now-vs-+10-years) |
| 3 | "3x multiplier vs 2x" | Phase 2 + 4 |
| 4 | "I need Early CI added" | Phase 5 |

Phase 6 close is then trivial — they've already cleared the four hoops.

---

## The pacing rule

If you're approaching the 25-minute mark and you're still in Phase 4, you're losing them. Compress the iPOS+ walkthrough, jump to the Phase 5 binary close, and book the implementation for the next session. **Better to close in two visits than to lose in one.**

## Drill (Part 2)

1. **Recite the 6 phases out loud** — name, time budget, micro-commit. Do it 3 times.
2. **Pick a real prospect** (or simulate). Run Phase 1–4 in front of a mirror. Time it. Target: 18 minutes total. Most FCs run 23+ on the first try.
3. **Memorise the standard close numbers** ($300k / 3x / 25-yr / drop-off 65 / ~$2,800/yr / ~$285k cash at 100). Recite without looking.
4. **Practise the Apple Store layering script** in 3 different tonalities. Pick the one that sounds most like you.
5. **Walk through one full quotation in iPOS+** — practise the surrender-value-column read live. If your iPOS+ access isn't familiar, fix that first; it's the Phase 4 weapon.
