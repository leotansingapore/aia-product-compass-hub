# Day 1 — PWV Foundations: 5-Min Intro + THE PWV Drawing Concept

> **Today's frame:** PWV is the canonical pre-retiree spine product. The 7-panel Drawing Concept is its irreplaceable asset — built live on whiteboard or iPad using the prospect's OWN CPF balances and existing policies. Master this single move and the rest of the curriculum is application; skip it and you're a brochure-reader.

This is the longest module in the track. Part 1 covers what PWV is. Part 2 covers THE Drawing Concept (the 7-panel signature whiteboard). Estimated read + drill: ~70 minutes.

# Part 1 — PWV in 5 Minutes: What It Is, Who It's For, Why It Exists

> **The one idea for Part 1:** PWV is a 5-year premium-paying ILP designed for pre-retirees converting accumulated wealth (CPF excess, maturing endowments, lazy cash) into a quarterly dividend income stream while preserving capital. Not a product pitch in the first 5 minutes — permission to have the rest of the conversation.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: describe PWV in two sentences, name the three structural "moats" vs CPF Life, recite the four bonus types, and recognise when PWV is the wrong product (under-50 prospects, no accumulated wealth).

---

## What PWV actually is

A SGD-denominated, **5-year premium-paying Investment-Linked Plan** issued by AIA. 100% of regular premium invested from day 1 (no upfront sales load) [Brochure p.2, PS p.1].

The product summary technically allows up to 8-year premium term, but the 5-year flagship structure is the field standard. Designed for pre-retirees aged 50-65 who have:
- CPF OA excess (after FRS at 55)
- Maturing endowments
- Existing legacy ILPs underperforming
- Lazy cash sitting in fixed deposits

PWV converts these into a quarterly dividend income stream that the client can either spend now or compound into a bigger income stream later.

The line that opens every PWV conversation:

> *"AIA Platinum Wealth Venture 2.0 is a 5-year commitment that gives you the freedom to put your money to other uses thereafter."*

Then the framing line:

> *"From day one, 100% of your premium goes to work. There's no upfront sales load eating into your capital."*

## The 3 structural moats vs CPF Life

PWV's positioning is NOT "CPF Life is bad" — CPF Life is compulsory and provides guaranteed income. PWV's three structural advantages address CPF Life's three structural limitations:

| | CPF Life | PWV |
|---|---|---|
| **Interest after 65** | 0% (annuity, not savings) | Continues compounding |
| **Bequest after 81** | 0% (depleted) | Capital guaranteed on death |
| **Liquidity** | None (locked) | Full liquidity after year 7 |

[PS p.1, Section 3.1]

The bridge:

> *"We're not saying CPF Life is bad — it's compulsory and it provides guaranteed income. But it may not be the most efficient tool for your full retirement picture."*

## The 4 bonus types (memorise)

| Bonus | Value | Trigger |
|---|---|---|
| **Welcome Bonus** | 16% / 26% / 35% (cumulative across 5 years) | Premium band: $7.8K-$8.99K / $9K-$11.99K / $12K+ [PS p.2, Section 3.3] |
| **Investment Bonus** | 2.5% per year | At start of policy years 9, 10, 11, 12 [PS p.2, Section 3.4] |
| **Performance Bonus** | 0.30% p.a. of Regular Premium Policy Value | From year 9 onwards [PS p.2, Section 3.5] |
| **Loyalty / Reset** | (covered Day 3) | After year 10 the supplementary charge stops |

## The 5 numbers you must know cold

| # | Number | What it means |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **S$7,800/year** | Minimum regular premium (S$650/month) [PS p.6, Section 6.1] |
| 2 | **3.60% p.a.** | Supplementary charge on Regular Premium Policy Value, monthly, **first 10 years only** [PS p.4, Section 5.2] |

## Target client profile

- **Age 50-65** approaching retirement
- **Has accumulated wealth** to redeploy (CPF excess, maturing endowments, legacy investments)
- **Wants quarterly income stream** plus capital preservation for family
- **Has at least 5 years** of premium-paying capacity

## Anti-market

> *"PWV isn't right for someone under 50 without accumulated wealth, or for someone who needs short-term liquidity in years 1-7. If you don't have at least 5 years of stable premium-paying capacity, this isn't your plan."*

## The 5-minute intro script

> *"This is AIA Platinum Wealth Venture 2.0 — PWV. The reason we use this for pre-retirees specifically: three things.*
>
> *One — capital preservation. Death benefit is the higher of total premiums paid or current policy value. Your family never receives less than what you put in.*
>
> *Two — quarterly dividends. From year 1, you can either receive cash payouts via PayNow or reinvest. You decide each quarter.*
>
> *Three — liquidity. After 7 years, full access to your cash value with no surrender charges. The plan works for you, not the other way around.*
>
> *We're not selling you a policy today. We're showing you whether you're ready for retirement, and where the gaps are.*
>
> *Want me to draw your retirement picture using your own numbers?"*

That's 60 seconds. End with the question — they say yes, you draw the 7 panels.

# Part 2 — THE PWV Drawing Concept (7-Panel Signature Whiteboard)

> **The one idea for Part 2:** This is the single most important section in the entire 5-day curriculum. The PWV Drawing Concept is a 7-panel sequential whiteboard built LIVE in front of the client using THEIR OWN CPF balances and existing policies. The client watches a story being constructed using their numbers — by Panel 7, they've invested 25 minutes of attention and the conclusion (buy PWV) feels like THEIR conclusion, not yours. Master this and you'll close cases that brochure-readers will never close.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to draw all 7 panels from memory in under 25 minutes, deploy each panel's verbatim script, recognise when to use the FRS-vs-ERS optional aside, and recognise when to skip the drawing (if the prospect is hostile or time-pressured).

---

## Why the drawing works (psychology)

Three reasons the live drawing beats slides:

1. **Specificity** — using the client's own OA, SA, MA balances (pulled live from CPF Healthcare Dashboard or SingPass) makes the gap feel real, not generic
2. **Construction** — watching a story being built in real time (vs reading slides) creates 3-5x more retention
3. **Ownership** — by Panel 7, the client has invested 25 minutes of attention, and the conclusion feels like their conclusion

> *"If they can re-tell it to their spouse that night, they will buy it."*

Replicate the drawing exactly. Don't innovate.

## The 7 panels (verbatim sequence)

### Panel 1 — Setup the CPF picture (use client's REAL numbers)

> *"Mrs Tan, you're 52 today. Let me draw your CPF picture. Your OA is $140,000, your SA is $235,000, your MA is $75,000. Combined OA plus SA: $380,000. At age 55, your OA and SA accounts are going to merge into something called the Retirement Account."*

Sketch:
```
Age 52 today               Age 55: OA + SA → Retirement Account (RA)
─────────────              ─────────────────────────────────────
OA  S$140,000              FRS:    S$236,000 (locked into RA)
SA  S$235,000              Excess: S$144,000 (stays in OA)
MA  S$75,000
```

### Panel 2 — The FRS reveal

> *"Let me check the CPF Life Estimator inside our presenter. Female, 52 years old, choosing FRS — the amount you need to set aside at 55 is $236,000. So out of your $380,000 combined balance, $236,000 is locked into the RA. The remaining $144,000 stays in your OA. That $144,000 is your ammunition."*

The phrase "**your ammunition**" is the verbatim — use it. It plants the funding-mechanism idea for Panel 7.

### Panel 3 — The growth from 55 to 65

> *"From 55 to 65, your $236,000 RA grows at 4% per annum. By age 65, it becomes about $350,000. At 65, you start receiving CPF Life payouts of about $1,760 per month. For as long as you live."*

Sketch:
```
Age 55         Age 65
──────         ──────
RA $236K  →    RA $350K (4% p.a.)
                   ↓
              CPF Life payout: ~$1,760/month for life
```

### Panel 4 — The three limitations (the emotional pivot)

This is the panel that creates the gap.

> *"Now here's something most people don't know. After age 65, your RA stops accumulating interest. The $1,760 you receive monthly is simply a drawdown of your own capital. Take out your calculator: $350,000 divided by $1,760 — that's 198 months, about 16 years. Your RA balance hits zero around age 81."*

Pause. Let it land.

> *"Three things to notice:*
> *One: no interest after 65 — you lose 4% of $350K every year.*
> *Two: no bequest after 81 — if you pass at 81 or later, your kids receive zero.*
> *Three: no liquidity — the $350K is fully locked up. If an emergency happens, you cannot touch it."*

Three things. Memorise the list.

### Panel 4b — Optional FRS vs ERS aside (only if client raises topping-up)

> *"FRS requires you to set aside $236K and pays $1,760/month. ERS requires $472K — exactly twice the capital — but pays $3,420/month, which is only 1.94 times. So topping up to ERS is actually less efficient on a per-dollar basis, plus you lock up more liquidity."*

Don't volunteer this. Use only if the prospect asks about ERS or topping up CPF.

### Panel 5 — Side-by-side PWV vs CPF Life

> *"Now let me draw the alternative. Same starting point — $200,000 invested over 5 years, $40,000 per year. Inside our PWV illustrator, at age 65 the value is also about $350,000.*
>
> *But look at the difference:*
> *CPF Life gives you $1,760/month. PWV at 6% dividend on $350K gives you about $2,041/month.*
> *CPF Life: no interest after 65. PWV: dividends continue.*
> *CPF Life: zero bequest after 81. PWV: capital guaranteed on death — the higher of account value or capital invested.*
> *CPF Life: no liquidity. PWV: capital fully liquid after 7 years."*

Sketch:
```
                CPF Life    PWV
Capital:        $350K       $350K
Monthly:        $1,760      $2,041
Post-65 growth: 0%          continues
Bequest at 85:  $0          capital floor + value
Liquidity:      None        Full after Year 7
```

### Panel 6 — The Retirement Income Gap chart

> *"What income do you actually want in retirement? Let's say $5,000 a month.*
>
> *CPF Life gives you $1,760. Your existing Manulife plan gives you $500 from age 70. Your Prudential plan gives you another $500. That's $2,760.*
>
> *Your gap is $2,240/month. At 6% dividend yield, that gap needs about $400,000 in capital. At 3% in fixed deposits, the same $2,000/month gap needs $800,000. At 1%, it needs $2.4 million."*

Capital Requirement Spectrum:

| Yield | Capital for $2K/month |
|---|---|
| 6% (PWV target) | **$400,000** |
| 3% (fixed deposit) | $800,000 |
| 1% (savings) | $2,400,000 |

The number that lands: **6% needs HALF the capital that 3% needs.** That's the leverage.

### Panel 7 — The Funding Mechanism (the "no new money" reveal)

This is the panel that turns the close.

> *"Here's the beautiful part. You don't need new money.*
>
> *Year 1, 2, 3 — you fund $40K from cash.*
> *Year 4 — your $144K OA excess unlocks at 55, that funds the next year.*
> *Year 5 — surrender value from your old endowment that matures at 56.*
>
> *The plan funds itself from resources you already have."*

The funding priority (memorise):
1. **CPF OA excess** at 55
2. **Policy surrender values** (maturing endowments, legacy ILPs)
3. **Liquid cash** (bank balances, fixed deposits)

The clinching line:

> *"You don't need new money to fund this plan. The plan funds itself from resources you already have."*

## When NOT to draw the 7 panels

- **Time-pressured meeting (<30 min)** — truncate to Panels 1-4 only, save Panels 5-7 for Meeting 2
- **Prospect is hostile or skeptical** — switch to Capital Requirement Spectrum chart only (Panel 6)
- **Prospect already understands CPF Life mechanics** — skip Panels 1-4, jump to Panel 5

For a fresh, normal pre-retiree, all 7 panels at full delivery in 25 minutes.
