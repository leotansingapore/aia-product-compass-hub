# Day 2 — Run the Meeting: 16-Point Discovery + 7-Phase Pitch Flow

> **Today's frame:** Discovery is where amateur FCs reveal themselves. The 16-point checklist is non-negotiable — every minute saved in discovery costs five minutes in objection handling. Then the source's explicit 7 phases run in order, with Phase 5 (Gap Analysis) as the emotional fulcrum and Phase 6 (Solution) showing the discipline of restraint.

This is a merged module — Part 1 covers the 16-point Discovery. Part 2 covers the 7-phase pitch flow. Estimated read + drill: ~65 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — The Fact-Find: 16-Point Discovery Deep Dive

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Run the full 16-point checklist EVERY time, even when the client seems impatient. Use SingPass IN the meeting — never accept "I'll send you my policies later." Every data point you skip becomes a hole in your proposal that the client uses to delay.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: deliver the SingPass-in-meeting move fluently, run the 16-point checklist in 15-20 minutes, identify the household decision-maker, and walk away with everything Phase 5 (Gap Analysis) needs.

---

## The bridging language for the data demand

The client may resist the depth of the discovery. Bridge with:

> *"Quality financial advice depends on the quality of information provided. The more I understand your situation, the better my recommendations will be."*

This converts the discovery from "interrogation" to "professional work."

## The SingPass-in-meeting move (highest-leverage tactic)

> *"Before we go further, can I help you log in via SingPass right now? It pulls every insurance policy across every insurer in one place — saves us both a lot of follow-up emails."*

This single move:
- Converts a 2-week document-chasing cycle into a 10-minute live extraction
- Reveals legacy policies the client may have forgotten
- Surfaces the cross-sell restructure opportunities (Day 4 Part 2)
- Demonstrates FC professionalism in a tangible way

Use it in 80%+ of pre-retiree meetings.

## The household decision-maker check

> *"Is there anyone else who's involved in financial decisions at home? I'd love to make sure they're part of the conversation from the start."*

If decisions are made jointly with spouse, schedule Meeting 2 with both of them. Don't waste a meeting that has to be re-run.

## The 16 data points

Run in any order, but capture all 16:

### Personal & retirement
1. **Age, DOB, retirement target age**
2. **Monthly income** (gross + net) plus bonus structure
3. **Monthly expenses** (fixed + variable)
4. **Desired retirement monthly income** (in today's dollars)
5. **Risk appetite** (1-10 scale or conservative/moderate/adventurous)
6. **Previous investment experience** (good AND bad)

### Capital sources
7. **CPF balances** — OA, SA, MA, RA (request screenshot or pull live via Healthcare Dashboard)
8. **Properties** — own/rent, mortgage balance, remaining tenure, rental income
9. **Investments** — stocks, bonds, unit trusts, fixed deposits, ETFs
10. **SRS account and balance** (deductions up to S$15,300/year for citizens/PRs)
11. **Endowment plans** + maturity dates + amounts

### Existing protection
12. **ALL insurance policies across ALL insurers** (use SingPass)

### Family + estate
13. **Family** — spouse income/assets, children ages, dependents
14. **Estate planning** — will, LPA, AMD, nominations status
15. **Who makes financial decisions** in the household

### Underwriting
16. **Health conditions** affecting underwriting

## The bridging question between sections

> *"What have you done so far for your retirement? Walk me through everything — CPF, properties, insurance, investments, even fixed deposits and SRS."*

Open-ended. Captures most of points 7-12 in a single answer.

## What a complete PWV fact-find looks like

15-20 minutes. You should walk away with:
- ✅ All 16 data points captured
- ✅ SingPass policy list pulled (or at least a complete inventory)
- ✅ CPF balances confirmed
- ✅ Decision-maker identified
- ✅ Household consent for Meeting 2 if spouse needed
- ✅ Health status for underwriting feasibility

If any data point is missing, the gap analysis fumbles. Go back and ask.

---

## Drill (Part 1)

1. **Memorise the 16 data points** in your own grouping. Recite without looking.
2. **Practise the SingPass-in-meeting script** — ask 3 different ways. Pick the most natural.
3. **Practise the bridging language** ("Quality financial advice depends on quality of information provided") in your own voice.
4. **Pull up your last 3 pre-retiree meetings.** For each, list which data points you missed.

# Part 2 — The 7-Phase Pitch Flow (Phases 1-7)

> **The one idea for Part 2:** The 7 phases run in non-negotiable order. Phase 5 (Gap Analysis) is the emotional fulcrum — the moment the client FEELS the cost of doing nothing. Phase 6 must NOT collapse into a product pitch — discipline of restraint at Phase 6 is what separates closers from pitchers. Then Phase 7 closes with same-day summary PDF.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver all 7 phases by name with time budgets, deploy the verbatim phrases for each, recognise Phase 5's emotional fulcrum, and avoid the Phase 6 collapse.

---

## The 7 phases (memorise the time budget cold)

```
Phase 1     Phase 2          Phase 3        Phase 4         Phase 5         Phase 6           Phase 7
Rapport →   Framework    →   Discovery  →   CPF Edu     →   Gap Analysis →  Solution Intro →  Close
(5-10 min)  (5 min)          (15-20 min)    (10 min)        (15 min)        (5 min)           (2 min)
```

Total: ~60 minutes for opening meeting (Meeting 1).

| Phase | Time | Micro-commit |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Rapport (FORM) | 5-10 min | Trust + primary concern surfaced |
| 2. Framework Introduction | 5 min | "I see how this works" |
| 3. Discovery Deep Dive (16 pts) | 15-20 min | All data captured |
| 4. CPF Education | 10 min | "I didn't know about age 81" |
| 5. Gap Analysis (emotional fulcrum) | 15 min | "I have a gap" |
| 6. Solution Introduction (RESTRAINT) | 5 min | "OK, prepare a proposal" |
| 7. Close (Meeting 2 booked) | 2 min | Meeting 2 within 10 days, WhatsApp group set up |

---

## Phase 1 — Rapport (FORM framework)

| Element | What to ask |
|---|---|
| **F**amily | Spouse, children, parents — relationships and dependants |
| **O**ccupation | Current role, years of experience, retirement target |
| **R**ecreation | Hobbies, travel, wellness |
| **M**oney | Surface the primary concern |

The bridging question:

> *"What does your ideal retirement look like?"*

Listen for emotional language — "I want to travel," "I worry about my parents," "I don't want to be a burden." These become anchors for Phase 5.

## Phase 2 — Framework Introduction (5 minutes)

Present the **4-Step Golden Retirement Planning Framework** without yet pitching PWV:

1. Retirement Readiness Assessment
2. Identify Redundant Assets
3. Optimize Portfolio
4. Execution & Review

The opening line:

> *"We specialize in helping pre-retirees optimize their retirement income."*

Then plant the no-pressure frame:

> *"There's absolutely no obligation to proceed with any recommendations."*

> *"Let me show you how this works with a typical client similar to you."*

Use a sample case study (NOT the prospect's data yet) to demonstrate the framework.

## Phase 3 — Discovery Deep Dive

This is Day 2 Part 1's 16-point checklist compressed into the meeting. Run all 16. SingPass in-meeting.

## Phase 4 — CPF Education (10 minutes)

This is where the 7-panel Drawing Concept (Day 1 Part 2) Panels 1-4 land. Walk the prospect through:
- CPF setup (their actual numbers)
- FRS reveal
- Growth from 55 to 65
- The three limitations (no interest after 65, no bequest after 81, no liquidity)

Verbatim phrases:

> *"After age 81, remaining capital goes to the CPF pool, not your family. CPF Life is an annuity, not a savings account."*

> *"Females receive slightly higher CPF Life payouts because of longevity tables."*

> *"If you have a property, you can pledge it and reduce your RA requirement."*

The age 81 fact is the emotional anchor. Let it land.

## Phase 5 — Gap Analysis (the emotional fulcrum)

This is THE moment the client feels the cost of doing nothing. Walk the gap:

> *"$4,000 desired minus $1,400 CPF Life equals $2,600 monthly gap. That's $31,200 per year."*

> *"At 3% in fixed deposits, you'd need $800,000 to generate $2,000 per month. At 6% in our dividend portfolio, you need $400,000. That's the difference between working 10 more years or retiring comfortably."*

The inflation reframe (always include):

> *"$5,000 today equals $7,000 at 65 equals $9,500 at 75. Inflation isn't a future problem — it's a today problem."*

[Inflation reference: 2.5-3% annually — Video 6]

The Capital Requirement Spectrum:

| Capital | At 6% dividend | At 3% FD |
|---|---|---|
| $200K | $1,000/month | $500/month |
| **$400K** | **$2,000/month** | $1,000/month |
| $600K | $3,000/month | $1,500/month |
| $800K | $4,000/month | $2,000/month |
| $1M | $5,000/month | $2,500/month |

Pause. Let it land. **Don't pitch PWV here.**

## Phase 6 — Solution Introduction (the restraint)

This is the discipline test. The prospect now WANTS the solution. The temptation is to pitch PWV. **DON'T.**

> *"Let me prepare a detailed proposal for our next meeting."*

> *"There are four passive income methods we can use: dividends, annuities, rental income, and optimizing redundant assets."*

Outline the direction (passive income via dividend-paying portfolio + restructured legacy assets) without naming the product.

The discipline:

> *"The client who watches you not pitch trusts you twice as much."*

## Phase 7 — Close the Meeting (2 minutes)

Three moves:

### 1. Summarise 3 specific findings

> *"Three things came out of today: [gap of $2,600/month], [no liquidity in CPF], [your old policy is yielding only 1.5%]."*

Specific numbers. Not vague summaries.

### 2. Book Meeting 2 within 10 days

> *"Can we meet again next [day], same time? It's important we don't lose momentum."*

Beyond 10 days, urgency dies. Push for within 7 days.

### 3. WhatsApp group + same-day summary PDF

> *"Before you leave — please add me on WhatsApp. I'll send you the summary PDF tonight."*

Include spouse on WhatsApp if applicable. Send the summary PDF that NIGHT — it reinforces the gap during the gap between meetings.

## The 4 passive income methods (mention at Phase 6)

1. **Dividend-paying portfolio** (PWV — but don't name it yet)
2. **Annuities** (CPF Life — already in play)
3. **Rental income** (property — surface only if relevant)
4. **Optimizing redundant assets** (the restructure cross-sell — Day 4 Part 2)

This positions PWV as ONE of four methods, which makes the eventual recommendation feel considered, not pushed.

## The pacing rule

Meeting 1 should NEVER cross 75 minutes. If you're at 75 and still in Phase 5, compress Phase 6 to 2 minutes and book Meeting 2. Don't trade fatigue for content.

1. **Recite the 7 phases by name** with time budgets. 3 times.
2. **Memorise FORM** (Family, Occupation, Recreation, Money).

5. **Practise the Phase 7 same-day-summary commitment** — book Meeting 2 + WhatsApp group + send PDF.
6. **Run a roleplay.** Pair with a peer. Run all 7 phases in 60 minutes. Time yourself.
