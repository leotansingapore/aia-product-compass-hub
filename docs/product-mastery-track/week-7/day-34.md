# Day 4 — Defence + Expansion: Top 5 Objections + APA→PWV Restructure

> **Today's frame:** Objections in Meeting 2 mean the client is close — they're processing the actual decision, not deflecting. Slow down, narrow, answer with a specific number. Then the cross-sell is the legacy-ILP restructure — the FC's biggest case-size lever. Always document both scenarios in writing before recommending surrender.

This is the longest module in the track. Part 1 covers the 5 objections. Part 2 covers the APA→PWV restructure sequence. Estimated read + drill: ~65 minutes.

# Part 1 — Top 5 Objections (the Conviction-Story Heart)

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Every objection is a buying signal. The discipline is to slow down, narrow vague objections to specifics, and answer with a specific number rather than a reassurance. The FC who memorises these five and responds without hesitation closes Meeting 2.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to recite all 5 conviction stories from memory, narrow vague objections with a clarifying question, deploy specific numbers (not vague phrases), and avoid the common Meeting 2 collapse.

---

## Objection 1 — "I need to think about it"

**The frame:** This is a vague stall. Narrow it before answering.

**The script:**

> *"Of course. To help you think clearly, what specific aspect would you like to consider? Is it the amount, the timing, or the product itself?"*

**The conviction:**
- Vague objections collapse when narrowed
- Three options surface the actual concern (usually one of the three)
- "Think about it" without a specific concern = stalling, not consideration

**Sources:** Video 30

---

## Objection 2 — "I want to discuss with my spouse"

**The frame:** Don't agree and lose momentum. Offer to meet both.

**The script:**

> *"That's important. Would it help if I met with both of you together? I can address any questions directly. When can you both be free?"*

**The conviction:**
- Single-spouse meetings to "go discuss" rarely return
- Dual-spouse meeting = same content delivered once, both sides aligned
- Offering to meet both signals confidence and respects the household decision-maker

**Sources:** Video 30 — and this is why Phase 1 of Day 2 ("Is there anyone else who's involved in financial decisions?") matters at the meeting start

---

## Objection 3 — "The market is too volatile right now"

**The frame:** Volatility is the REASON for dollar-cost averaging, not the reason against.

**The script:**

> *"That's exactly why dollar cost averaging over 5 years works — you're buying at different price points across the cycle. Plus the death benefit floor means your family receives at least the total premiums you've paid in, regardless of where the market goes."*

**The conviction:**
- 5-year DCA = buy at different price points = average down the entry
- Capital floor on death = catastrophic risk capped
- Market timing is impossible; structural protection is built-in

**Numbers:**
- 5 years of premium contributions at quarterly bid pricing [PS p.3, Section 4.1]
- Death benefit floor: total regular premium paid + total top-up premium - total withdrawals [PS p.1, Section 3.1]

**Sources:** Video 30, Video 31

---

## Objection 4 — "I prefer fixed deposits — they're guaranteed"

**The frame:** PWV's capital floor on death is also "guaranteed." The yield differential is the real story.

**The script:**

> *"Your capital is also guaranteed here on death — your family receives at least what you invested. The difference is your money works at 6% instead of 2.5%. Over 20 years, that's around $300K in lost compounding.*
>
> *And FDs roll at the prevailing rate — when SIBOR drops, your income drops. With PWV your dividend frequency is quarterly and your underlying fund yield has been more stable than FD rates."*

**The conviction:**
- Capital floor on death is contractual (re-emphasise)
- Yield differential = $7,000/year on $200K capital = ~$300K over 20 years (Video 30)
- FD reset risk — when SIBOR drops, income drops
- PWV stability historically better than FD rate fluctuation

**Numbers:**
- FD vs PWV: 2.5% vs 6% conservative on $200K = **$7,000 annual differential**
- Over 20 years compounding: **~$300K delta** [Video 30]

**Sources:** Video 30, [PS p.1, Section 3.1]

---

## Objection 5 — "I'll do it next year"

**The frame:** The cost of waiting is quantifiable. Turn the delay into a number.

**The script:**

> *"Every year you wait, the cost of the same outcome increases. You're 52 today. If we start now, your $144K OA excess at 55 lines up perfectly to fund year 4. Wait a year, you're 53 starting — same plan, same outcome, but you've burned one year of compounding.*
>
> *On a 5-year staggered plan, that one-year delay reduces your year-21 policy value by roughly $60K. Let me show you the delay cost analysis."*

**The conviction:**
- Delay cost is real and computable
- Funding mechanism (CPF OA excess at 55) requires timing alignment
- 1-year delay on 5-year staggered plan = ~$60K loss at year 21

**Numbers:**
- 1-year delay impact on year-21 policy value: **~$60K loss** [Video 30]
- This compounds — 2-year delay ≈ $120K-$130K loss
- Funding mechanism timing reference: Day 1 Part 2 Panel 7 — CPF OA excess unlocks at 55

**Sources:** Video 30, Video 4 (PWV Drawing Concept — for funding mechanism timing)

---

## The single line that handles 80% of pushback

When a prospect drifts back to "let me consider":

> *"You don't need new money — the plan funds itself from resources you already have. The question isn't whether to do this — it's whether to start now while the funding mechanism aligns with your CPF OA timing, or wait and lose a year of compounding. Every objection so far is solvable. Which is the one that's actually stopping you?"*

This narrows the conversation to the real concern. Ask. Wait.

# Part 2 — Cross-Sell: APA→PWV Legacy ILP Restructuring Sequence

> **The one idea for Part 2:** A pre-retiree with three under-performing legacy policies represents $150K-$300K of redeployable capital sitting in the wrong vehicle. The capital is INVISIBLE to the client until the FC pulls it together via SingPass and quantifies true yield. The discipline: never recommend surrender without writing the comparison both ways. The FC who handles this cleanly earns trust that compounds — the client refers spouse, sibling, colleague.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to: deploy the 5-step restructure sequence, recognise when to surrender vs hold, document the trade-off in writing, and redeploy freed-up cashflow into PWV.

---

## The 5-step restructuring sequence

Run in order. Skip any step and the recommendation lacks credibility.

### Step 1 — Audit (pull the legacy ILP via SingPass)

Get from SingPass:
- Total premiums paid (all years)
- Current fund value
- Current fund holdings
- Ongoing fees and charges
- Any guaranteed bonuses left to vest
- Surrender charge schedule

The script:

> *"Your Pro Achiever ILP — let's audit it. You've paid in $X over Y years. Current fund value is $Z. That's a true yield of [%]. Show me what the original projection said versus reality."*

### Step 2 — Quantify true yield

Total premiums paid → current value over years held = annualised IRR.

Real-meeting yield findings (from Video 19):

| Policy | True yield |
|---|---|
| **Pro Achiever ILP** ($67K paid over 7 years → $74K) | ~1.4% IRR |
| **NTUC whole life** (long-held) | 1.07% |
| **GE Living Assurance** ($48K cash vs $50K death after 29 years) | Protection essentially gone |
| **Prudential** | Cost of insurance escalation $11/$1K at 55 → $67/$1K at 69 |

These are real client cases. Use the same pattern.

### Step 3 — Compare PWV alternative

Same capital deployed into PWV at:
- 6% conservative dividend
- Capital floor on death
- Full liquidity from year 8

The math comparison:

```
Hold scenario (continue Pro Achiever):
  $4,800/year × next 6 years = $28,800 more in
  Projected fund value at age 65: ~1.5-2% IRR continues
  
Restructure scenario (surrender + redeploy into PWV):
  Surrender charge today (calculate)
  Net proceeds → into PWV
  Plus the $4,800/year freed up → into PWV top-ups
  Projected at 6% dividend with capital floor
```

### Step 4 — Document the trade-off

The discipline:

> *"Restructuring isn't always the answer. Sometimes the surrender charge plus forfeited bonuses make it better to hold. Let me run both scenarios so you can see clearly."*

Always document BOTH outcomes:

| Scenario | Cost today | 10-year outcome | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Hold | S$0 | Project from existing IRR | Forfeit funding flexibility |
| Surrender + redeploy | Surrender charge | Project at 6% dividend + capital floor | Free up cashflow, reset capital |

Write it. Sign it. Have the client sign it. **Never recommend surrender without this paper trail.**

The script:

> *"I'm not going to recommend surrender unless the math is clearly better after fees. And I'll document both scenarios in writing for your file."*

### Step 5 — Redeploy + sequence

Surrender proceeds + freed-up monthly premiums become the funding source for the new PWV.

> *"By restructuring these three policies, you'll free up about $7,000 per year in premiums. That funds part of the new plan without any new money out of pocket."*

The plan funds itself.

---

## The cash flow transformation (Video 38)

The reframe of "I can't afford new premium":

```
Before restructure:
  $4,800/year — Pro Achiever (yielding 1.4%)
+ $2,400/year — NTUC whole life (yielding 1.07%)
+ $1,800/year — GE Living Assurance (death benefit gone)
= $9,000/year going into low-return legacy plans

After restructure (surrender + redeploy):
  $X surrender proceeds → PWV top-up
+ $9,000/year freed cashflow → PWV regular premium
+ CPF OA excess at 55 → PWV year-4 funding
+ Maturing endowment → PWV year-5 funding
= Funded $40K-$50K/year PWV with no new money out of pocket
```

This is the funding mechanism that makes PWV "self-funding."

## When NOT to restructure

The compliance discipline. Don't restructure if:

1. **Surrender charge + lost benefits > redeployment gain** — math says hold
2. **Legacy policy has guaranteed bonuses approaching vesting** — wait for vesting first
3. **Existing rider provides protection that PWV doesn't replace** — keep the rider, layer PWV on
4. **Client is in a claim period** (CI claim pending, pre-existing condition disclosed) — fresh underwriting may decline
5. **Surrender would lock in a loss in a market downturn** — wait for market recovery

Respect any case where the math says hold. The FC who acknowledges this earns more trust than the FC who always recommends surrender.

## Cross-sell discipline rules

1. **Always pull SingPass IN the meeting** (Phase 3 of Day 2). Don't accept "I'll send later."
2. **Document surrender-vs-hold in writing** before recommending surrender.
3. **Respect the math** — sometimes hold is the right answer.
4. **Frame as "redeployment" not "cancellation"** — the client's mental frame matters.
5. **Use freed cashflow as the funding mechanism** — Day 1 Part 2 Panel 7 carries through.
