# Day 5 — Compliance + Close: Disclosures, Direct Close, Self-Claim Handoff

> **Today's frame:** Three honest disclosures (yearly-renewable, premium not guaranteed, 50%-at-75 + age-80 termination) plus the standard exclusion list. Then close on the four-option question. SQS paperwork is the lightest in the AIA stack — SingPass, no medical, no FHR for Plans 1-3. Post-sale: turn every claim into a referral.

This is the final module. Part 1 covers compliance + disclosures. Part 2 covers the direct close, SQS workflow, AIA+ self-claim handoff, post-sale rhythm. Estimated read + drill: ~50 minutes.

# Part 1 — Compliance + Risk Disclosures

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Three honest disclosures (yearly-renewable not guaranteed, price can change, cover steps down at 75 and ends at 80) + the standard exclusion list. Solitaire PA's compliance burden is the same as any RNF-regulated H&S product despite the lighter paperwork.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to deliver the disclosures cleanly, recite the major exclusions, distinguish covered Policy Extensions from exclusions, and pass an RNF audit.

---

## The 3 honest disclosures

### Disclosure 1 — Yearly-renewable, NOT guaranteed renewable

> *"This is a short-term accident and health policy. It's renewable yearly but **not guaranteed renewable**. AIA can decline renewal or change premiums with at least 31 days' written notice. The insurer can also give 30 days' notice to terminate."* [PS p.13]

The script:

> *"It's not a long-term policy like whole life. It renews every year. AIA can re-price the portfolio with 31 days' notice."*

### Disclosure 2 — 50% reduction at 75 + termination at 80

> *"From your 75th birthday renewal date, the basic-benefit insured amounts reduce by 50%. Optional benefits stop entirely. The policy itself terminates at age 80."* [PS p.3, p.14]

This is critical for older prospects:

> *"Get the cover now while you're young — it's at full strength until 75. After 75, basic benefits halve. After 80, gone."*

### Disclosure 3 — Premium not guaranteed

> *"Premium not guaranteed — AIA reserves the right to adjust at renewal with at least 31 days' written notice."* [PS p.13]

## Pre-existing conditions — guaranteed issue ≠ covered for claims

This is the most-misunderstood compliance point:

> *"Solitaire PA is guaranteed-issue — clients with pre-existing conditions (even cancer) can buy the policy."* [Video 8]

BUT:

> *"The policy explicitly excludes claims arising from pre-existing conditions."* [PS p.13]

**Don't conflate the two when speaking to clients.** Make this distinction crystal clear.

## The full RNF-clean closing block

Deliver as one block right before signature:

> *"Before we move forward, four things to make sure I've covered:*
>
> *One — this is a yearly-renewable accident and health policy. Not guaranteed renewable. AIA can re-price with 31 days' notice.*
>
> *Two — basic benefits reduce by 50% from your 75th birthday renewal. Optional benefits stop at 75. Policy terminates at 80.*
>
> *Three — pre-existing conditions are excluded from claims, even though the policy is guaranteed-issue. Plus exclusions for illegal activity, intentional self-injury, alcohol/drug-related accidents, professional sports, racing other than on foot or swimming.*
>
> *Four — 14-day free-look from receipt. Cancel within that window, you get the premium back minus medical fees.*
>
> *Any of those concern you?"*

If "no" — RNF audit pass. Move to SQS submission.

If "yes" — slow down. Address it.

## The "anti-market" disclosure

> *"This plan isn't right for someone who has generous, lifetime, portable group PA from their employer with no caps. Or someone who'd rather self-insure outpatient costs from cash. For most people, neither of those is true."*

# Part 2 — Direct Close, SQS Workflow, AIA+ Self-Claim Handoff

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Close is one sentence ("Plan 1, 2, 3, or 4?"). SQS paperwork is the lightest in the AIA stack — SingPass + no medical + no FHR for Plans 1-3. Post-sale: every PA claim processed within days via PayNow becomes a referral-quality moment. PA is a service-relationship product, not a one-off.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver the close cleanly, run SQS without notes, demo the AIA+ self-claim app, and book the post-sale claim-handoff service.

---

## The close — direct, single-sentence

> *"So which plan would you prefer — Plan 1, 2, 3, or 4?"* [Video 3, Video 7]

Most prospects pick Plan 3 or 4 after the comparison framing.

If they ask for guidance:

> *"For your profile and lifestyle, Plan 3 or Plan 4. Plan 4 if budget allows — the 4×/7× value matters at the higher tier. Plan 3 if you want a lighter premium with most of the cover."*

## The light-hearted close pattern

If the prospect is wavering on price:

> *"$2 a day is still cheaper than your cai fan. You're already spending so much on so many things. Why not just spend $1 per day for this?"* [Video 4, Video 7]

> *"Plan 2 is just $1 per day. You're already spending so much on food, transport, shopping, and holidays. $30 a month is nothing."* [Video 7]

## The price-discipline rule

If a prospect is comparing on $1 differences:

> *"If $1 a year is the deciding factor, that competitor's plan is fine for you. I won't compete on $1. What I compete on is the AIA+ self-claim experience and the relationship of having someone — me — handle your claims fast."*

Don't compete on price. Compete on service.

## Pre-existing conditions handling

| Scenario | Action |
|---|---|
| Cancer (pre-existing) | Issue policy (guaranteed-issue) — but disclose claim exclusion clearly |
| Active medical condition | Issue policy — disclose claim exclusion |
| Recent A&E history | Issue policy — claim eligibility depends on whether condition is pre-existing |

The discipline: issuance ≠ claim eligibility. Make this crystal clear.

## The AIA+ self-claim demo (the differentiator at delivery)

Demo at point of sale. The 5-minute claim demo is the AIA differentiator:

1. Snap a photo of receipt
2. Submit via AIA+ app
3. AIA pays out via PayNow
4. Usually within a few working days

> *"These are real claim approvals from actual clients — TCM, chiropractor, $14.50, $156. Just send a receipt to me and AIA pays now to you, usually within a few working days."* [Video 10]

> *"It's easy to claim and easy to process."* [Video 10]

Show real claim screenshots if you have them. Concrete > abstract.

## Post-sale service rhythm

Solitaire PA is a service-relationship product. Set up the rhythm at delivery:

| Trigger | What to do |
|---|---|
| Day 7-14 (free-look window) | Check-in WhatsApp |
| Any minor accident (claim event) | Process the claim immediately via AIA+ — turn it into a relationship deepener |
| Annual renewal (~13 months) | Vitality status check, plan review, any life-event coverage updates |
| Major life event (marriage, kid, job change) | Re-engagement |
| Age 70 | Final entry-age window — ensure plan is locked in |
| Age 75 | Reduction reminder — flag the 50% basic benefit cut |

## The benchmark (what good looks like)

A senior FC running this discipline produces:

- **3-5 Solitaire PA cases per week** (always paired with HSGM)
- **70%+ first-appointment close rate** — highest in the AIA stack
- **~90% Plan 3 or Plan 4 attach rate** (after 4×/7× framing)
- **1-2 PA claims processed per week per book** (the relationship-deepener)
- **2+ adjacent sells per closed case within 90 days** (HSGM upgrade, kids' plans via Star Protector Plus, GPP layered)

That's the target.

## The "case is closed" definition

A Solitaire PA case is NOT closed at SQS submission. It's closed when:

1. ✅ SQS submitted
2. ✅ Policy issued
3. ✅ 14-day free-look passed without cancellation
4. ✅ First GIRO debit confirmed
5. ✅ AIA+ app installed by the prospect
6. ✅ At least one claim processed (proof of service moat)
7. ✅ At least one referral or family-member follow-up

If you stop at SQS and never run #5-7, you closed half a case.
