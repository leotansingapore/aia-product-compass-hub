# Day 2 — Run the Meeting: Fact-Find + 7-Phase Pitch Flow

> **Today's frame:** The fact-find for PA is short but not skip-able. Three confirmations, then the 7-phase pitch flow. Always sold paired with HSGM (basic-medical bundle), never standalone.

This is a merged module — Part 1 covers the 5-minute fact-find. Part 2 covers the 7-phase pitch flow. Estimated read + drill: ~50 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — The Fact-Find: 5 Minutes, 3 Confirmations

> **The one idea for Part 1:** The fact-find is shorter than for life or wealth, but skipping it costs the close. Three confirmations carry the rest of the meeting: outpatient gap exists, lifestyle/commute creates real probability of minor accident, occupation class and income are clean for the recommended plan.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: deploy the existing-coverage audit, classify the prospect by occupation (Class 1-2 vs 3-4), recognise income restrictions for Plan 4, and walk away with the data SQS needs.

---

## The 3 confirmations

### Confirmation 1 — Outpatient gap exists

Audit existing coverage:
- Hospital plan? (HSGM, Income Shield, Pru, GE, etc.)
- Life/CI plan? (GPP, PLP, term)
- Employer-provided group PA?

If hospital plan exists but no PA → confirmed gap. Move on.

If employer GPA exists → ask cap. Most are S$50K-S$200K and tied to job. Solitaire PA Plan 4 covers up to S$2.25M with portability.

### Confirmation 2 — Lifestyle/commute creates probability

> *"Do you play sports?"* / *"Do you like buffets? Oysters?"* / *"Anyone you know ever got dengue?"* [Video 3]

> *"You might also want to look out for clients who are on motorbikes, who are more prone to accidents, or let's just say they are perhaps more active in nature."* [Video 5]

Capture in fact-find:
- Occupation
- Daily commute mode (MRT, Grab, motorbike, PMD, walk)
- Weekend activities / sports
- Whether children are active
- Recent A&E or polyclinic visits in family

Any "yes" to lifestyle risk = stronger pitch for Plan 3 or 4.

### Confirmation 3 — Occupation class + income clean for recommended plan

| Class | Examples | Premium loading |
|---|---|---|
| Class 1 | Office worker, teacher, banker | Standard |
| Class 2 | Salesperson, light technical | Standard |
| Class 3 | Light manual, supervisor | ~75% loaded |
| Class 4 | Manual labour, drivers | ~75% loaded |

[Brochure p.3, PS p.2]

**Plan 4 has an income threshold** — prospects on S$2,000-S$3,000/month income are auto-capped at Plan 3 by the SQS system [Video 8]. Verify before quoting Plan 4 to a borderline-income prospect.

## The KK Hospital opener (use only if prospect doesn't have own story)

> *"My client bought a hospitalization plan. The agent told him everything is covered. His child got injured and needed stitches at KK Hospital. He assumed — it's a hospital plan, going to a hospital, should be covered, right? But it wasn't. Why? Because it was outpatient. That's the loophole."* [Video 3, Video 5]

Then:

> *"Hospital plans require either minimum 6 hours hospitalization, or a surgical procedure. Outpatient treatments don't qualify."* [Video 5]

If the prospect has their own story (recent denial, outpatient bill paid out of pocket), USE THAT. Their story is more powerful than yours.

## Fact-find prompts (deploy in any order)

- *"What's your occupation?"*
- *"Daily commute?"*
- *"Weekend activities?"*
- *"Kids active in sports?"*
- *"Existing employer GPA cover? What's the cap?"*
- *"Any recent A&E or polyclinic visits in the family?"*
- *"Mr/Ms Prospect, did you know that even with a hospital plan, you're not covered for minor accidents?"* [Video 9]

## What a complete Solitaire PA fact-find looks like

5 minutes max. You should walk away with:

- ✅ Existing coverage audit (hospital plan + life/CI + employer GPA)
- ✅ Occupation + class
- ✅ Lifestyle / commute / activity risk
- ✅ Income (for Plan 4 qualification)
- ✅ Family unit (kids? Plan 2+ unlocks Star Protector Plus 10% discount)
- ✅ Recent accident or A&E story (theirs or family's — use as opener)

If any are missing, the close fumbles. Go back and ask.

## Reference numbers

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Plan 4 income threshold | Plans 1-3 no FHR; Plan 4 requires FHR if income insufficient [Video 8] |
| Issue age | 16-70 last birthday [PS p.2] |
| Class 1-2 vs Class 3-4 loading | ~75% on Plan 4 (S$855.69 vs S$1,512.75) |

# Part 2 — The 7-Phase Pitch Flow

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Open with the loophole story (theirs or KK), define accident, run the 3 coverage tiers (relatable → serious → extreme), draw the partial-disability gap, frame 4×/7× price-vs-coverage, anchor on daily cost, demo AIA+ app, close on "Plan 1, 2, 3, or 4?". 30 minutes total when bundled with HSGM.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver all 7 phases in sequence, demo the AIA+ self-claim app live, run the price/coverage 4×/7× framing without notes, and close on the four-option choice.

---

## The 7 phases

```
Phase 1     Phase 2          Phase 3              Phase 4         Phase 5            Phase 6           Phase 7
Open    →   Define +     →   Partial         →   Plan 1 vs 4 →   Daily cost     →   AIA+ app      →   "1/2/3/4?"
(KK)        3 tiers          gap diagram          (4×/7×)         anchor             demo
```

| Phase | Time | Micro-commit |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Open with loophole story | 3 min | Prospect feels the gap |
| 2. Define + 3 coverage tiers | 4 min | "OK, this is broader than I thought" |
| 3. Partial-disability gap diagram | 4 min | "I see the gap" |
| 4. Plan 1 vs Plan 4 (4×/7×) | 4 min | "More worth at higher tier" |
| 5. Daily cost anchor | 3 min | "It's cheaper than my kopi" |
| 6. AIA+ self-claim demo | 4 min | "Easy to claim" |
| 7. "Plan 1/2/3/4?" close | 2 min | Plan picked |
| **Total** | **~24 min** | **Plan + bundle locked** |

## Phase 1 — Open with loophole story

If prospect has own story (recent A&E denial, outpatient bill) — use theirs.

Otherwise, the KK Hospital story:

> *"My client bought a hospitalization plan. The agent told him everything is covered. His child got injured and needed stitches at KK Hospital. He assumed — it's a hospital plan, going to a hospital, should be covered, right? But it wasn't. Why? Because it was outpatient. That's the loophole."* [Video 3, Video 5]

Then:

> *"Hospital plans require either minimum 6 hours hospitalization, or a surgical procedure. Outpatient treatments don't qualify."* [Video 5]

## Phase 2 — Define accident + 3 coverage tiers

The definition:

> *"Accident = unexpected and unintentional. NOT self-inflicted... NOT minor illnesses (cough, fever don't count)."* [Video 1, Video 6]

Then walk the 3 coverage tiers:

### Tier 1 — Day-to-day (high frequency)

> *"Amateur sports, recreational sports, food poisoning, dengue fever... You play football, sprain your ankle. You go to a buffet, eat some oysters, lao sai. Insect bites — got bitten by a wasp at Botanic Gardens, hospital bills all covered."* [Video 1, Video 6]

### Tier 2 — Moderate (medium frequency)

> *"Vehicle accidents, motorcycle bumps, falls down stairs, food contamination at restaurants. Things that happen but you don't expect."*

### Tier 3 — Extreme (low frequency)

> *"Strike, riot, civil commotion, terrorism, natural disasters, hijack, murder, assault. Coverage is global, 24/7."* [PS p.12]

> *"It's a global coverage. JB also can."* [Video 6]

## Phase 3 — Partial-disability gap diagram

This is Day 1 Part 2's signature concept. Draw it. The TPD column vs Schedule of Indemnity column.

Live computation:

> *"Plan 4, Benefit 4 insured amount is S$750,000. Lose one finger phalanx — 5% payout. That's S$37,500 to you. Lose sight in one eye — 100% payout. That's S$750,000."*

Pause. Let it land.

## Phase 4 — Plan 1 vs Plan 4 (4×/7× framing)

The verbatim:

> *"Plan 4 is four times more expensive than Plan 1, but the coverage is seven times more."* [Video 4]

The math:

| | Plan 1 | Plan 4 | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual basic premium (Class 1-2, ages 16-65, 9% GST) | S$224.11 | S$855.69 | ~3.8× |
| Accidental Death | S$100,000 | S$750,000 | 7.5× |
| Major Dismemberment | S$300,000 | S$2,250,000 | 7.5× |
| Accident Medical Reimbursement (per accident) | S$2,000 | S$5,000 | 2.5× |

[Brochure p.2-3, PS p.1]

The framing:

> *"For 4× the price, you're getting 7× the value. It's more worth it."*

## Phase 5 — Daily cost anchor

> *"$2 a day is still cheaper than your cai fan. You're already spending so much on so many things. Why not just spend $1 per day for this?"* [Video 4, Video 7]

> *"Plan 2 is just $1 per day. You're already spending so much on food, transport, shopping, and holidays. $30 a month is nothing."* [Video 7]

The daily-cost ladder:

| Plan | Daily | Comparison |
|---|---|---|
| Plan 1 | S$0.62 | Less than ½ MRT fare |
| Plan 2 | S$1.02 | Cheaper than kopi-O |
| Plan 3 | S$1.63 | Cheaper than bubble tea |
| Plan 4 | S$2.34 | Cheaper than cai fan |

## Phase 6 — AIA+ self-claim demo (the differentiator)

This is the AIA service moat. Demo live if possible:

> *"Mr Prospect, let me show you this. The AIA Plus app has a self-claiming feature. The claim process only takes five minutes."* [Video 4]

Show the actual process:
1. Snap a photo of the receipt
2. Submit through AIA+ app
3. AIA pays out via PayNow
4. Usually within a few working days

If possible, show **real claim screenshots** from your own book — claim amounts ranging S$14.50 to S$156. Concrete > abstract.

> *"Through AIA, very easy. The AIA+ app has a self-claiming feature — the claim process takes about five minutes. You screenshot or photograph the receipt and send it. AIA pays you directly via PayNow, usually within a few working days. Other companies — you call someone, fill in forms, go down in person. Through us, it's seamless."* [Video 4, Video 10]

## Phase 7 — The "Plan 1, 2, 3, or 4?" close

Direct question. Don't elaborate:

> *"So which plan would you prefer — Plan 1, 2, 3, or 4?"* [Video 3, Video 7]

Most prospects pick Plan 3 or 4 after the comparison framing. If they ask for guidance:

> *"For your profile and lifestyle, Plan 3 or Plan 4. Plan 4 if budget allows — the 4×/7× value matters at the higher tier."*

## The price-discipline rule

If a prospect is comparing on $1 differences with a competitor, refer them to that competitor:

> *"If $1 a year is the deciding factor, that competitor's plan is fine for you. I won't compete on $1. What I compete on is the AIA+ self-claim experience and the relationship of having someone — me — handle your claims fast."*

## The pacing rule

If you're approaching the 30-minute mark and still in Phase 5, you're losing them. Compress to "Plan 1, 2, 3, or 4?" close, set up SQS for next session.
