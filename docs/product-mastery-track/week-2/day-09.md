# Day 4 — Defence + Expansion: PLP-vs-Everything + Cross-sell

> **Today's frame:** PLP earns its premium through optionality — pause, withdraw, dial down, outlive the term. The 5 objections all collapse into one core defense. Don't fight on price. Then layer correctly: PLP + GPP for booster years, PLP + UCC for estate floor with separate CI lever, PLP + APA for accumulation.

This is the longest module. Part 1 covers the 5 objections (PLP-vs-GPP is the tightest in the curriculum). Part 2 covers the 4 cross-sell stacks. Estimated read + drill: ~65 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — Top 5 Objections (the Conviction-Story Heart)

> **The one idea for Part 1:** PLP and GPP look similar from outside — both whole-life, both AIA. The actual difference: GPP is fixed-term-pay you must finish; PLP is a permanent asset with flexible cash engine. The five objections all collapse into one core defense — PLP earns its premium through optionality.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to recite all 5 conviction stories from memory, deploy the SFT-renewal-cost number ($21K/year for $500K cover age 65→95), and crush the "buy term and invest the rest" comparison with the post-65 reality check.

---

## Objection 1 — "PLP is more expensive than GPP — why pay more for the same death cover?"

**The frame:** GPP and PLP target different needs. GPP is fixed-term whole-life with multipliers/booster. PLP is permanent + flexible.

**The script:**

> *"GPP and PLP target different needs. GPP is a fixed-term whole-life with multipliers and a booster — high cover for 20-25 years, lower cover for life. PLP is a permanent floor that doesn't drop. With GPP you must pay the full term to keep cover; if you stop within the first 20 years, you lose everything. With PLP you can stop after 10 years and the policy continues to grow and protect. Different premium, different mechanic — PLP earns its premium through flexibility and the cash value engine."* [Video 5, Video 7]

The clinching line:

> *"The downside of GPP is that you can't pause the plan. For GPP you have to pay all the way for 25 years. The moment you stop, the plan stops. Whereas for PLP, you can stop paying — the policy still continues, the coverage is still there."* [Video 5]

**Numbers (sample comparison, $200/month × 20 yr pay, age 20, $200K cover):**

| | GPP | PLP |
|---|---|---|
| Total in | ~$48,000 | ~$48,000 |
| Total return at age 100 | ~$300,000–400,000 | ~$3,400,000 [Video 5, 7] |

**The conviction:** GPP buys booster cover; PLP buys a permanent asset.

---

## Objection 2 — "I don't need lifelong coverage"

**The frame:** Lifelong is the point of the floor. After 65, term cover gets prohibitively expensive.

**The script:**

> *"Lifelong is the point of the floor. GPP's high-cover period ends — its booster steps down, and many term plans die at 75. After 65, if you still want $500,000 of coverage, the SFT cost is roughly $21,000/year. With PLP, after 65 the insurance is essentially free because the sum-at-risk has gone to zero on Max. So 'lifelong' isn't an extra you're buying — it's the cost you avoid later."* [Video 9]

> *"Until age 100. If you wanted to extend an SFT from 65 to 95, $500,000 of cover, the pricing is about $21,000 per year. With PLP you don't pay that — it's already built in."* [Video 9]

**The conviction:** Lifelong is a cost you avoid, not an extra you buy.

---

## Objection 3 — "Buy term and invest the rest" (BTID)

**The frame:** APA + Term gives ~10% higher pure returns but loses lifelong cover. The post-65 SFT cost eats the 10% advantage.

**The script:**

> *"If you go pure investment plus term — APA at $900 plus SFT at $100 — your returns are about 10% higher than PLP at $1,000. But the SFT only goes to 75. After 75, you have nothing. PLP goes to 100. The 10% extra return on APA gets eaten by the term renewal cost after 65. So at the same premium you can get 10% more growth and lose lifelong cover, or you can get a slightly lower return with the floor permanently locked in. It's a trade, not a winner."* [Video 9]

**The discipline:**

> *"When you sell insurance, you need to learn both sides. Learn how to attack and learn how to defend. PLP's defense is the lifelong cover — APA's pure-investment attack is the higher pure return. Match the plan to the client."* [Video 9]

**Numbers (sample, $1,000/month $500K cover age 20):**

| Combination | Total at age 100 |
|---|---|
| PLP alone | ~$26M [Video 9] |
| APA pure (no term) | ~$32M [Video 9] |
| APA $900 + SFT $100 (term to 75) | ~$29M [Video 9] |

After incorporating post-65 SFT renewal cost (~$21K/yr to extend to 95), the APA+SFT advantage closes substantially.

**The conviction:** The 10% return advantage gets eaten by post-65 term renewal — and you lose lifelong cover.

---

## Objection 4 — "Inflation will erode the cover"

**The frame:** Two answers — milestone increase + Max-plan policy value tracking.

**The script:**

> *"Two answers. One: the milestone increase option lets you raise the insured amount up to S$200,000 across two exercises without medical underwriting on each major life event — marriage, birth of child, adoption. Two: the policy value compounds over decades, so on the Max plan the death benefit is the higher of insured amount or policy value — the policy value tracks growth and outpaces inflation in long horizons."* [Brochure p.1, PS p.2]

**Numbers:**
- Milestone: up to S$200,000 aggregate, lower of S$100,000 or 50% per exercise, before age 60 [PS p.2, Brochure p.1]
- Max plan: death benefit = higher of Insured Amount or Policy Value (so cover automatically grows with the policy value) [PS p.4]

**The conviction:** PLP has a built-in inflation hedge via Milestone + Max-plan growth tracking.

---

## Objection 5 — "Competitor whole-life is cheaper"

**The frame:** Competitor whole-life is usually par (3-4% bonus rate). PLP's investment engine is Mercer-guided and fully invested by Year 5 — long-term illustrated return is materially higher.

**The script:**

> *"The price is one number. The structure is what matters. Competitor whole-life is usually par — cash value compounds at a guaranteed plus non-guaranteed bonus rate that historically tracks 3-4%. PLP's investment engine is Mercer-guided and fully invested by Year 5 — the long-term illustrated return is materially higher.*
>
> *Plus PLP is the only one that lets you pause without losing cover, take partial withdrawals, and dial down coverage in retirement to maximise returns. You're not paying for the same product."* [Video 10, Video 11, Brochure p.4]

**Numbers / credentials:**
- Mercer credentials: "US$12.9 trillion in assets under advisement as at 30 June 2019" [Brochure p.4]
- Mercer track record: "over 40 years of providing investment advice" [Brochure p.4]
- 3 Mercer-guided portfolios: Pro Adventurous / Pro Balanced / Pro Cautious

**The conviction:** Competitor whole-life is a different product — cheaper because it does less.

---

## The single line that handles 80% of pushback

When a prospect drifts back to "let me just stick with cheaper":

> *"You're right that cheaper is cheaper. The question is what cheaper buys. With PLP, you're paying for a permanent asset that you can pause, withdraw from, dial down, and that grows with you. With cheaper alternatives, you're paying for a cheaper version of one feature. Match the price to what you actually need."*

# Part 2 — Cross-sell + Bundling: 4 Stacks

> **The one idea for Part 2:** PLP rarely sells alone. Its highest-leverage placement is as the permanent floor underneath a GPP + UCC stack — covering booster years with GPP, lifelong CI tail with UCC, and permanent death/legacy floor with PLP. For families: PLP + Child Critical Cover rider with conversion privilege. For HNW: PLP + Payor Benefit + Milestone Option.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deploy the 4 stack patterns per prospect type, recommend the rider strategy correctly (strip LCC, sell UCC separately for CI), recognise when PLP is the wrong product, and book the next cross-sell appointment.

---

## Stack 1 — Floor + Booster (PLP + GPP)

The most common pairing for prospects with peak-liability years (mortgage + dependants).

```
Years 30-55 (peak liability)        Years 55-100 (legacy / retirement)
═══════════════════════════════     ═══════════════════════════════
GPP booster: high cover             GPP booster: stepped down
PLP base:    permanent floor        PLP:        still running, value compounding

Combined cover:
Death + Major CI = GPP + PLP        Death + Major CI = PLP only (GPP at lower base)
Liquidity        = both              Liquidity        = PLP partial withdrawal
```

The narrative: GPP handles the worst-case during peak-liability years; PLP keeps running afterward for legacy/retirement.

## Stack 2 — Estate Floor (PLP + UCC for separate CI)

The default for clients who want CI cover but don't want LCC's higher charges loaded onto PLP.

The script (from Video 12):

> *"If someone really wants CI coverage, I will just sell them a UCC rider, I will not add on into the PLP. PLP is really just a starter plan for anyone looking for just a convenient way to get coverage and returns."* [Video 12]

The reasoning:
- LCC adds significant insurance charges to PLP (samples in Day 3 cheat sheet)
- UCC sold separately is more efficient — multi-claim CI without dragging down PLP's investment returns
- Clean architecture: PLP for protection floor + investment, UCC for multi-claim CI

> *"PLP becomes an all-in-one policy. A lot of my clients want their plans to have a bit of critical illness coverage and they also want the early CI coverage as well."* [Video 1]

(Video 1 makes the case for LCC inclusion. Video 12 argues for stripping. Match to prospect — load-LCC for prospects who want one plan, strip-LCC for cost-efficient prospects.)

## Stack 3 — Accumulation Layer (PLP + APA)

For prospects who already have solid protection cover and want pure accumulation.

The script (from Video 14):

> *"If a person wants coverage, my default stance is to sell APA plus GPP because there's gonna be less trouble along the way. But if you really want to defend yourself, PLP can stand on its own — it has the SFT embedded inside, and after 65, the insurance is essentially free."* [Video 9]

The mechanic:
- PLP for permanent protection + base accumulation (Mercer-guided)
- APA for pure investment growth (no insurance drag)
- Together: ~$7M PLP + APA compound over 60 years

**Numbers (Video 8 sample, $400/month total split):**

| Stack | Total at age 100 |
|---|---|
| PLP alone at $400/month | ~$7,000,000 |
| APA $200 + GPP $200 | ~$4.7M ($4.4M APA + $324K GPP cash value) |

PLP wins long-run by ~$2.3M but the GPP+APA combo gives a guaranteed-cash component.

## Stack 4 — Family / Multi-Generation (PLP + Child Critical Cover + Payor Benefit)

The legacy stack for parents.

### Child Critical Cover rider

> *"Buying this plan for your little one? Plan for guaranteed coverage. Protect your child against 25 medical conditions including ADHD, Autism & Dyslexia. Future premiums will be waived up to his/her 25th birthday upon diagnosis of any covered condition."* [Brochure p.5]

| Mechanic | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conditions covered | 25 child medical conditions including ADHD, Autism, Dyslexia |
| Coverage to | Age 21 |
| Conversion | Between 18th and 21st birthday with **NO underwriting** to whole-life or endowment |
| Policy size | S$10K – S$150K |
| Per-life limit | S$150K |
| Aggregated CI limit across AIA | S$3M |

[Video 15, Brochure p.5]

The conversion privilege is the sticky cross-sell — the child is locked in as a future client at 21.

### Payor Benefit Comprehensive Special / Early CPP

| Mechanic | Detail |
|---|---|
| Trigger (parent) | Diagnosis of 72 major OR 149 multi-stage CIs |
| Effect | Future premiums waived to child's **25th birthday** |

[Brochure p.5]

For a parent paying for a child's PLP, this is essentially mandatory.

## When NOT to load LCC into PLP

If the prospect:
- Already has GPP with CI rider
- Has UCC or plans to buy UCC
- Wants maximum investment growth (LCC charges drag the return)
- Is older (LCC charges scale with age — see Day 3 cheat sheet, age 80 LCC charge = $129.66 per $1,000 PIA)

— strip LCC. Sell UCC separately.

## Cross-sell discipline rules

1. **Match the stack to the prospect.** Floor+Booster for peak-liability families, Estate Floor for CI-conscious clients, Accumulation Layer for already-protected prospects, Family Stack for parents.
2. **Default-strip LCC if UCC is in play.** Don't double-pay for CI cover.
3. **Always plant the Child Critical Cover seed at delivery** for parents — the conversion privilege is the multi-generational cross-sell.
4. **Use Milestone Option** for any prospect under 60 — built-in inflation hedge.
5. **Don't try to bundle Stack 1 and Stack 3 in one appointment.** Two appointments minimum for a multi-product portfolio.
