# Day 5 — Compliance + Close: ILP Disclosures, Budget-Choice Close, Retention

> **Today's frame:** Three sentences verbatim every meeting (the ILP disclosures). Then close on two budget options. Match the portfolio to the prospect's risk profile (never above). Defend retention at delivery — a competing FC six months later can show the prospect the Year-1 charges still visible and convince them they bought a bad plan. The defense begins at delivery, not at the next review.

This is the final module. Part 1 covers ILP compliance + risk disclosures. Part 2 covers the budget-choice close, portfolio matching, retention defense. Estimated read + drill: ~50 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — ILP Compliance + Risk Disclosures

> **The one idea for Part 1:** PLP is an ILP — three pillar disclosures verbatim every meeting. Premiums not guaranteed. Policy values not guaranteed. Past performance not indicative. The 14-day free-look + 75/50/0% surrender schedule + 5-year reinstatement + suicide-year-1 limit complete the compliance picture.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to deliver all 3 ILP pillars cleanly, deploy the 14-day free-look + reinstatement disclosures, and pass an RNF sample audit.

---

## The 3 ILP pillar disclosures (verbatim, every meeting)

### Pillar 1 — Premiums and policy values are NOT guaranteed

> *"Investments in this plan are subject to investment risks including the possible loss of the principal amount invested. The performance of the ILP sub-fund(s) is not guaranteed and the value of the units in the ILP sub-fund(s) and the income accruing to the units, if any, may fall or rise. Past performance is not necessarily indicative of the future performance of the ILP sub-fund(s)."* [Brochure p.9, PS p.11]

The plain-English version:

> *"Premiums are NOT guaranteed. Policy values are NOT guaranteed. Fund prices can go up and down — there is a possibility of loss."* [Video 2]

### Pillar 2 — 14-day free-look period

> *"After purchasing a life insurance policy, you have a 14-day free-look period starting from the day you receive your policy documents to review the documents carefully. During this time, if you choose to cancel your policy, the insurer will refund you the premiums paid without interest, after adjustments for any change in market value of the underlying assets and any medical fees."* [PS p.8]

Trigger windows:
- Electronic policy: from SMS/email notification date
- Postal: 7 days from posting
- Hand-delivered: 7 days from delivery to courier/representative

[PS p.8]

### Pillar 3 — Long-term commitment + early surrender penalty

> *"As buying a life insurance policy is a long-term commitment, an early termination of the policy usually involves high costs and the surrender value, if any, that is payable to you may be zero or less than the total premiums paid."* [Brochure p.9, PS p.11-12]

Surrender schedule:
- Year 1: 75% charge
- Year 2: 50%
- Year 3+: 0%

[PS p.4]

## Other compliance points

### Charge variation notice

> *"The fees and charges set out herein are not guaranteed. We reserve the right upon prior notification of not less than 1 month in writing to you, to vary the fees and charges set out in the policy."* [PS p.12]

### Limitation of Benefit (suicide / misrepresentation / fraud)

| Trigger | Effect |
|---|---|
| Suicide within 1 year of issue/reinstatement | Liability limited to 100% of policy value |
| Material misrepresentation/concealment within 2 years | Liability limited to 100% of policy value |
| Fraud at any time | Liability limited to 100% of policy value |

[PS p.8]

### Reinstatement window

> *"Reinstatement: within 5 years from lapse date, subject to T&Cs; no back-payment of premiums required."* [PS p.8]

This is a useful safety net — if the prospect lapses due to financial stress, they have 5 years to reinstate without paying back-premiums.

### General exclusions

| Category | Exclusion |
|---|---|
| Self-injury / suicide | First policy year |
| Violation of law | Always |
| War / military service | Always |
| Aerial activities | Excluded EXCEPT commercial passenger flights and RSAF |

[PS p.20]

### CI exclusions (TCA + LCC)

| Category | Exclusion |
|---|---|
| Pre-existing conditions | Hard exclusion |
| HIV/AIDS | Excluded for fulminant hepatitis or major cancer |

[PS p.23-24]

### SDIC protection

> *"Protected up to specified limits by SDIC."* [Brochure p.9]

## The full RNF-clean closing block

Deliver as one block right before signature:

> *"Before we authenticate, four things to make sure I've covered:*
>
> *One — premiums are NOT guaranteed. Policy values are NOT guaranteed. Past performance is NOT indicative of future performance.*
>
> *Two — 14-day free-look from receipt of policy documents. During free-look you can cancel; AIA refunds premiums minus any adjustment for fund value movement.*
>
> *Three — early termination involves high costs. Year 1 surrender charge is 75%. Year 2 is 50%. Year 3 onwards no charge.*
>
> *Four — fees and charges aren't guaranteed. AIA reserves the right to vary with at least 1 month's written notice.*
>
> *Any of those four concern you?"*

If "no" — RNF audit pass. Move to portfolio selection.

If "yes" — slow down. Address it. Better 15 more minutes than a complaint.

## The "anti-market" disclosure (the trust move)

> *"This plan isn't right for everyone. The anti-market for PLP is people who:*
>
> *- Want pure investment only (APA is the right plan)*
> *- Are totally risk-averse to non-guaranteed returns*
> *- Can't commit at least 10 years to the plan*
> *- Need short-term access to funds*
>
> *If any of those describe you, PLP isn't right. Be honest with me."*

If they hesitate on the 10-year commitment, slow down. The wrong sale is worse than no sale.

## Bonus disclosure for AIA Vitality

> *"AIA Vitality PowerUp Dollar is also non-guaranteed. The 10% PowerUp Dollar of your Insured Amount adjusts annually based on Vitality status, but AIA reserves the right to vary fees with 1 month's notice."* [PS p.12, p.15-16]

For Vitality-enrolled prospects, this is a separate disclosure to deliver.

# Part 2 — Budget-Choice Close, Portfolio Matching, Retention Defense

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Two budget options, never one. Portfolio matched to risk profile (never above). 30-day check-in discipline to defend retention against competing-FC predation. The testimonial chain at delivery — when the prospect is happiest with the decision — is the highest-leverage referral moment in PLP's lifecycle.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver the budget-choice close cleanly, match the prospect to the right Mercer-guided portfolio, set up Vitality enrolment (when applicable), and book the 30-day check-in.

---

## The budget-choice close

The verbatim:

> *"Would you prefer starting at $2,400/year or $3,600/year? Your budget — which one would you prefer? Now of course afterwards you go into the iPOS."* [Video 11]

Two options. Both move forward. Don't give one option ever — that's a yes/no close.

## Portfolio matching (the discipline)

The rule:

> *"Match the portfolio to the client's risk profile — you can go lower or similar, not higher. If the client is cautious, propose Pro Cautious. If balanced, Pro Balanced. If adventurous, you can propose up to Pro Balanced."* [Video 2]

| Prospect risk profile | Recommended portfolio |
|---|---|
| Cautious | Pro Cautious |
| Balanced | Pro Balanced |
| Adventurous | Pro Balanced (or Pro Adventurous if explicitly requested) |

**Never propose ABOVE the prospect's risk profile.** This is RNF-tested and trust-critical.

The portfolio mechanics:

> *"Three portfolio options: Pro Adventurous, Pro Balanced, Pro Cautious. Reviewed annually based on Mercer's professional research."* [Brochure p.4]

Annual rebalancing is automatic — the prospect doesn't need to manage allocation themselves.

## The disclosure script (Video 2 verbatim)

> *"Please inform your customers that premiums are not guaranteed, the policy values are not guaranteed, the prices of the funds can go up and down, so there is a possibility of loss. There are risks of investments to take note of and past performance is not an indication of future performance. Please take note to inform your customer about the 14-day free-look period."* [Video 2]

This is the AIA-internal compliance reminder — deliver it externally too, in plain English.

## AIA Vitality at the close (when applicable)

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| Membership cost | S$8/month [Video 2] |
| PowerUp Dollar Base | 10% of Insured Amount [PS p.15] |
| Annual adjustment by status | Bronze -10% / Silver -5% / Gold +0% / Platinum +5% [PS p.15-16] |
| Range | Min 0%, max 150% of Base |
| Adjustment lock | Later of 75th birthday or 15th policy anniversary [PS p.15-16] |

For health-conscious prospects, this is meaningful — set up at delivery.

## The iPOS / illustrator presentation flow

Walk in this order:
1. Monthly contribution → projected returns
2. Retirement income scenario
3. Premium-holiday scenario
4. Ad-hoc withdrawal scenario
5. Urgency framing (start now vs later)

[Video 13]

## The retention defense (the highest-leverage move)

PLP has the highest retention risk in the AIA stack. Why: a competing FC 6 months later can pull up the prospect's iPOS screen, point at the Year-1 80% charge, and convince them they bought a bad plan.

The defense begins at delivery:

### Move 1 — The "laptop" pre-emption

At delivery, restate the premium charge schedule and the Year-5+ allocation ramp. Make sure the prospect understands the front-loading is a feature, not a bug.

### Move 2 — The 30-day check-in (calendared)

Book the check-in BEFORE the prospect leaves the room. Send the calendar invite. The script:

> *"30 days from now we'll review your iPOS screen together. By then you'll have seen the first month's debit. We'll talk through any questions and confirm the policy is performing as expected."*

This pre-empts the "I checked my account and it looks low" moment that drives free-look cancellations.

### Move 3 — The testimonial chain at delivery

The verbatim:

> *"The reason people buy from me is they like and trust me. See this chain? Each person referred the next. That's how I've built my practice."* [Video 14]

Show the chain literally — names of past clients (with their permission), the chain of referrals from each. This is social proof at the moment of maximum trust.

### Move 4 — The 12-month review

Standard annual review — but for PLP specifically, the agenda includes:
- Premium charge transition (Year 5+ → 100% allocation)
- Vitality status check + PowerUp Dollar adjustment
- Milestone Increase eligibility (if life event occurred)
- Portfolio rebalancing review (Mercer-driven)
- Top-up opportunity assessment

## The "case is closed" definition

A PLP case is NOT closed at iPOS submission. A case is closed when:

1. ✅ iPOS submitted
2. ✅ FHR signed
3. ✅ Policy contract issued
4. ✅ 14-day free-look window passed without cancellation
5. ✅ First GIRO debit confirmed (Day 30)
6. ✅ 30-day check-in completed
7. ✅ Vitality enrolment (if applicable)
8. ✅ At least 1 referral name captured

If you stop at iPOS and never run #4–8, you closed half a case.

## The benchmark

A senior FC running this discipline produces:

- **1–2 PLP cases per week** (PLP is the slower, higher-ticket sell)
- **40%+ first-appointment close rate** (lower than HSGM/UCC because of qualifying)
- **<5% free-look cancellation rate** (good retention discipline)
- **Average premium S$200–500/month** range
- **Multi-product attach rate 70%+** (PLP is rarely sold standalone)

That's the target.

- C) Premiums guaranteed, policy values NOT guaranteed, past performance indicative
- D) Only the past-performance disclosure is required

**Why:** The 3 pillars: premiums NOT guaranteed, policy values NOT guaranteed, past performance NOT indicative [Brochure p.9, PS p.11].

2. **The 14-day free-look period begins on:**
- A) The application submission date
- B) The day the policy documents are received (electronic from SMS/email; postal +7 days; hand-delivered +7 days from delivery to courier/representative) ✓
- C) The first GIRO debit date
- D) The 30th day of the policy

**Why:** Free-look starts when the prospect receives the policy documents — with deemed-receipt rules for electronic, postal, and hand-delivered cases [PS p.8].

3. **The reinstatement window after lapse is:**
- A) 1 year
- B) 2 years
- C) 5 years from lapse date, subject to T&Cs, with no back-payment of premiums required ✓
- D) 10 years

**Why:** Reinstatement is allowed within 5 years of lapse date with no back-premiums required [PS p.8].

4. **The Limitation of Benefit for suicide within Year 1 of issue or reinstatement is:**
- A) Full Insured Amount paid
- B) Liability limited to 100% of policy value ✓
- C) Nothing paid
- D) 50% of Insured Amount paid

**Why:** Suicide within 1 year of issue/reinstatement limits AIA's liability to 100% of policy value [PS p.8].

5. **The portfolio matching rule is:**
- A) Always recommend Pro Adventurous for highest returns
- B) Always recommend Pro Cautious to be safe
- C) Match the portfolio to the prospect's risk profile — you can go lower or similar, never higher ✓
- D) Let the prospect choose without input

**Why:** Match risk profile — never propose above. RNF-tested and trust-critical [Video 2, Day 5 Part 2].

6. **The AIA Vitality PowerUp Dollar Base is:**
- A) 1% of Insured Amount
- B) 5% of Insured Amount
- C) 10% of Insured Amount, adjusting -10/-5/+0/+5% by Bronze/Silver/Gold/Platinum, locking at later of 75 or year 15 ✓
- D) 50% of Insured Amount, fixed for life

**Why:** Base PowerUp = 10% of IA; adjusted annually by Vitality status; min 0%, max 150% of Base; lock at later of 75th birthday or 15th policy anniversary [PS p.15-16].

7. **AIA reserves the right to vary fees and charges with how much prior written notice?**
- A) 1 week
- B) Not less than 1 month in writing ✓
- C) 6 months
- D) Fees cannot be varied

**Why:** AIA reserves the right to vary fees and charges with no less than 1 month's prior written notice [PS p.12].

8. **The budget-choice close rule is:**
- A) Always offer one option to keep the close clean
- B) Always offer two budget options (e.g., $2,400/year vs $3,600/year) so the prospect chooses between two yeses ✓
- C) Offer five tiers and let the prospect pick
- D) Don't mention price; close on Insured Amount only

**Why:** Two budget options is the rule — both move forward; never give a yes/no close [Video 11, Day 5 Part 2].

9. **The retention defense (the highest-leverage move) starts when?**
- A) At the 12-month annual review
- B) At delivery — the laptop pre-emption + booked 30-day check-in + testimonial chain — defending against competing-FC predation that points at Year-1 charges ✓
- C) Only after a free-look cancellation
- D) Never — clients churn naturally

**Why:** The defense begins at delivery — laptop pre-emption, calendared 30-day check-in, testimonial chain, plus the 12-month review [Day 5 Part 2].

10. **Scenario: A PLP case has been submitted via iPOS and FHR signed. By Day 5's discipline, when is the case actually "closed"?**
- A) When iPOS is submitted
- B) When the policy contract is issued
- C) When all 8 steps are done: iPOS submitted, FHR signed, contract issued, free-look passed, first GIRO debit confirmed, 30-day check-in completed, Vitality enrolled (if applicable), at least 1 referral captured ✓
- D) When the first premium is paid

**Why:** The case-is-closed definition has 8 steps — stopping at iPOS submission is closing only half a case [Day 5 Part 2].
