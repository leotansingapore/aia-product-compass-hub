# Day 2 — Run the Meeting: Fact-Find + 5-Step Pitch Flow

> **Today's frame:** A clean fact-find prevents the wrong sale. Three qualifying conditions, then the 5-step pitch flow that converts the abstract age-100 illustration into a felt monthly profit. The linearisation step is the one most FCs skip — and the one that lands.

This is a merged module — Part 1 covers the qualifying fact-find. Part 2 covers the 5-step pitch flow with the linearisation math. Estimated read + drill: ~60 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — The Fact-Find: Qualifying the Prospect Before Pitching PLP

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Three qualifying conditions, in order: (1) 10+ year horizon and accepts non-guaranteed returns, (2) protection foundation (hospital, accident, base CI/death) at least mapped, (3) financial picture clear enough to right-size cover and premium. Anchor on the 25X rule for retirement; use the budget→coverage table for the recommendation.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: deploy the 25X rule fluently, deliver the budget→coverage table from memory, recognise the 5 disqualifying conditions, and walk away with all the data iPOS needs.

---

## The 3 qualifying conditions (in order)

### Condition 1: 10+ year horizon AND non-guaranteed-returns tolerance

PLP is an ILP. The first 10 years carry the No-Lapse Privilege but require the front-loaded premium charges. Without a 10+ year horizon, the prospect loses money.

The script:

> *"This plan needs at least 10 years. The reason: in years 1-4, a chunk of your premium goes to charges. From year 5, it's 100% allocated. From year 10, it's 102% with the bonus. If you pull out before year 10, you take a hit. Before we go further — can you commit 10 years to this?"*

If "no" — pivot to APA (lower minimum commitment) or term/GPP (no investment risk).

### Condition 2: Protection foundation mapped

The prospect needs hospital, accident, base CI/death cover BEFORE PLP is layered on. PLP layered on a missing hospital plan is malpractice.

The script:

> *"Before we talk about PLP — what does your protection picture look like? Hospital plan? Accident? CI cover via GPP or term? Let me see what's already in place."*

If gaps exist, acknowledge:

> *"Let's address the foundation first. PLP is a permanent floor — you'd want HSGM and at least basic CI in place before we layer this. We can talk PLP today as a plan, but we should book a separate appointment for HSGM first."*

### Condition 3: Financial picture clear

You can't right-size PLP without knowing income, fixed obligations, dependants, business interests, existing whole-life cover.

The script:

> *"What are your top priorities right now? What are your goals in life? What would financial freedom look like for you?"* [Video 14]

Capture:
- Annual income / business income
- Existing whole-life or estate planning instruments
- Dependant timeline (kids' independence, parents' care, spouse's protection)
- Business succession plan (if SME owner)
- Legacy figure (if applicable)

## The 25X rule (anchor on financial freedom)

> *"Always anchor back to financial freedom and financial independence — they need a certain sum whereby their passive will exceed their expense. So if they need $2,000 per month, they need at least $24,000 per year of passive — they need to have quite a large portfolio of $600K-$900K."* [Video 14]

The math:

```
Monthly expense × 12 × 25 = Portfolio target
$2,000 × 12 × 25 = $600,000
$5,000 × 12 × 25 = $1,500,000
$10,000 × 12 × 25 = $3,000,000
```

Use this to convert vague "I want to retire comfortably" into a number the prospect anchors on.

## The 5 disqualifying conditions

When NOT to pitch PLP:

> *"Client wants pure investment only, prefers DIY/robo-advisors, totally risk-averse, can't afford $100/month minimum, wants short-term access to funds."* [Video 14]

If any of these apply:
- Pure investment → APA
- DIY/robo → don't sell PLP, sell HSGM/GPP for protection only
- Risk-averse → endowment or term
- < S$100/month budget → wait until cashflow allows
- Short-term liquidity need → not insurance, not now

## The features-vs-benefits soft sell

When you DO recommend PLP, frame the features as benefits the prospect would value:

> *"Would you like a plan that gives your family 10 years of income security if you're gone, that you can pause if life happens, and that grows even after you stop paying?"* [Video 14]

This is a single sentence that touches three differentiating features (cover, pause, post-payment growth) and ties them to outcomes the prospect cares about.

## What a complete PLP fact-find looks like

8–12 minutes (longer than other products because of the qualifying conditions). You should walk away with:

- ✅ Annual income / business income
- ✅ 10-year commitment confirmed
- ✅ Non-guaranteed-returns tolerance confirmed
- ✅ Protection foundation mapped (hospital, accident, CI, term)
- ✅ Existing whole-life / estate instruments
- ✅ Legacy figure (for estate-planning prospects)
- ✅ 25X retirement anchor
- ✅ Pre-recommendation budget band ($100/200/300/400+/month)

If any are missing, the close fumbles. Go back and ask.

# Part 2 — The 5-Step Pitch Flow

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Anchor → Juxtapose → Illustrator → Linearise → Choose. The linearisation step is the one most FCs skip — and it's the conversion lever. "$7.8 million at age 100" is too distant to feel real. "$1,445 every month for the rest of your life" lands.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to: deliver the 5 steps in sequence, run the linearisation math live with the prospect's calculator, deploy the GPP-vs-PLP juxtaposition, and close on two budget options.

---

## The 5 phases

```
Phase 1     Phase 2          Phase 3          Phase 4         Phase 5
Anchor   →  Juxtapose     →  Illustrator   →  Linearise   →   Choose
            (vs inferior     (iResource +     (monthly $       (two budget
            alternative)     scenarios)       profit)          options)
```

| Phase | Time | Micro-commit |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Anchor on goal (25X / legacy / freedom) | 4 min | Goal expressed in dollars |
| 2. Juxtapose vs inferior alternative | 4 min | "I see why PLP is different" |
| 3. iResource illustrator (3 scenarios) | 6 min | "The numbers look real" |
| 4. Linearise into monthly profit | 4 min | "$1,400/month — that's real income" |
| 5. Two-budget-option close | 3 min | Budget picked |
| **Total** | **~25 min** | **Plan locked** |

## Phase 1 — Anchor on the goal

For most prospects, the anchor is the 25X rule (retirement) OR a legacy figure (estate planning).

Estate-planning anchor:

> *"What does the protection floor for your family look like if anything happens to you? Number, not feeling — what's the minimum your family needs to be okay?"*

Retirement anchor (25X):

> *"What monthly income do you need from age 65 onwards? Multiply by 12 and 25. That's your portfolio target."*

This number drives the Insured Amount recommendation.

## Phase 2 — Juxtapose against an inferior alternative

The most common juxtaposition: a 25-year fixed savings plan or buying GPP + ProAchiever separately.

The script for the savings-plan juxtaposition:

> *"After 25 years, do you think $87k is really going to be beneficial? Is it anywhere near the $2 million retirement goal we discussed? And actually after 25 years, you'd probably find some way — eh, where do I put this $87,000 — and you still have to buy another savings plan again."* [Video 5]

The script for the GPP + APA juxtaposition (high-net-worth prospect):

> *"You could buy ProAchiever at $200/month plus GPP at $200/month — same total premium as PLP at $400. APA gets you ~$4.4M at age 100, GPP gets you ~$324K of cash value, total ~$4.7M. PLP at the same premium gets you ~$7M. Different mechanic, different result."* [Video 8 — sample numbers]

Don't bash the alternative. Show the trade.

## Phase 3 — iResource illustrator (3 scenarios)

Walk three scenarios, in this order:
1. **Pause scenario** — premium holiday, what happens to cover and value
2. **Partial withdrawal scenario** — for a major purchase or emergency
3. **Regular withdrawal scenario** — at age 65, $5,000/month

[Video 5]

The structural script:

> *"Mr. Prospect, this is how much you get at age 100. Total premiums paid: ~$28,000. Total returns: ~$7.8 million. And you have all these features: pause for several years with minimal impact, withdraw for car / education / travel, regular withdrawal of $5,000/month from age 65, short-term payment minimum 10 years, lifelong coverage, lifelong returns."* [Video 5]

## Phase 4 — Linearise into monthly profit

This is the move most FCs skip. The script:

> *"Take out your calculator. Put in 942,800. Minus 162,000. What's your profit? $780,000. Divide by 45 years — $17,333/year. Divide by 12 — that's $1,445 per month. By putting in just $300/month, you're earning $1,400+ every single month in the long term."* [Video 14]

The formula:

```
Linearised monthly profit = (Total return − Total premium paid) / Years held / 12
```

Make the prospect own the calculator. Their hand types the numbers. Their brain processes them.

## Phase 5 — Two-budget-option close

Never give one option. Always two. The principle from UCC carries over:

> *"Between $300 a month or $400 a month, which one do you prefer, Mr. Prospect?"* [Video 5]

Both answers move forward. The prospect chooses between two yeses.

| Profile | Total in | Total return |
|---|---|---|
| 20-yo, $200/mo GPP equivalent | ~$48,000–49,000 | ~$300,000–400,000 [Video 5, 7] |

## The urgency framing (Video 11)

For prospects who say "let me think":

> *"If you start now versus 10 years from now — pricing is cheaper today, you accumulate more cash value over time, age-related premium increases are avoided."* [Video 11]

Three reasons to start now, all numerical, no emotional pressure.

## The pacing rule

If you're approaching the 30-minute mark and still in Phase 4 (linearisation), you're losing them. Compress to one budget option, lock the principle, book delivery for next session. **Better to close in two visits than to lose in one.**
