# Part 1 — The Numbers: Premium Charges, Sum-at-Risk, Riders, Surrender

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Memorise three numeric concepts cold — the premium charge schedule (80/55/50/8/0%), the Sum-at-Risk formula (drives Max's insurance-free-over-time mechanic), and the LCC rider mechanics (300% cap, Power Reset, waiting periods). ## Top-up premium charge: flat 5%

Different from regular premium — top-ups are a flat 5% charge per top-up [PS p.3]. Use top-ups to inject lump sums opportunistically.

## Sum-at-Risk math (drives Max's killer feature)

The formula:

```
Sum-at-Risk = Insured Amount + total top-up premiums − total withdrawals − policy value
```

[Product Summary p.4]

When SAR ≤ 0 in any month, **no Benefit Charge is payable for that month** [PS p.4].

What this means in plain language:
- Buy at age 30 with S$200,000 Insured Amount
- Pay regular premium for 20 years
- Policy value compounds and crosses S$200,000 around year 30-40 (depends on returns)
- From that point onward — **insurance is free** (on the Max plan)

The verbatim:

> *"For Max, the death benefit is the higher of insured amount or cash value. Once your policy value crosses beyond $100,000, you're not paying for any insurance anymore."* [Video 6]

> *"If the Sum-at-Risk is zero or negative in a given month, no Benefit Charge is payable for that month."* [PS p.4]

This is the structural cost-saving that separates PLP Max from competitor whole-life plans.

## The 10-Year No-Lapse Privilege

> *"Within the first 10 policy years, the policy will not lapse even if policy value is insufficient to pay all charges, provided all regular premiums are paid and no withdrawals made."* [PS p.7, Brochure footnote 8]

## Rider mechanics (3 main riders)

### TDA (Total Disability and Accidental — accelerates basic, ends at 70)

Accelerates the basic death benefit. Pays out on TPD before age 70. [PS p.18-19]

### TCA (Total Critical Acceleration — accelerates basic for 73 major CIs, ends at 100)

Accelerates the basic death benefit on diagnosis of any of 73 major-stage CIs. Critical illness per-life limit S$3,000,000 aggregated across AIA. Ends at age 100. [PS p.21-24, p.22]

### LCC (Lifetime Critical Cover — additional, NOT accelerating)

The flagship rider. Adds an ADDITIONAL benefit (does not accelerate basic). Covers 150 multi-stage CIs + 15 special conditions. Terminates at 300% payout. Ends at age 100. [PS p.25-30]

| LCC mechanic | Detail |
|---|---|
| Conditions covered | 150 multi-stage CIs + 15 special conditions [PS p.25-30] |
| Maximum payout | **300%** of Insured Amount across multiple Medical Condition claims [PS p.25] |
| Power Reset | Prevailing Insured Amount restored to 100% **12 months** after last diagnosed Medical Condition [PS p.29, Brochure footnote 4] |
| Special Condition payout | 20% of Insured Amount, max S$25,000 per condition, up to 10 claims across 15 special conditions [PS p.29] |
| Angioplasty cap | 10% of Prevailing Insured Amount, max S$25,000 [PS p.25] |

# Part 2 — Top 5 Client Questions: Crisp, Scripted Answers

> **The one idea for Part 2:** The five questions clients actually ask are not the same as the technical issues an FC worries about. They are friction points: "where's my money", "will it get more expensive", "what if I can't pay", "what if it crashes", "can I take it out". One-breath sentences, each tied to a specific page in the Product Summary so the FC can show, not just say.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver each answer in under 30 seconds, with the page citation that makes it authoritative.

---

## Q1 — "Why does my account look so low after I put in $10K?"

The honest answer: front-loaded premium charge schedule.

> *"There are charges in the first year, second year, third year, fourth year for all these up-front fees — that means not the full amount is being invested. Think of it like you buy a laptop — upfront there are a lot of costs, but beyond that the charges tend to get lower and eventually drop to zero. From the 5th year onwards, 100% of your premium goes to investment."* [Video 1, Brochure p.3]

The reframe:

> *"Year 1 is the worst-looking year. By Year 6 you'll see the trajectory. By Year 10, with the 2% bonus, you're getting 102% allocated. The plan rewards patience."*

[Source: PS p.3]

## Q2 — "Will my insurance cost go up as I age?"

On Max — no. On Plus — yes (sum-at-risk constant).

> *"If you're on the Max plan, your insurance cost actually goes down — your sum at risk goes down, and in fact goes to zero. Once your policy value crosses your insured amount, no more insurance charges are deducted."* [Video 2, Video 6]

The mechanic:

```
Year 5 (sample, age 35, $200K IA):  Policy value $25K  →  SAR = $175K  →  cost based on $175K
Year 25 (age 55):                    Policy value $250K →  SAR = $0    →  cost = $0
```

The bonus message:

> *"After 65, the insurance is essentially free."* [Video 9]

[Sources: Video 2, Video 6, PS p.4]

## Q3 — "What if I can't afford the premium one year?"

Premium Holiday is built in. After Year 2, no holiday charge.

> *"You can pause the plan with the premium holiday feature — after the first two years, there's no charge for pausing. Coverage continues even while you're paused. The plan won't lapse during the first 10 years as long as your premiums have been paid and you haven't withdrawn."* [Video 1, Video 2, PS p.7]

The discipline:
- Years 1-2: Premium Holiday = S$50/month charge
- Year 3+: Premium Holiday = S$0 charge
- Years 1-10: No-Lapse Privilege protects against insufficient policy value (if regular premiums paid + no withdrawals)

[Sources: PS p.4, p.7, Brochure footnote 8]

## Q4 — "What happens if the investments crash?"

The 10-year No-Lapse Privilege is the safety net.

> *"Within the first 10 years, your policy will not lapse even if the policy value drops to zero, as long as all regular premiums are paid and you haven't withdrawn anything. That's the No-Lapse Privilege."* [PS p.7, Brochure footnote 8]

After year 10, the policy depends on policy value alone — but by then, you've had 10 years of compounding plus the 2% bonus, so the value should be substantial.

The framing:

> *"It's not a Year-1 plan. It's a Year-30 plan. The structure protects against short-term market crashes; the long-term mechanics protect against long-term inflation."*

## Q5 — "Can I take money out?"

Yes — after end of Year 2, no charge for partial withdrawals.

> *"After the second policy year, you can withdraw any time. Minimum withdrawal is $1,000 and you need to keep at least $1,000 in the policy. No charge for partial withdrawals after Year 2."* [PS p.6, Brochure p.3]

The trade-off to disclose:

> *"Withdrawals reduce your policy value, which on the Max plan reduces your insurance-free-over-time benefit. If you take out $50K at age 50, you may push back the year your insurance becomes free. So plan withdrawals deliberately."*

[Sources: PS p.6, Brochure p.3]

## Bonus question — "What about Vitality?"

> *"AIA Vitality membership is S$8/month. Your PowerUp Dollar — 10% of your Insured Amount — adjusts annually by your Vitality status. Bronze means it shrinks 10% a year. Platinum means it grows 5% a year. Min 0%, max 150% of Base. The adjustment locks at the later of your 75th birthday or 15th policy anniversary."*

[Source: PS p.15-16, Video 2]

For health-conscious prospects, this is meaningful additional cover at no extra premium.
