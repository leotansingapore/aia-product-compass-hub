# Day 2 — Run the Meeting: Fact-Find + 8-Phase Pitch Flow

> **Today's frame:** The fact-find is what most FCs skip and what decides whether the pitch lands. Three things first: confirm what the prospect already has via CPF Healthcare Dashboard, get their own ranking via the four-pillar question, and read urgency. Then escalate through 8 phases — pain before solution, every time, with checkpoints.

This is a merged module — Part 1 covers the fact-find. Part 2 covers the 8-phase pitch flow. Estimated read + drill: ~60 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — The Fact-Find: What to Ask Before Pitching HSGM

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Walk in asking the four-pillar question and listening — the prospect points at hospitalisation themselves, and the rest of the meeting is downhill. Walk in already pitching and the prospect resists.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: pull up the CPF Healthcare Dashboard with the prospect in 60 seconds, run the four-pillar discovery question naturally, surface the urgency signal (pre-existing-condition risk), and walk away with everything IPOS needs.

---

## Step 1 — CPF Healthcare Dashboard check (60 seconds)

The fastest way to know if the prospect already has an IP:

> *"Quick one — do you mind logging into the CPF Healthcare Dashboard? It'll show us exactly what you have right now. Just MediShield Life means no IP yet. If there's an IP listed, we'll see which insurer."*

Three outcomes:

| What you see | Conversation type |
|---|---|
| Only MediShield Life | **Fresh acquisition** — full HSGM pitch |
| HSGM (existing AIA) | Service review or rider upgrade conversation |
| Competitor IP (Pru, GE, NTUC, etc.) | **Switching conversation** — apply discipline (Day 4 Objection 5) |

If switching, only proceed if the prospect is healthy and has no claims history. Otherwise leave the existing IP in place and sell complementary cover (CI / accident / life) instead. Switching a claims-history prospect risks losing them to a fresh-underwriting decline.

## Step 2 — The four-pillar discovery question

Lay out the four protection categories. Ask which matters most. Don't pre-decide:

> *"Out of these four — hospital, accident, critical illness, death or disability — which one do you think is most important to you?"*

Then the follow-up:

> *"Which of these four do you think is the first line of defence?"*

The prospect points at hospitalisation themselves about 80% of the time. The other 20% need the script from Day 1 Part 2 ("if you get into an accident, you'll be hospitalised first…").

Either way, the prospect has now ranked the priorities **out loud**. Mirror their ranking back to them. They've now committed to caring about hospitalisation.

## Step 3 — Surface the pain (real bills)

Bring out specific numbers. Pick one that matches the prospect's life stage / family situation:

| Real claim example | Use case |
|---|---|
| Breast cancer surgery: **S$213,000** | Family history of cancer, women 35+ |
| General hospital expenses: **S$145,000** | Generalist conviction |
| Chemotherapy 9 days: **S$12,000** | "Even short stays add up" |
| Two-day hospital stay: **S$19,000** | "It happens fast" |
| Complex case: **S$44,000** | Mid-tier, easy to imagine |

[All from Video 6]

Then the framing question:

> *"Recently my client was hospitalised for [X] days. The bill was S$[Y]. Could you write a cheque for that today?"*

Specific worked example for rich-but-uncovered prospects:

> *"S$40,000 hospital bill against S$20,000 in savings = goes into debt."*

The prospect's brain finishes the sentence themselves.

## Step 4 — Surface urgency (pre-existing condition risk)

If the prospect says *"I'll buy next time"*:

> *"What if something happens between now and then? If you develop a pre-existing condition before you buy, you may face outright rejection, loading on premiums, or exclusions on certain conditions."*

The cost of waiting isn't just inflation — it's the underwriting window closing. Every year delayed is age + risk of any condition surfacing.

## Step 5 — Capture the IPOS-quote data

Before you open IPOS, you need:

- ✅ Age next birthday
- ✅ Smoker / non-smoker (smoker premium ~**40.8% higher** [Video 3])
- ✅ Existing employer/corporate medical (annual cap, exclusions)
- ✅ Existing IP and rider (from CPF Healthcare Dashboard)
- ✅ Family history of cancer (drives Cancer Care Pro recommendation)
- ✅ Ward preference (private vs public vs B1)
- ✅ Bank for GIRO
- ✅ Cadence preference (almost always monthly)

If any of these is missing, the close fumbles. Go back and ask.

## Reference numbers to know during fact-find

| Item | Number |
|---|---|
| Smoker premium loading | ~40.8% higher than non-smoker |
| Premium climb age 25→35 | ~+44.5% (illustrative: S$583 → S$842) |
| Plan A vs Plan B premium gap (age 25) | ~59.7% higher |
| Plan A vs Plan B premium gap (age 35) | ~48% higher |

Use these to honestly answer "is it worth waiting?" — the answer is no, costs explode with age + Plan A becomes harder to justify the longer they wait.

# Part 2 — The 1-1 Pitch Flow: 8 Phases with Checkpoints

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Pain comes before solution every single time. Never name a feature before naming the problem it solves. Pre-authorisation only makes sense after the prospect feels the pain of paying S$50K upfront and waiting two weeks for reimbursement. Use checkpoints — never lecture more than 2–3 minutes.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to: name all 8 phases in order, deploy the problem-feature-benefit pattern on every feature, demo Teladoc + AIA Quality Healthcare Partners + AIA+ app live from your phone, and close on the two-option binary.

---

## The 8 phases

```
Phase 1     Phase 2              Phase 3       Phase 4         Phase 5        Phase 6       Phase 7         Phase 8
Open    →   First-Line-Defence → Pain      →   Value-Stack →   Price-Anchor → Reveal    →   Demo Diff   →   Two-Option Close
            (4-pillar question)  (real bills) (S$2M / 13mo)   (let them guess)
```

| Phase | Time | Micro-commit |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Open with four-pillar question | 3 min | Prospect ranks priorities |
| 2. Position hospital as first line of defence | 2 min | "Yes, hospitalisation comes first" |
| 3. Establish pain (real bill examples) | 4 min | "I couldn't write that cheque" |
| 4. Value-stack the headline features | 4 min | "Sounds comprehensive" |
| 5. Ask prospect to guess the price | 2 min | Anchor at S$200/month |
| 6. Reveal the actual cash portion | 2 min | "That's surprisingly affordable" |
| 7. Demo differentiators (problem-feature-benefit) | 8 min | Multiple "I see why I need that" moments |
| 8. Two-option close | 3 min | Plan A or Plan B picked |
| **Total** | **~28 min** | **8+ small yeses** |

## Phase 3 — Establishing pain (the script)

> *"In Singapore, medical bills can be very expensive — S$10K, S$20K, S$50K, even S$100K or S$200K. How would you feel if you suddenly had to pay a S$30K hospital bill when you only have S$10K in savings?"*

Then sharpen with a specific scenario:

> *"Imagine you have to pay S$50K upfront and wait two weeks for the money to come in. Do you have S$50K to pay for the surgery? Even if you do, would you want to wait two weeks?"*

For young-adult prospects who push back on private:

> *"Your priority for a public bed as a young adult is very low. You might wait three to seven hours just to get a bed. You cannot choose your doctor, you share rooms, and the waiting time is long."*

## Phase 7 — Demo differentiators (the problem-feature-benefit framework)

Never name a feature without naming the problem first. Four examples, in order of leverage:

### Pre-authorisation

> *"Imagine you have to pay S$50K upfront and wait two weeks for the money to come in."* [Pause. Wait.]
>
> *"With pre-authorisation, AIA processes within 3 working days BEFORE the surgery. The bill goes direct to AIA — you don't pay upfront."*

### AIA Quality Healthcare Partners (preferred provider panel)

> *"Do you want to speak to some shady doctor? Some doctors see you have insurance and overcharge or prescribe unnecessary treatments. With AIA's preferred provider panel — over 600 specialists vetted by AIA — that risk is screened out. Plus you get appointment booking within 3 working days."*

### Teladoc personal case management

> *"If a doctor says you need chemo, is that 100% the right treatment? Wouldn't you want a second opinion, or even a third? With Teladoc personal case management, you get connected to over 50,000 leading global specialists. This service normally costs around S$20,000. It is free for our customers."*

### AIA+ app + telemedicine

> *"Imagine queueing three or four hours at a GP just for an MC. With this, get an MC online in about a minute."*

Demo all four LIVE if possible — open the AIA+ app, show the preferred-provider directory, show the Teladoc launch screen. Physical demo beats any slide.

## Phase 8 — The two-option close

Never ask "do you want to buy?" — that's a one-shot binary that invites *"let me think about it."* Always ask:

> *"Which would you prefer — public or private?"*

Both answers move the meeting forward to IPOS. The rider is **assumed in** — never offer with-rider/without-rider as the choice (that fragments the close).

The bridge into Phase 8 (after Phase 7 demos):

> *"Is it okay if I take the next 10 minutes and walk you through the numbers?"*

Permission re-ask — gives them control while you set up IPOS.

## Reference numbers for Phase 6 (price reveal)

Verify in IPOS at quote time — these move with each rate update. Approximate bands:

| Plan | Young adult cash band |
|---|---|
| HSG Max A (private) | S$70–S$80/month |
| HSG Max B (public A-class) | S$18–S$20/month |

Plus ~S$29/year MediSave shortfall for young Plan A (the AIA top-up exceeds the Additional Withdrawal Limit by ~S$29).

## Phase reference: AIA Quality Healthcare Partner specialist count

The brochure says **over 600**. Older videos say "over 380" (Video 0) or "900+" (Video 6/7) or "967". Use the brochure number and verify live on aia.com.sg/qualityhealthcare. [Brochure p.5]

## Pacing rule

If you're approaching the 30-minute mark and you're still in Phase 7, you're losing them. Compress the demo, jump to the two-option close, set up IPOS while reinforcing value, and book delivery for next session. **Better to close in two visits than to lose in one.**

3. **Practise the problem-feature-benefit on all 4 differentiators** (pre-auth, preferred providers, Teladoc, AIA+ app). Each delivered in under 60 seconds.
4. **Open the AIA+ app on your phone** and walk through the preferred-provider directory. Practise the live-demo move.
5. **Practise the two-option close** in 3 tonalities. Pick the one that sounds most natural.
