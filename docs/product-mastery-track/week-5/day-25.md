# Day 5 — Compliance + Close: Disclosures, Two-Option Close, Paperwork, Delivery

> **Today's frame:** Compliance moves are not optional and not boring. The four MAS-required disclosures, said out loud and acknowledged, are what stand between a clean sale and a chargeback during free-look. The two-option close — "private or public?" with rider assumed in — is the natural end of a meeting run correctly from the four-pillar question onward.

This is the final module. Part 1 covers the four mandatory disclosures + RNF-relevant phrasing. Part 2 covers the two-option close, IPOS workflow, breakfast-comparison framing, and post-close service rhythm. Estimated read + drill: ~50 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — Compliance + Risk Disclosures

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Four lines, said out loud and acknowledged by the prospect, separate a compliant book from an MAS complaint: premiums-not-guaranteed, one-IP-per-MediSave, MAS switching disclosure, value-added-services-not-contractual. Make them part of the close, not a footnote.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to deliver all four mandatory disclosures cleanly in under 90 seconds total, recognise when HSGM is the wrong product (and say so), and pass an RNF sample audit without a coaching note.

---

## The four mandatory disclosures

### Disclosure 1 — Premiums are not guaranteed

> *"Premiums are not guaranteed. AIA reserves the right to review and increase premiums on renewal, and premiums also rise with age."*

The brochure citation: [Brochure p.20]

The full script:

> *"Just to flag clearly — your premium today is not locked in for life the way a whole-life premium is. AIA can re-price the portfolio. Premiums also climb with age automatically. The good news: premiums don't go up because YOU claimed individually — that's the no-claim-loading guarantee. But across the whole HSGM portfolio, AIA can re-price."*

### Disclosure 2 — One MediSave-approved IP per Insured

> *"You can use your CPF MediSave account to purchase only one MediSave-approved medical insurance scheme per Insured at any one time."* [Brochure p.22]

This is critical because: if the prospect has an existing IP elsewhere, buying HSGM via MediSave **automatically terminates the old IP on the back-end.** They don't choose which to keep — the system enforces the rule.

The script:

> *"Important — only one MediSave-approved IP per person at a time. If we move forward with HSGM, the IP system back-end will auto-terminate any existing IP you have. Want to confirm you've checked the CPF Healthcare Dashboard already?"*

### Disclosure 3 — MAS switching disclosure (verbatim)

> *"You are discouraged from switching from an existing accident and/or health insurance policy to a new one without considering whether the switch is detrimental, as there may be potential disadvantages with switching. A penalty may be imposed for early policy termination and the new policy may cost more or have fewer benefits at the same cost."* [Brochure p.22 — verbatim]

This is **MAS-required language.** Don't paraphrase it. Read it or recite it word-for-word.

The lead-in:

> *"I want to flag this clearly — MAS requires me to say this. You're discouraged from switching from an existing health insurance policy to a new one without considering whether the switch is detrimental. There may be potential disadvantages — early termination penalty, the new policy may cost more or have fewer benefits at the same cost. Let's go through the comparison together before you decide."*

### Disclosure 4 — Value-added services are not contractual

> *"These services — Teladoc, AIA Vitality, WhiteCoat consultations, AIA Quality Healthcare Partners — are great current value-adds, but they are not contractual benefits. AIA can change or withdraw them. What is contractual is on the policy schedule and the policy contract."* [Brochure p.21]

This protects the FC against future "but you said Teladoc was free!" complaints.

## The "anti-market" disclosure (the trust move)

The single biggest credibility move:

> *"This plan isn't right for everyone. The anti-market for HSGM is people who:*
>
> *- Will only ever use C-class public-hospital wards (MediShield Life is enough for that)*
> *- Don't want to pay any premium for upgrade options (HSGM is the upgrade)*
> *- Have a pre-existing condition that would be excluded — better to talk to an underwriter directly first*
>
> *If any of those describe you, HSGM might not be the right plan. Be honest with me."*

If the prospect hesitates on any of these, slow down. The right move is sometimes to NOT sell.

## The pre-existing exclusion details

The waiting periods to disclose:

| Condition | Waiting period |
|---|---|
| Pregnancy complications | 10 months |
| Congenital abnormalities | 10 months (eligible expenses within first 24 months from child's birth) |
| Living donor organ transplant | 24 months |
| Anything pre-existing or surgery before policy start | **Hard exclusion** |

[Brochure p.20]

The script:

> *"Pre-existing exclusions apply. Anything diagnosed or treated before the policy start date isn't covered. That's why we buy HSGM when you're young and healthy — to lock in coverage before anything shows up on a medical report."*

## The RNF-clean closing block

Deliver as one block right before IPOS authentication:

> *"Before we authenticate, four things to make sure I've covered:*
>
> *One — premiums are not guaranteed. AIA can re-price the portfolio. Premiums also climb with age.*
>
> *Two — only one MediSave-approved IP per person at a time. If we move forward, your existing IP (if any) auto-terminates.*
>
> *Three — MAS requires me to flag: switching from an existing health insurance policy may be detrimental. There may be early-termination penalties; the new policy may cost more or have fewer benefits.*
>
> *Four — services like Teladoc, Vitality, WhiteCoat are current value-adds, not contractual. AIA can change or withdraw them.*
>
> *Any of those four concern you?"*

If "no" — RNF audit pass. Move to Phase 8 paperwork.

If "yes" — slow down. Address it. Better 15 more minutes than a complaint.

# Part 2 — The Two-Option Close, Paperwork, Delivery, Post-Close Service Rhythm

> **The one idea for Part 2:** A clean close looks like one question — *"private or public?"* — followed by a smooth IPOS workflow while you reinforce value, walk through the four mandatory disclosures, and book the policy-anniversary review. The close is not an event; it's the natural end of a meeting that was run correctly.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver the two-option close cleanly in under 5 minutes, set up IPOS in front of the prospect with rider + Cancer Care Pro defaulted in, run the breakfast-comparison reframe naturally, and book the post-close review rhythm.

---

## The two-option close (the only close)

> *"There is really only one decision to make. Would you prefer to go private or public?"*

Both answers move forward. The rider is **assumed in** — never offer with-rider/without-rider as the choice (that fragments the decision and signals doubt).

If the prospect picks **private** → HSG Max A + AIA Max VitalHealth Pro A + Cancer Care Pro (default).

If the prospect picks **public** → HSG Max B + AIA Max VitalHealth Pro B (Cancer Care Pro is Pro-A only).

If the prospect picks **B Lite** → HSG Max B Lite + AIA Max VitalHealth Pro B Lite (this is rare; usually budget-driven, often a temporary stop-gap).

## Close while you set up IPOS

Use the time to reinforce value. The kopi-vs-cover frame:

> *"While I set up the quote, let me just summarise. S$2 million a year, guaranteed for life, 13 months before and after, claim again and again every year. Cheaper than a daily Singaporean breakfast."*

> *"How much do you think a typical kopitiam breakfast costs? S$5? S$6? Times 30 days, that's S$150–S$180 a month. Your hospital plan with rider is around S$80 a month for private — about S$2 a day, less than a kopi and toast."*

That reframe converts most price hesitations.

## Cancer Care Pro — included by default

Don't ask "do you want Cancer Care Pro?" — that fragments the decision. Default it in:

> *"Cancer Care Pro lifts your outpatient cancer drug coverage. I always include it by default on Plan A — let me know if you want to remove it, but it's what most clients keep."*

Most clients keep it. The opt-out frame is more powerful than opt-in.

## Quote one number — cash all-in

When the IPOS quote is ready:

> *"I'll pull up the IPOS quote now. The cash portion all-in is S$[X] a month — that's the rider plus the small MediSave shortfall. The base plan and the MediShield Life portion come out of MediSave automatically."*

ONE number. Don't introduce a fork between with-rider/without-rider. The without-rider version is structurally weaker.

## IPOS workflow path

Memorise the IPOS path:

```
Hospitalisation Plan
  → Hospital Plan
    → HealthShield Gold Max
      → Plan A or B (or B Lite)
        → add AIA Max VitalHealth Pro A/B/B-Lite
          → add Cancer Care Pro (Plan A only)
            → add Emergency Care Pro (optional, recommended for travel/active prospects)
```

## Two-screen setup (recommended)

If the meeting room allows:

- **Laptop:** runs IPOS quote
- **iPad:** shows brochure summary / benefit table page
- **Phone:** has AIA+ app open for any final demo (preferred-provider directory, Teladoc launch screen)

Physical demo > slides > verbal description.

## Delivery — the documents and the rhythm

At end of meeting, hand over:

- Brochure
- Product Summary
- Policy Schedule (when issued)
- Signed application acknowledgments

Then explain the timeline:

> *"You'll get the policy contract by post in about [X] working days. Please read it within the 14-day free-look period. If you change your mind during free-look, you can cancel and get the premium back."*

## The post-close service rhythm

HSGM is a 30-year asset. Set up the rhythm at delivery:

| Trigger | What to do |
|---|---|
| Day 7–14 (free-look window) | Check-in WhatsApp — "anything you want to ask before free-look closes?" |
| Day 30 | First GIRO debit confirmation |
| Day 90 | Cross-sell ask (PA, CI, spouse) + referral ask |
| Annual policy anniversary | Review meeting — Vitality status check, life event check |
| Major life event (marriage, child, job change) | Re-engagement |

Set the calendar entry **in front of the prospect.** They see you do it. Trust signal compounds.

## The benchmark (what good looks like)

A senior FC running this close discipline at full strength produces:

- **3–5 HSGM cases per week** (closed)
- **60%+ first-appointment close rate** (HSGM has the highest close rate in the AIA stack — the need is concrete)
- **~90% rider attach rate** (almost everyone takes Pro A/B with rider)
- **~80% Cancer Care Pro attach rate** on Plan A
- **2+ adjacent sells per closed case within 90 days** (PA, CI, APA, spouse)

That's the target the entire 5-day track is calibrated to.
