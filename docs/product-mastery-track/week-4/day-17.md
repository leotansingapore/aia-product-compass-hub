# Day 2 — Run the Meeting: 5 Power Questions + 5-Phase Pitch Flow

> **Today's frame:** Open every UCC conversation with discovery questions, not product. The 5 power questions surface the four structural gaps in the prospect's existing cover. The 5-phase flow then converts the gaps into a closed case in 25 minutes — with the prospect calculating their own total premium on their own calculator.

This is a merged module — Part 1 covers the 5-power-question fact-find. Part 2 covers the source's explicit 5-phase pitch flow. Estimated read + drill: ~60 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — The Fact-Find: 5 Power Questions That Surface Bundled-CI Gaps

> **The one idea for Part 1:** The questions are more powerful than the calculator. Calculators are crutches; what closes is the prospect's own answers exposing the four structural gaps. Listen for: (1) no Early CI, (2) no multi-claim, (3) CI bundled with death/savings, (4) coverage shortfall vs 5-year income-replacement need.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: deliver all 5 power questions in sequence, recognise which structural gap each "no/I-don't-know" answer reveals, deploy the 5-year recovery-period frame for income replacement, and pivot from any answer into UCC.

---

## The 5 power questions (in order)

The discipline: ask, listen, mirror back. Don't pitch UCC until you've gotten at least 3 of these answered.

### Question 1 — "Does your existing CI plan cover Early CI?"

> *"Does your whole life plan actually cover you for early CI?"* [Video 4]

If "no" → UCC covers 42 Early-Stage conditions. Gap surfaced.
If "yes" → ask which conditions and the cap. Most bundled Early CI riders cap at $50K-$100K. UCC's per-life Early/Intermediate cap is S$350K [Brochure p.14].

### Question 2 — "Is your existing plan multi-claim?"

> *"Is your policy a multi-claim? If it's not then I would suggest that you also look into this."* [Video 9]

If "no" or "I don't know" → the staircase concept lands. Gap surfaced.

### Question 3 — "If you claim once, can you claim again?"

> *"Once you claim once, can you claim again? The answer is no. If you claim once, can you claim for death? The answer is no as well. If you claim once, can you claim for savings? The answer is no."* [Video 4]

This is the question that surfaces the "bundled collapse" problem. The prospect's brain finishes the sentence themselves.

### Question 4 — "Does the CI claim affect your death cover and surrender value?"

> *"If you claim for early CI, what do you think happens to the major CI? In fact, the early CI will actually reduce the coverage for the major CI."* [Video 4]

The accelerated-CI mechanic makes bundled plans inferior — every Early CI claim shrinks the death benefit. UCC doesn't.

### Question 5 — "Can you buy another CI plan after claiming?"

After a CI claim on a bundled plan, the prospect typically becomes uninsurable for fresh CI. UCC's Ultimate Reset Benefit means you don't need a new plan — the existing one resets.

## The 5-year recovery-period frame

The default income-replacement multiple in fact-find is **5 years** (extend to 10 years for prospects with dependants):

> *"The reason and the rationale why 5 years is the norm is because most critical illnesses last for 5 years. You can't work for 5 years. And it's not just for yourself — when you can't work for that few years, you also don't want your family members to work."* [Video 9]

Concrete:
- $80,000/year income × 5 years = $400,000 CI need
- $80,000/year × 10 years (with dependants) = $800,000

If the prospect has $200,000 single-claim CI in GPP today, the gap is $200K-$600K depending on family situation. UCC layers on top.

## The calculator-is-a-crutch principle

> *"The calculators are just a crutch. The main important thing is the questioning."* [Video 4]

Use the DBR (Don't Buy Insurance Again) calculator only as visual confirmation of what the questions surfaced. Don't lead with it.

## What a complete UCC fact-find looks like

5–8 minutes max. You should walk away with:

- ✅ Existing CI plan(s) — coverage amount, plan type (single-claim or multi-claim), Early CI rider yes/no
- ✅ Annual income (the 5x or 10x anchor)
- ✅ Dependants (5-year vs 10-year frame)
- ✅ The "if you claim, what happens" answer in their own words
- ✅ At least one structural gap acknowledged

If any is missing, the close fumbles. Go back and ask.

# Part 2 — The 5-Phase Pitch Flow

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Phase 1 Opening → Phase 2 The Pitch (one picture) → Phase 3 Time Value of Money (optional, for restructure) → Phase 4 Riders → Phase 5 Case Studies / Close. Always make the prospect calculate total premium themselves on their own calculator. Hand them the choice; no matter which one they choose is still a purchase.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to: name all 5 phases, deliver Phase 2 in under 5 minutes, sequence the riders correctly (Enhancer default, ECPWP after term), and close on the prospect's choice of term.

---

## The 5 phases

```
Phase 1     Phase 2          Phase 3 (opt.)  Phase 4         Phase 5
Open    →   Pitch (picture) → TVM         →  Riders      →   Case Studies / Close
            (5 min)          (restructure)   (sequenced)
```

| Phase | Time | Micro-commit |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Open with 5 power questions | 5–8 min | At least 3 gaps surfaced |
| 2. Pitch — the staircase visual | 5 min | "Yes, I can claim again" |
| 3. TVM math (only if restructuring existing CI) | 4 min | "I see the savings" |
| 4. Term decision + riders sequenced | 5 min | Term picked, Enhancer in, ECPWP added |
| 5. Case studies + close | 4 min | Sign |
| **Total** | **~25 min** | **Term picked, riders sequenced** |

## Phase 2 — The Pitch (the staircase + 3-option close)

After the staircase visual (Day 1 Part 2), drive to the pricing question:

> *"Mr. Client, what do you think affects the cost of this plan?"* [Video 5]

Let them list factors. Confirm: age, coverage amount, coverage duration. Then present three terms:

| Term | Sample annual premium (25-yo female, S$200K cover) | Total premium |
|---|---|---|
| To Age 65 | ~S$1,400 | ~S$56,000–70,000 over 40–50 yrs [Video 5/9] |
| To Age 75 | ~S$2,328 | ~S$116,000 over 50 yrs [Video 5] |
| To Age 85 | ~S$3,320 | ~S$199,000 over 60 yrs [Video 5] |

The premium loading: To 75 ~+40% over To 65. To 85 ~+100% (double). [Video 1, Video 3]

Then make the prospect own the math:

> *"Can you take out your calculator and calculate with me how much you'll be paying in total?"* [Video 5]

Ownership of the number drives ownership of the decision.

## Phase 2 — The risk-reward reframe

Don't frame the term decision as "more expensive" vs "cheaper." Frame as risk:

> *"It's more expensive because it's riskier for the company — they know you'll most likely claim something before 85. But it's safer for you."* [Video 5]

Then the choice question:

> *"Would you prefer something that's safer at S$3,320 a year, or something that's riskier at S$1,400 a year?"* [Video 5]

The closing principle:

> *"Always try to give clients a choice — when you give them a choice, no matter which one they choose is still a purchase."* [Video 9]

## Phase 4 — Rider sequencing

Order matters. Two rules:

### Rule 1: Enhancer is selected by default

> *"By default I will always select the Enhancer."* [Video 7]

The Enhancer adds same-illness recurrence cover. Don't ask the prospect to opt in — default it in. Mention it at the close, never the opening.

### Rule 2: Hold ECPWP (Premium Waiver) until AFTER the term decision

> *"This rider — share this rider at the end, so that you can stack your value."* [Video 7]

If you mention Premium Waiver in Phase 2, you've fragmented the term decision. Wait until Phase 4.

The Premium Waiver math (Day 5 Part 2 covers fully):

| Scenario | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Best case (claim year 2 of 50-yr term) | S$2,328 × 49 = S$114,072 of premiums waived against ~S$168/yr cost = **~679x return** [Video 7] |
| Worst case (no claim ever) | ~S$8,400 paid over 50 yrs [Video 7] |

That's the asymmetric risk-reward close on the Premium Waiver.
