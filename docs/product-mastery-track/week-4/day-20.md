# Day 5 — Compliance + Close: 6 Compliance Pillars + Term-Then-Rider Close

> **Today's frame:** State the 6 compliance pillars in your own voice, in plain English, before the prospect signs. Then close on the prospect's choice of term, sequence the Enhancer (default) and ECPWP (after term), and hand off Teladoc/WhiteCoat/Vitality at delivery. The prospect who signs is also the prospect who refers.

This is the final module. Part 1 covers the 6 compliance pillars. Part 2 covers the term-then-rider close, Premium Waiver framing, AIA Vitality, post-close handoff. Estimated read + drill: ~50 minutes.

---

# Part 1 — Compliance + Risk Disclosures (6 Pillars)

> **The one idea for Part 1:** Six pillars, said out loud and acknowledged, separate a clean book from an MAS complaint. The LIA 2019 standard definitions cover the 37 severe-stage conditions; the early/intermediate definitions are AIA-determined — flag this so the prospect knows where to read more.

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to deliver all 6 disclosures cleanly in under 90 seconds total, recognise when UCC is the wrong product, and pass an RNF sample audit.

---

## The 6 compliance pillars

### Pillar 1 — 14-day free-look period

> *"You have 14 days from receipt of the policy documents to free-look. If you change your mind, you can cancel and get the premium back."* [PS p.8]

Trigger windows:
- E-policy: from date of SMS/email
- Mailed: 7 days after posting
- Hand-delivered: 7 days from courier handover

### Pillar 2 — 7-day survival period

> *"Survival period — you must survive 7 days from the date of diagnosis or surgical procedure for any claim to be payable."* [PS p.3, Enhancer p.1]

This applies to all stages, all claims.

### Pillar 3 — 90-day waiting period (cardiac and cancer)

> *"There's a 90-day waiting period for the named cardiac and cancer conditions — heart attack, major cancer, coronary artery bypass, angioplasty, and other serious coronary artery disease — measured from the policy issue or reinstatement date."* [PS p.7]

Plus for Catastrophic CI Benefit under the Enhancer: 90-day waiting for Heart Attack (Catastrophic) and Major Cancer (Catastrophic), measured from the later of issue/reinstatement of the basic policy or the supplementary benefit. [Enhancer p.7]

### Pillar 4 — 2-year Enhancer relapse waiting

> *"The Enhancer's relapse benefit requires 2 years between claims for the same illness. So if you claim for cancer this year, the next cancer claim under the relapse benefit can only be paid 2 years later."* [Enhancer p.6]

This applies to all 5 Ultimate Relapse Critical Illness Conditions.

### Pillar 5 — Pre-existing exclusion

> *"Pre-existing conditions are excluded unless declared and specifically accepted at underwriting. Suicide within 1 year of issue or reinstatement returns premiums only — no benefit payable."* [PS p.10-11]

The brochure phrasing:

> *"There are exclusions such as pre-existing conditions, whereby no benefits will be payable. Waiting period and survival period are applicable before the benefits will be payable."* [Brochure p.14]

### Pillar 6 — Non-guaranteed premium + zero/low surrender value

Two parts:

**Premium not guaranteed:**

> *"Premiums payable for your policy are not guaranteed. These rates may be adjusted based on future experience or to meet our obligations under your policy. But the adjustment is made across the portfolio, not on an individual basis — your premium doesn't go up because YOU claimed."* [PS p.9]

**No cash value / zero surrender value:**

> *"As buying a life insurance policy is a long-term commitment, an early termination of the policy usually involves high costs and the surrender value, if any, that is payable to you may be zero or less than the total premiums paid."* [PS p.12]

UCC is non-participating, no cash value. Early termination = lose all premiums paid.

## The full RNF-clean closing block

Deliver as one block right before signature:

> *"Before we authenticate, six things to make sure I've covered:*
>
> *One — 14-day free-look from receipt. Cancel within that window, you get the premium back.*
>
> *Two — 7-day survival period. You must survive 7 days from diagnosis or surgery for the claim to be payable.*
>
> *Three — 90-day waiting on the named cardiac and cancer conditions, from policy issue.*
>
> *Four — 2-year wait between Enhancer relapse claims for the same illness.*
>
> *Five — pre-existing conditions are excluded unless declared and accepted at underwriting. Suicide in year 1 returns premiums only.*
>
> *Six — premiums are not guaranteed. UCC has no cash value, so early termination means losing what you've paid.*
>
> *Any of those six concern you?"*

If "no" — RNF audit pass. Move to signature.

If "yes" — slow down. Address it. Better 15 more minutes than a complaint.

## The "anti-market" disclosure

> *"This plan isn't right for everyone. The anti-market for UCC is people who:*
>
> *- Want cash value or surrender value (UCC has none)*
> *- Need death cover as the primary purpose (UCC's death benefit is only 5% non-accidental)*
> *- Aren't sure they can hold the premium for the full term*
>
> *If any of those describe you, UCC isn't the right plan. Be honest with me."*

## Coverage caps and aggregate limits to disclose

| Limit | Value |
|---|---|
| CI per-life across all AIA CI policies | S$3,000,000 [PS p.11] |
| Accidental Death Benefit aggregate | S$5,000,000 across all prior AIA policies [PS p.1-2] |
| Catastrophic CI Benefit | One-time 100% top-up, doesn't reduce base [Enhancer p.7-8] |

If the prospect is asking for more cover than the aggregate limit allows, refer to multi-insurer top-up strategy.

## LIA 2019 standard definitions disclosure

> *"The 37 severe-stage critical illness conditions marked in your policy follow the LIA Standard Definitions Version 2019 — that's the industry-standard wording. The early-stage and intermediate-stage definitions are AIA-determined, which means they may differ from a competitor's plan. Read both carefully — I'll point out the key ones."* [PS p.7]

Important disclosure for cross-shopping prospects.

## Other compliance items

| Item | Detail |
|---|---|
| SDIC protection | Up to specified limits [Brochure p.14, PS p.12] |
| Underwriter | AIA Singapore Private Limited |

# Part 2 — The Term-Then-Rider Close, AIA Vitality, Post-Close Handoff

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Close on the term, then sequence riders. Enhancer is default. ECPWP comes after the term decision with the asymmetric ROI framing (best case ~679x, worst case ~S$8,400). Hand off Teladoc, WhiteCoat, AIA Vitality before the prospect leaves the meeting.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver the term-then-rider close cleanly in under 5 minutes, sequence the Enhancer + ECPWP correctly (never reverse), frame the Premium Waiver as asymmetric risk-reward, and book the post-close service rhythm.

---

## The term-then-rider close

The sequence:
1. Confirm term (To 65 / 75 / 85)
2. Enhancer is in by default
3. THEN present ECPWP with the ROI framing
4. THEN AIA Vitality
5. Sign

Don't reverse. If you mention ECPWP before the term is locked, you fragment the decision.

The choice principle from Video 9:

> *"Always try to give clients a choice — when you give them a choice, no matter which one they choose is still a purchase."*

## ECPWP — the asymmetric risk-reward close

Frame the rider as a one-way bet:

> *"We have something called a waiver of premium rider. The moment you claim once, your plan becomes completely free for the rest of the coverage period.*
>
> *The best case is you paid S$168 and you received S$114,000 worth of free insurance premiums.*
>
> *The worst case is you pay an extra S$8,400 over your lifetime and never use it."*

[Video 7 — verbatim]

Sample math (25-yo, S$200,000 cover, To Age 75): +S$168/year (~S$15/month). At ~S$2,328/year base premium, claim in year 2 = S$2,328 × 49 = **S$114,072 waived**. That's a ~679× return on the rider in best case.

Most prospects take the Premium Waiver. Default it as recommended.

## AIA Vitality at the close

> *"Add AIA Vitality to AIA Ultimate Critical Cover and get a 10% premium discount for the plan and UCC Enhancer for the first year. Keep up a healthy lifestyle and get up to 15% premium discount as you improve your AIA Vitality status."* [Brochure p.8]

The annual adjustment by status:

| Status | Annual adjustment |
|---|---|
| Bronze | +2% |
| Silver | +1% |
| Gold | -1% |
| Platinum | -2% |

Range: minimum cumulative 85%, maximum 100% [PS p.9].

For health-conscious prospects, this is a real reason to enrol Vitality — set up at delivery.

## Other riders to mention if relevant

| Rider | Coverage |
|---|---|
| Critical Protector Waiver of Premium (II) | 72 major-stage CIs [Brochure p.5] |
| Early Critical Protector Waiver of Premium (II) | 149 early/intermediate/major CIs [Brochure p.5] |
| Payor Benefit / Early Critical Protector Payor Benefit (II) | For child policies — waives child's premium until child's 25th birthday or end of waiver rider term, whichever earlier |

For a prospect buying UCC for a child, Payor Benefit is essentially mandatory.

## The Teladoc + WhiteCoat handoff at delivery

These are post-sale value-adds — set them up at delivery, not at the close (would dilute the term decision):

### Teladoc Personal Case Management

> *"You have access to Teladoc Health's Personal Case Management service — a dedicated physician case manager and medical advice from a panel of over 50,000 leading global specialists. Daily service from 0900 to 2100 hrs."* [Brochure p.6]

This is a real benefit for any future medical decision — second opinions on cancer treatment, surgical options, etc.

### WhiteCoat Telemedicine + Chronic Home Care

| Service | Detail |
|---|---|
| WhiteCoat Telemedicine | (per Brochure p.7) |
| WhiteCoat Chronic Home Care | From S$30/month, 12-month enrolment, paid upfront [Brochure p.7] |

For older prospects or those with chronic condition risk, the Chronic Home Care subscription is worth flagging at delivery.

## Application paperwork

The order at signature:

1. ✅ Full medical disclosure (pre-existing exclusion is the single largest claim-decline reason)
2. ✅ Beneficiary nomination
3. ✅ GIRO / recurring premium setup
4. ✅ Policy contract issued
5. ✅ 14-day free-look starts on receipt

The script:

> *"Full medical disclosure now. If we miss anything pre-existing here, it becomes a claim-decline reason later. Better to over-disclose now than under-disclose."*

## Post-close service rhythm

UCC is a 30-50 year asset. Set up the rhythm at delivery:

| Trigger | What to do |
|---|---|
| Day 7-14 (free-look window) | Check-in WhatsApp |
| Day 30 | First GIRO debit confirmation |
| Day 90 | Cross-sell ask (GPP if missing, APA if cashflow freed, spouse if married) |
| Annual policy anniversary | Vitality status check, rider review |
| Major CI claim | Teladoc activation reminder, Catastrophic eligibility check |
| Major life event (marriage, child, job change) | Coverage review |

Set the calendar entry **in front of the prospect.** They see you do it. Trust signal compounds.

## The benchmark (what good looks like)

A senior FC running this discipline produces:

- **2-3 UCC cases per week** (typically as layered upsells to GPP holders)
- **50%+ first-appointment close rate** (UCC is more "second appointment" than HSGM)
- **~80% Enhancer attach rate** (default-in framing)
- **~50% ECPWP attach rate** (asymmetric ROI is convincing)
- **~30% Vitality enrolment** at delivery

That's the target.

## Quiz
1. **How long is the UCC free-look period, and from when does it run for an e-policy?**
- A) 7 days from purchase
- B) 14 days from receipt of policy documents (from date of SMS/email for e-policy) ✓
- C) 30 days from issue
- D) 21 days from cover-start date

**Why:** The free-look period is 14 days from receipt of policy documents; for e-policy it runs from the date of the SMS/email notification [PS p.8].

2. **The Enhancer relapse benefit requires what waiting period between claims for the same illness?**
- A) 6 months
- B) 1 year
- C) 2 years ✓
- D) 5 years

**Why:** The Enhancer's Ultimate Relapse CI Benefit requires a 2-year waiting period between same-illness claims and applies to all 5 named relapse conditions [Enhancer p.6].

3. **The 90-day waiting period under base UCC applies to which group of conditions?**
- A) All 150 conditions
- B) All cancer conditions only
- C) Heart Attack of Specified Severity, Major Cancer, Coronary Artery By-pass Surgery, Angioplasty, and Other Serious Coronary Artery Disease ✓
- D) All early-stage conditions

**Why:** The 90-day waiting period (measured from policy issue or reinstatement) applies specifically to those five named cardiac and cancer conditions [PS p.7].

4. **Which of the following is one of the 6 compliance pillars stated before signing?**
- A) Premiums are guaranteed for life
- B) Pre-existing conditions are excluded unless declared and specifically accepted at underwriting; suicide within 1 year of issue or reinstatement returns premiums only ✓
- C) UCC includes a guaranteed cash value at age 65
- D) The policy automatically converts to whole life at age 75

**Why:** Pillar 5 covers the pre-existing exclusion plus the suicide-within-1-year clause that returns premiums only with no benefit payable [PS p.10-11].

5. **Which AIA Vitality discount applies on UCC and the UCC Enhancer in the first year?**
- A) 5%
- B) 10% upfront discount, with future discounts up to 15% based on Vitality status ✓
- C) 25% flat
- D) 0%; Vitality only applies to APA

**Why:** AIA Vitality grants a 10% upfront discount on UCC and the UCC Enhancer for the first year, with future discounts up to 15% as the prospect's Vitality status improves [Brochure p.8, PS p.9].

6. **The annual Vitality status adjustment applies how, with what cumulative range?**
- A) Bronze +5% / Silver +3% / Gold -3% / Platinum -5%, range 50%–100%
- B) Bronze +2% / Silver +1% / Gold -1% / Platinum -2%, minimum cumulative 85%, maximum 100% ✓
- C) Bronze +10% / Silver +5% / Gold -5% / Platinum -10%, no range
- D) Status has no effect on premium

**Why:** Annual Vitality adjustments are Bronze +2% / Silver +1% / Gold -1% / Platinum -2%, with a minimum cumulative 85% and maximum 100% [PS p.9].

7. **What is the asymmetric ECPWP framing for a 25-yo, S$200K cover, To Age 75 case?**
- A) Best case: S$168 paid → S$114,072 of premiums waived (~679x); worst case: ~S$8,400 paid over 50 years and never used ✓
- B) Best case: S$1,000 paid → S$10,000 waived (~10x); worst case: S$50,000 paid
- C) Best case: zero return; worst case: zero loss
- D) Best case: 100% premium refund regardless of claim

**Why:** Video 7's verbatim framing puts ECPWP best case at ~679x return (S$2,328 × 49 = S$114,072 waived) versus a worst case of ~S$8,400 paid across the 50-year term and never used.

8. **A prospect asks "Will my premium go up because I claimed?" What is the correct compliance disclosure?**
- A) "Yes, claimants get individual premium loadings."
- B) "Premiums are not guaranteed and may be adjusted, but the adjustment is made across the portfolio, not on an individual basis — your premium does not go up because YOU claimed." ✓
- C) "Premiums are guaranteed for life — they will never change."
- D) "I cannot answer that question."

**Why:** Pillar 6 disclosure clarifies premiums are non-guaranteed and may be adjusted, but adjustments are portfolio-wide rather than triggered by an individual's claim [PS p.9].

9. **A prospect's primary need is death cover and they want surrender value. What is the correct anti-market disclosure?**
- A) Sell them UCC anyway and explain it later
- B) "UCC has no cash value, and the death benefit is only 5% non-accidental — if death cover is your primary need, UCC is not the right plan; let's look at GPP, Term, or PLP instead." ✓
- C) "Buy UCC and cancel after the free-look period."
- D) Tell them the death benefit is 100%.

**Why:** The anti-market disclosure tells the prospect honestly when UCC is wrong for them — including primary death-cover needs, where UCC's 5% non-accidental death benefit makes it unsuitable.

10. **The 37 severe-stage critical illness conditions on UCC follow which definitional standard, and how should the FC frame this for a cross-shopping prospect?**
- A) AIA-determined for all 150 conditions, no industry standard
- B) The 37 severe-stage conditions follow LIA Standard Definitions Version 2019 (industry-standard wording); the early- and intermediate-stage definitions are AIA-determined and may differ from competitors — read both carefully ✓
- C) MAS-issued definitions for all conditions
- D) WHO definitions applied across all stages

**Why:** The PS clarifies that the 37 severe-stage conditions use LIA Standard Definitions 2019 while early- and intermediate-stage definitions are AIA-determined; this matters when a prospect cross-shops [PS p.7].
