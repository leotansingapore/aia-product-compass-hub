# Part 1 — The Numbers: Single-Claim Mechanics, Caps, Enhancer Economics

> **The one idea for Part 1:** "Unlimited" means unlimited DIFFERENT major-stage CIs across the policy life — NOT unlimited claims for the same illness. Same-illness recurrence requires the Enhancer (5 named relapse conditions, 50% per claim, 100% total cap) plus a one-time Catastrophic CI Benefit (100% top-up that doesn't reduce base cover).

By the end of Part 1 you should be able to: state every key number from memory, draw the Enhancer's relapse + catastrophic mechanics on a whiteboard, recite the per-episode caps without notes, and walk through the Premium Waiver ROI math.

---

## Base UCC — single-claim per illness

Critical foundation: only one claim per critical illness stage per illness. Total payouts across all stages of one illness cannot exceed 100% of coverage amount [PS p.3-4].

> *"UCC's unlimited payout does not mean unlimited claims for the same illness. The reset is not recurring for the same condition."* [Video 2]

This is why the Enhancer matters:

> *"The most common CIs — cancer, heart attack — often recur. But the base plan only covers once. That's why the Enhancer matters."* [Video 2]

> *"For just $7 more a month, you get coverage if cancer or heart attack comes back."* [Video 2]

## Per-episode caps (memorise)

| Stage | Cap |
|---|---|
| Early Stage per episode | S$350,000 per life across all UCC policies (same insured, same CI) [Brochure p.14] |
| Intermediate Stage per episode | S$350,000 per life [Brochure p.14] |
| Combined Early + Intermediate (across all stages) | 500% of coverage amount in aggregate [PS p.4] |
| Major Stage — Angioplasty & Other Invasive Treatment for Coronary Artery | 10% of Current Insured Amount, **capped at S$25,000** [PS p.3] |
| Survival period (all stages) | 7 days from diagnosis or surgery [PS p.3] |
| 90-day waiting period (cardiac + cancer) | Heart Attack of Specified Severity, Major Cancer, Coronary Artery By-pass Surgery, Angioplasty, Other Serious Coronary Artery Disease [PS p.7] |
| Death Benefit (non-accidental) | 5% of Insured Amount [PS p.1-2] |
| Accidental Death Benefit | 100% of Insured Amount, capped at S$5,000,000 aggregate [PS p.1-2] |
| CI per-life limit (across all AIA CI policies) | S$3,000,000 [PS p.11] |

## The Enhancer — what it adds

### Ultimate Relapse Critical Illness Benefit

Pays the lower of **50% of coverage amount or current insured amount** per relapse. Total Relapse payouts cannot exceed **100% of base coverage** [Enhancer p.1-2].

5 named conditions:
1. Re-diagnosed Major Cancer
2. Recurred Heart Attack
3. Recurred Stroke
4. Repeated Major Organ / Bone Marrow Transplantation
5. Repeated Heart Valve Surgery

[Enhancer p.2, Brochure p.4]

**Relapse waiting period:** 2 years from prior admitted claim of the same illness [Enhancer p.6].

### Catastrophic CI Benefit — the asymmetric add-on

> *"The Catastrophic CI Benefit pays an additional 100% of your coverage amount, regardless of any claims made previously, and this payout does not reduce the coverage amount of your AIA Ultimate Critical Cover."* [Brochure p.4]

5 Catastrophic conditions:
1. Major Cancer (Catastrophic — Stage IV with distant metastasis)
2. Heart Attack (Catastrophic — ejection fraction persistently <30%, NYHA Class IV)
3. Major Organ Transplant (heart and lung simultaneously)
4. Stroke (Catastrophic — permanent loss of 3 of 6 ADLs)
5. Paralysis (Catastrophic — total irreversible loss of all four limbs ≥6 weeks)

[Enhancer p.7-8, Brochure p.4]

This is the **one-time 100% top-up** — pays on first eligible diagnosis, doesn't reduce base coverage.

## ECPWP (Early Critical Protector Waiver of Premium) economics

Sample: 25-yo, S$200,000 cover, To Age 75 [Video 7]:

| Scenario | Numbers |
|---|---|
| Base UCC alone | ~S$2,328/year |
| With ECPWP rider | ~S$2,496/year |
| Rider cost | ~S$168/year (~S$15/month) |
| **Best case** (claim year 2 of 49 remaining) | S$2,328 × 49 = **S$114,072 waived** = ~679× return |
| **Worst case** (no claim, 50 years paid) | **~S$8,400 paid** without claim |

The framing:

> *"We have something called a waiver of premium rider. The moment you claim once, your plan becomes completely free for the rest of the coverage period. The best case is you paid $168 and you received $114,000 worth of free insurance premiums. The worst case is you pay an extra $8,400 over your lifetime and never use it."* [Video 7]

Asymmetric risk-reward.

# Part 2 — Top 5 Client Questions: Crisp, Scripted Answers

> **The one idea for Part 2:** Five questions cover ~80% of what comes up in a real meeting. Each hinges on the small print of "unlimited." Memorise the exact mechanic — the prospect's fluency test is asking the same question 3 different ways.

By the end of Part 2 you should be able to deliver each answer in under 30 seconds with a closing question.

---

## Q1 — "Is UCC really unlimited?"

**A:** Unlimited for major-stage claims across **different** critical illnesses, with a 12-month reset between claims. Same-illness recurrence requires the Enhancer rider, which pays the lower of 50% of cover or current insured amount per relapse, capped at 100% of coverage in total.

[Sources: PS p.3, Enhancer p.1-2, Video 2]

## Q2 — "What does the 100% reset actually mean?"

**A:** Quoting the Product Summary directly:

> *"If your basic policy is in force 12 months after the date of the latest diagnosed critical illness, you will enjoy the benefit of having the Current Insured Amount restored to 100%. There is no cap on the number of times the Ultimate Reset Benefit is granted, as long as your basic policy is in force."* [PS p.7]

## Q3 — "What happens to my Early CI cover if I claim?"

**A:** Total Early + Intermediate payouts across all UCC policies covering the same insured cannot exceed S$350,000 per life for the same CI. Combined Early + Intermediate stage payouts cannot exceed 500% of coverage amount in aggregate. Each stage of a critical illness can only be claimed once.

[Sources: PS p.4, Brochure p.14]

## Q4 — "What's the survival period and waiting period?"

**A:** Three waiting periods to know:

| Period | Duration | When |
|---|---|---|
| Survival period | 7 days | From diagnosis or surgery date |
| Waiting period (cardiac + cancer) | 90 days | From issue/reinstatement |
| Relapse waiting (Enhancer) | 2 years | From prior admitted claim of same illness |

The 90-day waiting applies specifically to: Heart Attack of Specified Severity, Major Cancer, Coronary Artery By-pass Surgery, Angioplasty and Other Invasive Treatment for Coronary Artery, Other Serious Coronary Artery Disease.

[Sources: PS p.3, p.7, Enhancer p.6]

## Q5 — "What if I die — does my family get anything?"

**A:** Yes, but UCC is pure-CI. Death benefits are limited:

| Type | Payout |
|---|---|
| Non-accidental death | 5% of Insured Amount |
| Accidental Death | 100% of Insured Amount (capped at S$5M aggregate across all prior AIA policies on same insured) |

The policy terminates upon either payout.

For full death cover, the prospect needs GPP / Term / PLP — UCC is the CI layer, not the death layer.

[Sources: PS p.1-2, Brochure p.3]

## Bonus question — "Will Vitality help?"

**A:** Yes — AIA Vitality offers an immediate **10% premium discount** on UCC and the UCC Enhancer for the first year, with future discounts up to **15%** based on Vitality status. Annual adjustment by Bronze (+2%) / Silver (+1%) / Gold (-1%) / Platinum (-2%); minimum cumulative 85%, maximum 100%.

[Sources: PS p.9, Brochure p.8]

> *"Add AIA Vitality to AIA Ultimate Critical Cover and get a 10% premium discount for the plan and UCC Enhancer for the first year. Keep up a healthy lifestyle and get up to 15% premium discount as you improve your AIA Vitality status."* [Brochure p.8]

## Quiz
1. **What does "unlimited" actually mean on the base UCC plan?**
- A) Unlimited claims for the same illness
- B) Unlimited major-stage claims across DIFFERENT critical illnesses, with a 12-month reset between claims ✓
- C) Unlimited claims at any stage with no waiting period
- D) Unlimited claims for cancer only

**Why:** Base UCC pays unlimited major-stage claims across different CIs with a 12-month reset; same-illness recurrence requires the Enhancer rider [PS p.3, Video 2].

2. **What is the Catastrophic CI Benefit?**
- A) A 50% top-up payable on any second cancer diagnosis
- B) A one-time additional 100% of coverage amount that does NOT reduce the base UCC coverage ✓
- C) An automatic premium waiver for any catastrophic event
- D) A monthly income benefit during recovery

**Why:** The Catastrophic CI Benefit pays an additional 100% of coverage amount one-time, regardless of prior claims, and does not reduce the base UCC Insured Amount [Brochure p.4, Enhancer p.7-8].

3. **The Enhancer's Ultimate Relapse CI Benefit covers how many named conditions, and what is the cap structure?**
- A) 3 conditions, 100% per relapse
- B) 5 conditions, lower of 50% of cover or current insured amount per relapse, capped at 100% of base coverage in total ✓
- C) 10 conditions, 25% per relapse, no total cap
- D) 5 conditions, 100% per relapse, no total cap

**Why:** The Enhancer covers 5 named relapse conditions paying the lower of 50% of cover or current insured amount per relapse, with a total cap of 100% of base coverage [Enhancer p.1-2].

4. **The CI per-life limit aggregated across all AIA CI policies on the same insured is:**
- A) S$1,000,000
- B) S$2,000,000
- C) S$3,000,000 ✓
- D) S$5,000,000

**Why:** AIA's aggregate CI per-life limit across all of their CI policies on the same insured is S$3,000,000 [PS p.11].

5. **For a 25-yo, S$200,000 cover, To Age 75 profile, the ECPWP rider costs roughly how much per year and produces what best-case waiver?**
- A) ~S$50/year, ~S$10,000 waived best case
- B) ~S$168/year, ~S$114,072 waived best case (~679x return) ✓
- C) ~S$500/year, ~S$200,000 waived best case
- D) ~S$1,000/year, ~S$50,000 waived best case

**Why:** ECPWP costs ~S$168/year (~S$15/month); claiming in year 2 of the 50-year term waives 49 × S$2,328 = ~S$114,072, roughly a 679x return on the rider [Video 7].

6. **What is the worst-case ECPWP outcome (no claim ever made) over a 50-year term?**
- A) Premium refunded in full
- B) ~S$8,400 paid in rider premiums and never used ✓
- C) ~S$50,000 paid in rider premiums
- D) Rider premiums increase year-over-year

**Why:** With no claim across the 50 years, the prospect pays approximately S$8,400 in rider premiums and never uses the waiver — the trade-off in the asymmetric risk-reward framing [Video 7].

7. **Which of the following is the correct UCC death-benefit structure?**
- A) 100% on all deaths, capped at S$3M
- B) 50% non-accidental, 50% accidental
- C) 5% of Insured Amount for non-accidental death; 100% for accidental death (capped at S$5M aggregate across all prior AIA policies on same insured) ✓
- D) Death benefit is excluded entirely

**Why:** UCC pays 5% of Insured Amount on non-accidental death and 100% on accidental death, with the accidental payout capped at S$5,000,000 aggregate across all prior AIA policies on the same insured [PS p.1-2].

8. **A prospect asks: "Is UCC really unlimited?" What is the correct crisp answer?**
- A) "Yes, completely unlimited for everything including same-illness."
- B) "Unlimited for major-stage claims across different CIs with a 12-month reset; same-illness recurrence requires the Enhancer, which pays 50% per relapse capped at 100% of cover total." ✓
- C) "No, capped at three claims total."
- D) "Only unlimited for cancer."

**Why:** This is the canonical Q1 scripted answer, distinguishing "different illness" unlimited claims (base) from "same illness" recurrence (Enhancer) [PS p.3, Enhancer p.1-2, Video 2].

9. **A prospect asks: "What's the survival period?" Which response is fully correct?**
- A) 7 days from diagnosis or surgery date, with separate 90-day waiting on cardiac/cancer from policy issue, plus 2-year Enhancer relapse waiting ✓
- B) 14 days from diagnosis
- C) 30 days from diagnosis with no other waiting periods
- D) Survival period only applies to major-stage claims

**Why:** Survival period is 7 days from diagnosis or surgery; 90-day waiting on the named cardiac and cancer conditions runs from issue/reinstatement; the Enhancer's relapse benefit requires 2 years between same-illness claims [PS p.3, p.7, Enhancer p.6].

10. **Aileen, 35, asks: "If I get diagnosed with major cancer at year 1.5 of my UCC policy, will I be paid?" Based on the waiting period rules, what should the FC say?**
- A) "No claim possible — the 90-day waiting period excludes all cancer claims for the first year."
- B) "Yes, the 90-day waiting on Major Cancer is from policy issue, so 1.5 years in is well past it; you'd need to satisfy the 7-day survival period from diagnosis." ✓
- C) "Major cancer is excluded under UCC."
- D) "You must wait 2 years from issue to claim cancer."

**Why:** The 90-day waiting on Major Cancer applies from issue or reinstatement, so a year-1.5 diagnosis is past the waiting period; the only remaining requirement is the 7-day survival period from diagnosis [PS p.3, p.7].
