## 3. How the FLEXI + Income Guarantee changes the math

Combine FLEXI (up to $4,200/month during exam prep) + Income Guarantee (up to $4,000/month after licensing, full-time).

That means:

- **During exam period** (typically 2-4 months): you're earning FLEXI pay

Compare that to starting a traditional business where you'd be burning capital for 3-5 years before break-even. The math isn't even close.

## Q1. What's the difference between this career and a 9-to-5?

Four real differences:

- **Income:** 9-to-5 is capped regardless of hours. FA is variable and scales with output. Upside uncapped.
- **Time:** 9-to-5 is schedule-imposed. FA is self-scheduled.
- **Role:** Most corporate jobs are *cost centres*. FA is a *profit centre* — you own the economic output you create.
- **Security:** Corporate security is the employer's call. FA security comes from renewals, diversified book, and financial independence — none of which anyone can take from you.

Neither is inherently better. Depends on what you want.

---

## Q2. How is this like a franchise — but with less risk?

Real franchise (Ya Kun, Subway):
- **$200K+ upfront**
- **10% royalty on revenue forever**
- **3+ years to break-even**

FA career:
- **$0 upfront**
- **No royalty**
- **Cash-flow positive from month one**

The company invests in *you* — sponsored exams, FLEXI allowance, subsidised leads — not the other way around. Risk structure is reversed.

## Q3. Why does this scale better than a service business?

Service business (interior design, marketing agency) has a delivery ceiling. More sales = more post-sale work = eventually the business breaks.

FA has zero post-sale delivery on your side. Once a policy is sold: underwriters process, fund managers grow, claims team pays, admin handles paperwork. You keep selling without delivery load growing. An FA with 1,000 clients runs with 1–2 admin helpers. A marketing agency at similar revenue runs 40.

## Q4. What are the five ideal-career traits FA offers?

1. **Income** — scalable, uncapped
2. **Flexibility** — self-scheduled
3. **Fulfilment** — real impact on real lives
4. **Autonomy** — self-employed professional
5. **Security** — renewal income compounds, doesn't depend on one employer

Most careers deliver 1–2. FA structurally delivers all five. Rare.

## Q5. How can introverts succeed?

Much of top-advisor performance is built on listening, not talking. I'm an introvert who stammered as a kid. I did Toastmasters during NS to work on speaking. Five-time MDRT, three-time COT. Introverts are structurally good at:

- Ask more questions before speaking
- Comfortable with silence (when clients say the truth)
- Don't over-sell
- Build trust through depth, not charisma

Training system provides scripts and frameworks — so you don't need to "wing it" from extroversion. See [[day-11|Day 11]] for full answer.

## Q7. How does income grow from renewals and back-end?

Three passive-income layers stack:

1. **Renewals** — commissions annually as clients keep paying premiums. By year 5+, steady book's renewals match or exceed new-business commission.
2. **Career Benefit** — AIA bonus that eventually matches renewal commission 1:1, doubling your renewal stream vs competitors.
3. **Accumulated Premium Fund (APF)** — starts paying from year 7. 1–1.5% of monthly premiums from policies closed 7 years prior. Compounds.

Even if you stopped selling entirely in year 10, existing book continues to pay materially. See Day 6 for my own passive-income progression: $50K Y1 → $101K Y6.

## Q14. What scripts and tools are provided for objections?

Key frameworks:

- **ART Framework:** Acknowledge → Relate → Throw it back. Turns objections into conversations.
- **Pre-framing:** Address common objections *before* client raises them — eliminates up to 90% of them.
- **"Ask for a no":** Instead of *"are you open to meeting?"* (invites *"let me think"*), use *"would you be against me sharing this?"* Natural *no* moves things forward.
- **Ghosting handling:** Create safe space, acknowledge delay, offer low-pressure path, accept *no* cleanly.
- **Positive vocabulary:** *"questions"* not *"concerns"*; *"explore"* not *"try"* — negative framing prompts negative emotions.

Plus: integrated concept-presentation refined over 7 years, LinkedIn outbound systems, referral scripts, flowcharts, iSmart CRM.

| Metric | Monthly | Weekly | Daily |

| Cases closed | 5 | 1.25 | — |

| Prospecting/calling hours | 50 | 12 | 1.5–2 |

About **18 hours real work per week** with efficient systems. With stronger systems, closer to half the traditional "15 appointments a week" benchmark while still hitting $10K/month.

Numbers scale proportionally — for $10K FYC/month, roughly double the activity.
