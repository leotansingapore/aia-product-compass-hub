# Assignment 6 — Create Your Vision Board

> **What you're producing:** A one-page vision board — a visual artefact of what your life looks like if this career works the way you want it to. Not decoration; a decision-making anchor for the hard weeks.

## Why this matters

Pledge sheets are contracts. Business plans are maps. Vision boards are **fuel**. They're what you look at at 10:47pm on a Wednesday when you don't feel like making the last three dials, and the image reminds you why the dials exist.

New FCs who skip this one tend to drift in month 3 — the month where novelty fades and grind compounds. A specific, visual picture of the life you're building protects you from generic *"I'll quit and find something more meaningful"* thinking, because the vision *is* the meaning.

## The walkthrough

Watch [Leo's vision board Loom](https://www.loom.com/share/3dbda27b81f24a089e766702348c3076) before you start. It shows the format and the traps to avoid.

## The template

Pick any Canva vision-board template and edit it for yourself: **[Canva vision-board templates](https://www.canva.com/templates/s/vision-board/)**.

If you'd rather build your own in a different tool (Figma, Keynote, physical collage + photo), that's fine. One page, dense with images, light on text.

## What to put on it

Hit at least 4 of these 6 categories. Be **specific and visual** — a photo of *the exact apartment view* beats the word "home". A photo of *the trip* beats the phrase "travel more".

| Category | Prompt |
|---|---|
| **Lifestyle** | Where do you want to live? What does your daily routine look like? |
| **People** | Who's in the picture with you — specifically? Parents taken care of? A partner? Kids? |
| **Money** | What does "enough" look like? Not a round number — an image: a paid-off HDB, no-mortgage condo, parents in a home you chose, a specific car if that matters to you |
| **Career** | MDRT plaque? Court of the Table? A team you lead? A title at a specific milestone? |
| **Growth** | Books you'll have read. Skills you'll have mastered. A body you'll have kept. |
| **Give-back** | What you'll do with the margin this career creates — family, cause, community |

## The three rules

1. **Specific beats aspirational.** *"Bring my parents to Japan"* with a photo of the ryokan you'd book > *"travel with family"* with a stock sunset.
2. **Present tense framing.** *"I am debt-free by Dec 2027"* not *"I want to be"*. The brain reads present-tense commitments differently.
3. **One page, dense.** If it's spread across 10 pages you'll never look at it. One image you see every day > a deck you open once.

## Where to put it once you're done

- **Phone wallpaper** — this is the highest-frequency surface you own
- **Laptop desktop background** — second highest
- **Printed + stuck on the wall** above your desk — for tactile reinforcement

Pick at least one. The board is worthless if it lives in a folder.

## Submission

1. Upload the PDF / image / Canva export here, OR paste the shareable Canva link in the notes.
2. Share it to your **onboarding group chat** for team visibility.
3. A team call reviews everyone's boards ~1–2 months after onboarding submission — this is when you hear what peers put on theirs, which always sharpens your own.

## A note on revision

Most FCs revise the board around month 6 and again at year 1. That's healthy — the specifics should sharpen as you learn what you actually want. Don't treat v1 as final; treat it as the starting image. The only bad vision board is the one that doesn't get made.
