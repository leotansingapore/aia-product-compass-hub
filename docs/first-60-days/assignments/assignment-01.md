# Assignment 1 — Roleplay: Two CSTs (Wealth Angle + Risk Management Angle)

> **What you're producing:** **Two** recorded roleplay videos. Each is a separate Client Strategy Talk (CST) with a different opening angle. Both end at the door-opener — neither attempts to close on a product. The point is to internalise both flows so you can pick whichever fits the prospect in front of you.

## Why two CSTs and not one

A CST is the 5–15-minute structured concept presentation you deliver between rapport-building and the recommendation meeting. It's not a sale — it's the *frame* that earns the next meeting.

There are two anchor angles every FC must master:

| Angle | Source | Lead with this when… |
|---|---|---|
| **A — Wealth Angle** (Retirement Step-by-Step) | [Day 52](../../first-60-days/week-9/day-52.md) | The prospect is interested in *growing money*, retirement, investing, or has explicitly said they want to plan for the future |
| **B — Risk Management Angle** (Total Wealth Concept / TWFPS) | [Day 14](../../first-60-days/week-3/day-14.md), [Day 53](../../first-60-days/week-9/day-53.md) | The prospect is interested in *protection*, has a young family, has a fear of losing income, or is risk-averse |

Most prospects respond to one angle more than the other. The producer who can deliver both — and pick the right one in the first 5 minutes of rapport — converts more meetings than the one who only knows one angle.

This assignment forces you to record both, so when you sit down with a real prospect, the right CST is already in your hands.

## Part A — Record the **Wealth Angle CST** (Retirement Step-by-Step)

### What you must cover (in order, on paper, in front of camera)

#### 1. Capture the 5 inputs (1–2 min)

Ask, write, confirm:

| # | Input | Default if unsure |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Current age | (must have) |
| 2 | Retirement age | 65 |
| 3 | Life expectancy | 85 |
| 4 | Target monthly lifestyle (today's dollars) | same as current monthly spending |
| 5 | Inflation | 2.5% |

#### 2. Draw Diagram 1 — The Problem (5 steps, 4–5 min)

Use a horizontal timeline. Draw the inflation slope rising from today. **Make the prospect guess** the inflated number before revealing it.

- **Step 1:** Mark today, draw the inflation slope, label *"$X/mth — your desired lifestyle"* and *"2.5% p.a. inflation"*
- **Step 2:** Ask them to guess the inflated monthly cost at retirement, then reveal the number
- **Step 3:** Calculate the lump sum live: `inflated × 12 × retirement years = $X.XM needed`
- **Step 4:** Draw the depletion line from retirement down to $0 at end-age, labelled *"Capital depletes"*
- **Step 5:** Calculate the no-investing monthly savings need (`lump sum ÷ years ÷ 12`). Write the number large. Pause. Don't speak first.

#### 3. Draw Diagram 2 — The Solution (4 steps, 3–4 min)

Same timeline, fresh page or different colour. This time the curve grows steeper because of compound returns.

- **Step 1:** Green growth slope. Label *"Invest $X/mth"* — the dramatically smaller number
- **Step 2:** *"Achieve $X.XM at 65 — reinvest for dividends"*
- **Step 3:** Two-paths comparison — grey dashed depletion vs solid green capital-preserved with dividend arrows hanging down
- **Step 4:** Side-by-side comparison cards — WITHOUT investing (Save $X/mth → $0 at end) vs WITH investing (Invest $X/mth → capital preserved + dividend income)

#### 4. Door-opener (30 sec — recording stops here)

> *"I'm not going to suggest anything specific today. The right plan depends on what's already in CPF, your time horizon, your real risk tolerance — none of which we've covered. Mind if I run a proper fact-find with you, then come back with an actual plan that gets you to $X.XM at $X/mth instead of $X/mth?"*

### Wealth-Angle recording requirements

- 8–15 minutes total
- Pen-and-paper diagrams visible to camera throughout (or whiteboard / iPad)
- Both diagrams drawn live — not pre-drawn
- The "guess the number" pause in Step 2 of the Problem must be visible
- The pause after the no-investing reveal in Step 5 must be at least 3 seconds
- End at the door-opener — do **not** name a product

## Part B — Record the **Risk Management Angle CST** (Total Wealth Concept)

### What you must cover (in order, on paper, in front of camera)

#### 1. Draw the two boxes (1 min)

On a piece of paper, draw two boxes side by side. Label them **Financial Capital** and **Human Capital**.

> *"Which one do you think you have more of right now — Financial Capital or Human Capital?"*

Almost everyone correctly answers Human Capital. That's the opening — they've conceded the bigger asset is the one nobody insures.

#### 2. Surface Human Capital (1 min)

> *"Human Capital is your future earning power — your career, your years of working ahead. For most people in their 30s, it's worth $2–4 million in future income. Way bigger than what's in the Financial box today. But you've probably never had it valued."*

The "you are your own golden goose" line works here:

> *"You are your own golden goose. Your bank account is small. Your ability to keep producing income is the asset that matters."*

Pause. Let it land.

#### 3. Draw the conversion arrow (1 min)

Draw an arrow from Human Capital → Financial Capital. Write four words on it: **earn, spend less, save, invest**.

> *"Building wealth is just this loop, repeated for 30 years."*

#### 4. The "health or wealth" bridge (30 sec)

> *"Health or wealth — which is more important?"*
>
> (Prospect: *"Health, of course."*)
>
> *"Then we can't just talk about investing. We also have to talk about protecting the engine that produces the wealth in the first place."*

#### 5. Draw the TWFPS four pillars (3–4 min)

Under Human Capital, draw four boxes. Under Financial Capital, draw one box.

| Pillar | Benchmark coverage |
|---|---|
| **Death / Disability** | 10–20× annual income |
| **Critical Illness** *(weakest link, often ignored)* | $100K + 3–5× annual income |
| **Hospital & Accident** | Hospital plan + Medishield upgrade + PA |
| **Investment & Savings** | Global balanced strategy |

Walk through each. Use the *"hospital plan pays the hospital, CI plan pays YOU"* line for the CI pillar. Use the cancer-drug rationale for the $100K buffer (one vial of certain cancer drugs is ~$10K per dose, often not covered by hospital plans).

#### 6. The protection-question close (1 min)

Underline Human Capital. Ask:

> *"If this stopped tomorrow — accident, illness, anything — how long does what's in the Financial box last your family?"*

Most prospects can't answer. That silence is the assignment for the next meeting.

#### 7. Door-opener (30 sec — recording stops here)

> *"I'm not going to recommend anything today. The right plan depends on the size of both boxes today and where you want them in 20 years. Mind if I run through a few questions to actually understand your situation? Then I can put a real plan together for you."*

### Risk-Angle recording requirements

- 8–15 minutes total
- Pen-and-paper diagrams visible to camera (two boxes + arrow + four pillars)
- The "which one do you have more of?" opening question must be in the recording
- The "health or wealth" bridge must be in the recording
- All four pillars must be drawn — not just named
- End at the door-opener — do **not** name a product or quote a premium

## What "passing" looks like — for both videos

### The pass criteria (both videos, separately)

| Criterion | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| **Diagram integrity** | Both diagrams drawn live on paper/whiteboard, fully visible to camera |
| **Numbers confidence** | Math done in front of the prospect, no calculator panic, lump-sum number written large |
| **Concept fidelity** | The CST follows the source-material structure (Day 52 for Part A, Day 14 for Part B) — no skipped steps |
| **Silence discipline** | At least one 3+ second pause after a closing question or big-number reveal |
| **Door-opener landed** | Specific permission asked for the next meeting (fact-find), no product named |
| **Natural delivery** | Script is internalised, not read. You can glance at notes — you can't read off them |
| **One objection handled** | Brief the actor to throw at least one objection (e.g., *"that's too big a number"* or *"I need to ask my parents"*) and you handle it with a Day 14 / Day 53 reframe |

### Common reasons people fail

- **Pre-drew the diagrams.** The whole point is the live drawing — the prospect watching the gap appear in real time. Pre-drawn = re-take.
- **Skipped the guess-then-reveal in Wealth Angle Step 2.** That pause is the conversion. Don't skip it.
- **Named a product at the end.** The CST opens the door. The product comes in the *next* meeting. Naming a product on this CST = re-take.
- **Read off a script visibly on screen.** Memorisation is the assignment. Notes off-camera are fine.

## How to record

### Equipment

- Phone or laptop camera, mounted at chest height pointing at you + the paper
- Good lighting (window light or ring light)
- One continuous take per CST — edits show
- Whiteboard, iPad, or A4 paper — your choice, but the writing must be legible to the camera

### The actor

- A friend, spouse, sibling, or fellow Pre-RNF cohort member
- Brief them beforehand on the prospect profile they should play:
  - **For Part A (Wealth Angle):** Age 25–40, working professional, some savings, no specific retirement plan, vaguely interested in investing
  - **For Part B (Risk Angle):** Age 30–45, has a family or partner, mortgage, may have employer hospital coverage but nothing else, slightly resistant to "buying insurance"
- Ask them to throw one objection per video — but otherwise let the conversation flow

### Take strategy

- Re-take freely until you have a version you'd send to a real prospect
- Submit your **best take per CST**, not your first
- If you re-take more than 5 times for either video, the issue is usually that you haven't internalised the source material yet — go back and re-read Day 14 / Day 52 before recording again
