# Assignment 1 — Roleplay: Total Wealth Concept & Canned Sales Track

> **What you're producing:** A single recorded roleplay (video, 8 to 15 minutes) where you run a prospect through the **Total Wealth** concept using a canned sales track. The goal is to reach the close — not to improvise brilliance.

## Why this matters

By Week 8 you're meeting real prospects. Between now and then the single biggest variable in your close rate is whether your opening concept delivery feels natural, numbers-led, and emotionally resonant. Scripts aren't a crutch — they're the scaffold that lets you focus on the client, not on what to say next.

This assignment forces you to internalise the Total Wealth story end-to-end: the freedom frame, the numbers, the silence discipline, and the appointment-setting close.

## The canned sales track — what you must cover

Run this structure end-to-end. Timings are targets, not laws.

### 1. Rapport (under 3 min)
Comment on something specific about them — family photo, office, shared contact. Ask, don't tell. Rephrase their answer. Move on.

### 2. Mission statement + agenda (under 1 min)
One sentence of what you do: *"I help [target audience] reach financial freedom through a simple framework called Total Wealth."*
One sentence of the agenda: *"Today we will understand where you are, share what's possible, and see if next steps make sense."*

### 3. The Total Wealth concept (4 to 6 min)
This is the heart of the roleplay. Walk through:
- **What financial freedom actually means** — passive income > monthly expenses.
- **The freedom number** — ask them their monthly expenses, multiply by 12, divide their target passive income by an expected yield.
- **The Bank A vs Bank B illustration** — on paper. $100/month in Bank A at 0.05% vs Bank B at a compounding return. Pause after writing each number. Let the delta land.
- **Where they are today vs where they need to be** — the gap surfaces by itself if you've asked their expenses and their current savings.

### 4. Silence + the appointment close (2 to 3 min)
After the gap is visible, stop talking. Do not fill the silence with product.
Then: *"There are a few ways I can help you close this — would Tuesday 3pm or Thursday 7pm work better to go through options properly?"*

## What a passing recording looks like

- You reach the appointment close — not the product recommendation.
- You handle at least one objection in the live recording.
- You use the pen-and-paper Bank A vs B illustration (on screen, visible to camera).
- You do not read off a script visibly. The track is memorised.
- You pause after asking the close. Do not speak first.

## How to record

- Use a phone or laptop camera. Ring light optional, good lighting mandatory.
- Have a friend / spouse / sibling roleplay as the prospect. Brief them beforehand:
  - Age 28 to 40, working professional, has some savings but no plan
  - Budget around $500 to $1,500/month after expenses
  - Polite but slightly skeptical — they've met FCs before and it didn't stick
- Record one continuous take. Edits show. Retakes are fine — just pick your best.

## Scoring rubric

| Criterion | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| **Concept integrity** | Total Wealth framework delivered end-to-end, freedom number surfaced |
| **Numbers confidence** | Bank A vs B written live, no calculator panic |
| **Silence discipline** | At least one 3+ second pause after a closing question |
| **Close attempted** | Specific appointment times offered, not "let me know" |
| **Objection handled** | Handled one live objection without derailing |
| **Natural delivery** | Script is clearly internalised, not read |
