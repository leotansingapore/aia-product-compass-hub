# Assignment 3 — Joint Field Observation: Reflection Worksheet

> **What you're producing:** A completed reflection worksheet after sitting in on one real prospect or client meeting run by a senior FC or your mentor. You observe — you don't pitch — and then write what you saw through the lens of everything you've learned.

## Why this matters

Reading about SPIN and watching SPIN happen are different skills. Most new FCs get to Week 8 knowing every framework on paper but freeze in the first live meeting because they've never seen the frameworks *as they actually look in the room*. This observation collapses that gap.

The reflection isn't a summary — it's a diagnosis. You're looking for the moves the senior FC made that you would have missed, and the moves you'd do differently.

## How to set this up

1. Ask your mentor or any senior FC in the team. Most will say yes.
2. Confirm prospect consent — you're introduced as "a colleague observing".
3. Sit, take notes by hand or on phone discreetly. Say nothing unless directly addressed.
4. Debrief the senior FC for 10 minutes after the meeting. Their read of what happened is part of the reflection.

## The worksheet

Copy this into a Google Doc / PDF and fill each section. Short, specific answers beat long general ones.

---

### Setup

- **Senior FC observed:** _________________
- **Date & meeting type:** _________________ (first meeting / follow-up / policy review / close)
- **Prospect profile:** age range, occupation, relationship stage, apparent DISC style
- **Meeting outcome:** booked follow-up / proposal delivered / closed / not pursuing

### Section 1 - Opening (first 10 minutes)

- How did the senior FC open rapport? What specifically did they comment on or ask?
- When did they deliver their mission statement? What were the words they used?
- What agenda did they set? Was it 3 items, or different?
- **Your observation:** what move did they make here that you wouldn't have thought of?

### Section 2 - The Questions (the fact-find)

- Roughly what % of the meeting was the senior FC talking vs listening?
- Estimate how many Situation questions you heard. How many Problem questions? How many Implication questions?
- What was the single best question you heard? Write it word-for-word.
- When the prospect gave a short answer, what did the senior FC do? (Ask follow-up / pause / move on)
- **Your observation:** a question you would have asked but they didn't — was that a gap or a smart cut?

### Section 3 - The Concept / Presentation

- Which concept did they lead with? (Total Wealth / Bank A vs B / Protection ladder / CST Risks / other)
- Did they use pen and paper, a tablet/illustration, or verbal only?
- At what point did the prospect show the first visible emotional shift? What triggered it?
- **Your observation:** was the concept over-explained, under-explained, or pitched exactly right for this prospect's profile?

### Section 4 - Handling pushback

- What was the first objection raised? What were the exact words?
- How did the senior FC respond — in the 3-beat structure (Acknowledge / Reframe / Redirect) or differently?
- Was there an objection they did NOT handle well? What did you notice?
- **Your observation:** how would you have responded?

### Section 5 - The Close

- How did the senior FC move to the close? What words did they use?
- Was there silence after the close? How long? Who broke it?
- Did they close on product or close on appointment / next step?
- **Your observation:** was this meeting front-loaded (sale in-meeting) or back-loaded (more meetings needed)? Was that the right call?

### Section 6 - Debrief with the senior FC

Ask these after the meeting. Write their answers verbatim where possible.

1. "What did you see that I probably missed?"
2. "If you could replay one moment, what would you do differently?"
3. "What question did the prospect ask that you almost didn't have a good answer for?"
4. "How did you decide which concept to lead with?"

### Section 7 - Your takeaways (the real reflection)

Write 5 bullet points — not a paragraph, not a list of compliments. Each one must be **specific and actionable for your own next meeting**. Bad: *"Senior FC was very confident."* Good: *"When prospect said they already had insurance, senior FC asked 'when was the last time you looked at what's actually covered?' — I'm using this."*

### Section 8 - One thing you'd steal

One single technique, phrase, or move you're adopting into your own approach. Name it. Commit to using it in your next 3 meetings.
