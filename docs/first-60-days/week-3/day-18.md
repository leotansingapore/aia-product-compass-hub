# Day 18 — The 10X Rule: Mindset Shift

> **The one idea for today:** Most people miss their targets because they set targets that feel comfortable — then plan actions proportional to comfortable. The 10X Rule inverts this: set targets 10× bigger, then take actions 10× bigger. The math almost always delivers you to the original comfortable target, and often well beyond.

## What you'll walk away with

By the end of today you should be able to:

1. **State** the 5 principles of the 10X Rule from memory.
2. **Apply** 10X thinking to ONE goal you're currently setting too conservatively.
3. **Distinguish** between 10X'ing your **target** vs 10X'ing your **actions** — and why the rule requires both.

---

## 1. The rule

From Grant Cardone's book of the same name, stripped to mechanics:

> **Set targets 10 times greater than what you believe you can achieve, and take actions 10 times greater than what you believe are necessary to achieve your goals.**

This is not a motivational slogan. It's a planning discipline with specific rules.

## 2. The 5 principles

### Principle 1 — Set massive goals
**Ordinary goals produce ordinary results.**

A new FC who aims for $60K FYC in Year 1 plans cautiously — 5 meetings a week, friendly follow-ups. They usually land around $60K.

A new FC who aims for $300K FYC in Year 1 plans aggressively — 3 outreach attempts per day, every relationship mined, public content running, mentor meetings weekly. They often land at $150K–200K. **Still 2.5–3× the first person.**

The goal doesn't need to be hit 100% to be worth setting. 10X goals force 10X plans — which produce 3–5× outcomes.

### Principle 2 — Take massive action
**Take actions 10× greater than you think are necessary. Over-prepare. Over-deliver.**

The typical new-FC calendar has 3 hours of actual prospecting a week. 10X'ed, that's 15 hours. The typical new-FC posts on LinkedIn once a month. 10X'ed, that's 10 posts a month.

You will feel ridiculous doing this in Week 1. That's the point. Everyone else is doing 1× activity and wondering why they're stuck.

### Principle 3 — Never reduce your target; increase action
**The 10X Rule assumes the target is never the problem. The effort is.**

When you miss a monthly goal, the lazy response is to say, "that was unrealistic." The 10X response is: "what action do I need to 10X next month?"

If you miss $20K FYC in Month 3, don't lower the Month 4 target to $15K. Raise the calls from 50/week to 150/week. Raise the warm outreach from 10/week to 30/week.

**Targets don't get achieved by lowering them.**

### Principle 4 — Expect failures
**Average actions never produce successful results. Failure is the tollbooth, not the stop sign.**

10X activity produces more visible failures than 1× activity. More calls = more rejections. More posts = more silence. More meetings = more no-sales.

**The failures are feedback,** not fatal. They tell you:
- Which opener isn't working (adjust).
- Which audience doesn't care (move on).
- Which product narrative falls flat (rewrite).

A new FC with 50 failures a week is learning 50× faster than one with 5.

### Principle 5 — Overcome fear with action
**Don't think your way out of fear. Move your way out.**

Fear before a cold call is real. The only thing that kills it reliably is **making the call.** Fear before a public post is real. The only thing that kills it is **hitting post.**

Thought precedes action for most things. For fear, action precedes clarity.

## 3. Just take action — figure it out later

A quiet but important sub-principle:

> **Commit first, figure it out after.**

Most people need to know *how* they'll do something before they commit to doing it. That's backwards. The commitment is what generates the learning needed to figure it out.

**Examples for a new FC:**
- "I'll run an Instagram content series." → Start before you know what Week 3's post will be.
- "I'll call 100 people this month." → Start calling before you know who 100 are.
- "I'll get 3 appointments by Friday." → Start outreach on Monday without a pre-built plan for 3 confirmations.

The learning happens in the doing. The plan refines itself as you move.

## 4. Where 10X thinking actually fails

The 10X Rule works for **behaviour**, not for **results you don't control.** It falls apart when misapplied.

### It works for:
- Activity volume (calls, outreach, content, meetings).
- Study volume (products, objections, skills).
- Preparation intensity (role-plays, scripts).
- Fear thresholds (difficult conversations, awkward asks).

### It doesn't work for:
- **Client outcomes.** You can't 10X a client's buying decision.
- **Skill acquisition speed.** Some skills take reps, not intensity.
- **Relationships.** Close relationships need *time*, not 10× effort.
- **Compliance.** You don't 10X your way around licensing.

The rule is about **inputs you control.** Don't use it as a weapon against things you don't.

## 5. A worked example — your next 30 days

Most new FCs set some version of: "I'll get 5 meetings this month and close 2."

**10X'ed:**
- Set the target at **20 meetings, 8 closes.**
- Plan to **outreach 150 people** (vs the timid 30).
- Plan **10 pieces of public content** (vs the timid 1).
- Plan **2 role-plays/week with mentor** (vs the timid 0.5).
- Plan **5 hours/week of product study** (vs the timid 1).
- Plan **10 reconnection coffees** with old contacts (vs the timid 2).

Realistic landing: maybe you get **12 meetings and 4 closes.** That's:
- **2.4× your original meeting target.**
- **2× your original close target.**
- A much bigger skill base to build on next month.

10X targets → 3× results is a normal outcome. 1× targets → 1× results (if you're lucky).

## 6. The shadow side

The 10X Rule can wreck you if you take it as a permanent state. No one sustains 10× effort for 10 years.

**The honest pattern:**
- **Sprints of 10X** — 60 to 90 days at a time, around critical quarters.
- **Plateaus of 3–5X** — steady states where systems run.
- **Recovery periods** — where you strip back to sustain health.

The new FCs who burn out in Year 2 are usually the ones who tried to run 10X for 12 months straight. The ones who build 30-year careers run 10X sprints inside a sustainable year.

Your first 60 days is one of those 10X sprints. After that, you'll find the cadence that fits you.
