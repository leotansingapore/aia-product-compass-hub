# Day 36 — TVM Mastery Drill: Live Client Speed Test

> **The one idea for today:** Concepts don't earn commissions. **Problem-solving speed** in client meetings does. By the end of today, you should be able to run a full TVM calculation in front of a real prospect in under 90 seconds. Today is reps.

> **📍 The TVM material has been consolidated.** All concept and the 10 practice problems live on **[Day 28 — Time Value of Money: The Complete Reference](../week-5/day-28.md)**. Day 31 was the warm-up drill; today (Day 36) is the **live-meeting speed test**.

> **🛠 Calculator:** [calculator.net Finance Calculator](https://www.calculator.net/finance-calculator.html) — open it in a separate tab and use it for every problem below. Same tool you used on Day 28 / Day 31; the speed test is muscle memory on this exact UI.

## What you'll walk away with

By the end of today you should be able to:

1. **Solve all 10** Day 28 practice problems within 60 seconds each (90 seconds for Problem 9, the two-step gap-closer).
2. **Demonstrate** a sub-90-second TVM calculation in a live roleplay setting.
3. **Identify** which of the 5 common error patterns trips you up most often — and have a personal mitigation.

## 1. The full 10-problem run

Open **[Day 28 §13](../week-5/day-28.md#13-practice-problems--the-10-essentials)** in another tab. **Solve all 10 problems back-to-back, timed.**

| Target time | What it means |
|---|---|
| **Under 10 minutes total** | You're meeting-ready |
| **10–15 minutes** | Practise the slow ones tomorrow |
| **Over 15 minutes** | Re-read Day 28 sections 1–11, then run again |

Don't skip Problem 10's mental math — that one builds the intuition for "is this answer in the right ballpark?" which protects you from quoting an absurd number to a client.

## 2. Live client speed test (the actual proficiency bar)

This is the difference between an FC who *knows* TVM and one who can *use* TVM in front of a paying client.

### The test

Sit a friend, spouse, sibling, or fellow Pre-RNF cohort member down. Tell them you're practising. Hand them this list of prompts and have them pick **3 at random** (don't let you see which):

> *"I'm 35, want $1M at 65, can save $1,500/month — can I get there?"*
>
> *"I want $250K for my kid's university in 18 years — what monthly amount do I save?"*
>
> *"This plan promises $85K at age 60 if I pay $1,500/year. Is that a good return?"*
>
> *"My monthly expenses are $5,000 today. In 30 years at 2% inflation, what will the same lifestyle cost?"*
>
> *"I have $100K in CPF at 40, need $800K at 65. CPF grows 4%, my plan grows 5%. How much do I need to add monthly?"*
>
> *"At 6%, how long does my money take to double?"*

### Pass criteria

For each of the 3 picked:

- [ ] You opened the calculator and started entering values within **10 seconds**.
- [ ] You spoke the variables out loud as you entered them ("FV is one million, n is 25 years, i is 6, PV is zero, mode BGN, solving for PMT…").
- [ ] You produced a **client-friendly answer** (not a raw calculator number) within **90 seconds total** of the question being asked.
- [ ] If they pushed back ("what if I save $1,000 instead?"), you re-ran the calculation in under 30 seconds.

If you missed any of the four bars, **the issue is reps, not knowledge**. Run the test again tomorrow with 3 different prompts.

## 3. The 5 error patterns — your personal mitigation

From [Day 28 §14](../week-5/day-28.md#14-common-error-patterns--the-80-trap). Mark the one that trips *you* up most:

| # | Error | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Sign error (PMT +/−) | Write "OUT = −, IN = +" on a sticky on the inside of your meeting folder |
| 2 | Mode mix-up (BGN vs END) | Default your calculator to BGN at the start of every meeting |
| 3 | Period mismatch (years vs months) | If PMT is monthly, write "÷12" next to your i value as you enter it |
| 4 | Nominal-vs-real confusion | When the client says "after inflation", say "let me adjust" out loud so you remember to subtract |
| 5 | Forgetting to inflate the target | Always run the inflation step *first* when the goal is in today's dollars |

Star the one you're most prone to. That's the one to drill.

## 4. Final readiness check (the meeting bar)

Before you call this day done, you should be able to truthfully tick all of these:

- [ ] All 10 Day 28 practice problems solved within 60 sec each (90 sec for #9).
- [ ] Sub-90-second live test passed on 3 of 3 prompts.
- [ ] You can name your most common error pattern AND your mitigation for it.
- [ ] You can run the calculator while looking at the other person's face — not staring at your screen.

If all four are ticked, you're ready to use TVM in a real prospect meeting. **Don't move forward without this.**

## Quick quiz

1. **The proficiency bar for a TVM-ready FC is:**
 - A) Knowing all the formulas by heart
 - B) Solving 10 problems in 10 minutes AND running a live calc in under 90 sec ✓
 - C) Owning a Casio FC-100V
 - D) Memorising the calculator manual

 **Why:** The actual bar is performance under live conditions — speed AND smoothness in front of a real human. Knowing formulas (A) and owning specific hardware (C) are inputs to that performance, not the bar itself. The manual (D) is reference material, not a credential.

2. **Why does the live-meeting speed test specify 90 seconds?**
 - A) MAS regulation
 - B) Below ~90 seconds, the client doesn't visibly disengage; above it, you start losing them ✓
 - C) That's the average time of a TVM calculation
 - D) To match the speed of competing FCs

 **Why:** 90 seconds is the threshold where a client can still hold a thread of attention without checking their phone. It's not a regulation (A), not the average compute time (C is reverse causation), and not a competitive benchmark (D).

3. **A new FC scores 8/10 on the Day 28 practice problems but fails the live test (takes 3 minutes per question with the prospect waiting). The right next step is:**
 - A) Practise more problems in private
 - B) Practise the live test specifically — record yourself if needed ✓
 - C) Switch to a faster calculator
 - D) Move on, these are edge cases

 **Why:** The gap is *delivery under live conditions*, not knowledge. More private practice (A) builds knowledge they already have. The fix is more reps with the speaking-out-loud + person-watching-you constraints in place. Recording themselves makes the awkwardness visible; they fix it.
