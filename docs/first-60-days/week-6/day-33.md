# Day 33 — Understanding CPF: The Singapore Foundation

> **The one idea for today:** Every Singaporean client's retirement plan starts with CPF — whether they realise it or not. If you can't walk a client through their own CPF statement, you can't recommend anything above it. CPF is the floor your plans stand on.

## What you'll walk away with

By the end of today you should be able to:

1. **Explain** the 4 CPF accounts, their interest rates, and what each is for.
2. **Calculate** a client's approximate CPF contribution from their monthly salary.
3. **Navigate** the key milestone ages (55, 65) and what happens at each.

---

## 1. CPF in one paragraph

The Central Provident Fund (CPF) is Singapore's **mandatory social security system.** Both employees and employers contribute monthly. Funds are distributed across four accounts, each with a specific purpose and interest rate. As a client grows older, contribution rates decrease (giving them more take-home cash), but the savings that built up earlier continue earning interest.

For a new FC: CPF is the **bedrock of almost every retirement conversation.** Never recommend an additional retirement product until you've accounted for what CPF will produce.

## 2. The 4 accounts

| Account | Used for | Current interest rate |
|---|---|---|
| **Ordinary Account (OA)** | Housing, insurance, investment, children's education | Up to **3.5% p.a.** (min 2.5%) |
| **Special Account (SA)** | Retirement-related investments | Up to **5% p.a.** (min 4%) |
| **MediSave Account (MA)** | Hospitalisation, approved medical insurance | Up to **5% p.a.** (min 4%) |
| **Retirement Account (RA)** | Created automatically at age 55; funds CPF LIFE monthly payouts | Up to **5% p.a.** (min 4%) |

**The extra 1% p.a.:** CPF pays an additional 1% interest on the **first $60,000** of combined balances, with up to $20,000 from OA counted.

**The extra 1% p.a. for 55+:** another 1% on top of that for members aged 55 and above on the first $30,000.

**Why SA is so valuable:** it compounds at 4%+ guaranteed. A 35-year-old SA balance of $50K becomes ~$162K at age 65 — without any additional contributions. Most private products can't match the risk-adjusted return.

## 3. CPF contribution rates

Rates vary by age. As age rises, rates drop (reflecting lower housing needs and more cash flexibility).

| Age | Employer (%) | Employee (%) | Total (%) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| 55 and below | 17 | 20 | **37** |
| 55–60 | 13 | 13 | 26 |
| 60–65 | 9 | 7.5 | 16.5 |
| Above 65 | 7.5 | 5 | 12.5 |

**Quick mental math for under-55 clients:**

> **Monthly CPF contribution = 37% × gross salary.**

For a $6,000/month earner: **$2,220/month** flows into CPF.

**Allocation (under-55):**
- **OA:** about 2/3 of total → $1,480.
- **SA:** about 1/6 → $370.
- **MA:** about 1/6 → $370.

(Actual allocations vary by age band — get exact from the CPF Board website. But this approximation is good for quick conversations.)

### The client realisation

Most clients have no idea they're "saving" ~$2,000/month through CPF. When you show them, two things happen:

1. They realise they're further along than they thought.
2. They often immediately ask, "is what I'm saving enough?" — which is your opening.

## 4. The CPF timeline — key ages

### Age 55 — the first decision point

At **age 55**, a **Retirement Account (RA)** is automatically created. Your SA and OA transfer into RA up to the **Retirement Sum.**

Three Retirement Sum tiers (2026 estimates, rise annually):

| Sum tier | What it provides |
|---|---|
| Basic Retirement Sum (BRS) | Lower monthly payout; members can pledge property to keep only BRS in RA |
| Full Retirement Sum (FRS) | Standard monthly payout; typically 2× BRS |
| Enhanced Retirement Sum (ERS) | Higher monthly payout; typically 3× BRS |

**What you can withdraw at 55:**
- **If you have at least the FRS in your combined CPF:** you can withdraw the **excess above FRS** from OA + SA.
- **If you don't have the FRS:** you can withdraw a small amount ($5,000 minimum), but most of your CPF stays locked as retirement savings.

**Planning implication:** hitting the FRS by 55 gives you flexibility. Falling short locks more money until later.

### Age 65 — CPF LIFE begins

At **age 65** (for most), **CPF LIFE** — a lifelong annuity — starts paying out from the RA.

Three CPF LIFE plans:

| Plan | Payout profile | Best for |
|---|---|---|
| **Basic** | Lower monthly payout; larger bequest to beneficiaries | Those who want to leave more for family |
| **Standard** (default) | Moderate monthly payout; moderate bequest | Most members |
| **Escalating** | Starts lower but increases 2% p.a. for life | Members worried about inflation |

**The key insight:** CPF LIFE pays **for life** — the member cannot outlive the payout. This solves the single biggest retirement risk: living longer than your savings.

## 5. The client conversation frame

When you sit with a client, walk them through their CPF in this order:

1. **"Do you know how much goes into your CPF each month?"**
 Most don't. Share the 37% rule.

2. **"Do you know what your balance is?"**
 About half the clients don't. Get them to log in and check.

3. **"Do you know what your CPF will pay you at 65?"**
 Use the CPF calculator on cpf.gov.sg or the AIA iResource tool.

4. **"Is that enough?"**
 Compare to their desired retirement income. Almost always there's a gap.

5. **"Let's plan how to close the gap."**
 This is where your products (ILPs, endowments, SRS top-ups) come in.

**The wrong approach:** ignoring CPF and just pitching products. Clients see through it immediately.

**The right approach:** CPF first, then layer on top. You become an educator, not a salesperson.

## 6. Common CPF questions (and your answers)

**"Should I top up my SA for tax relief?"**
- SRS and CPF top-ups give tax relief up to certain limits.
- CPF RSTU (Retirement Sum Top-Up) gives up to $16K tax relief per year ($8K self + $8K family).
- **Good for:** high-income clients who need tax relief AND can wait for the money (locked until 55+).
- **Not good for:** clients who may need liquidity, or whose income is low enough that tax relief is minimal.

**"Should I use OA to invest in CPFIS?"**
- CPFIS (CPF Investment Scheme) allows OA/SA money to be invested in approved instruments.
- Makes sense only if the investment **consistently beats** the OA rate (2.5% floor) + risk.
- Most CPFIS investors **underperform** OA's guaranteed rate. Proceed cautiously.

**"Can I use OA to pay my mortgage?"**
- Yes. OA is the default housing account.
- Trade-off: money used for mortgage **stops compounding at 2.5%.** Cash financing may sometimes be better.
- Good rule: compare mortgage rate vs OA rate. If mortgage < 2.5%, pay with cash if possible and let OA grow.

**"Is CPF really mine?"**
- Yes. Upon death, CPF balances pass to nominated beneficiaries (via CPF nomination, NOT your will).
- If no nomination, public trustee distributes per intestacy laws.
- **Action for every client:** confirm their CPF nomination is up to date. Most clients have never done one.

## 7. Why CPF is often the best "product" you can recommend

Counterintuitive truth: for many clients, the best recommendation you can make is **"top up your SA."**

- Guaranteed 4% floor (vs most ILPs projecting 4–6% with market risk).
- Tax relief (up to $8K personal RSTU).
- Forced saving until 55.
- Compounding on a locked base.

You don't get commission for this advice. Which is exactly why it builds **long-term trust** when you give it.

**The durable FC-client relationship:** "my advisor told me to top up SA before selling me anything. I trust them." That trust pays you for 20 years.
